The National Guard’s top general told Congress on Friday that it would follow the Constitution and the law when he was asked about the possibility President Donald Trump would order troops to polling places for the midterm elections.

The remarks at a U.S. House Appropriations subcommittee [hearing](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FUHHCHO2bbo "(opens in a new window)") came as Democratic lawmakers also voiced unease over the [continuing deployment](https://www.newsfromthestates.com/article/federal-appeals-court-extends-national-guard-presence-dc) of nearly 2,500 National Guard members [in Washington, D.C.](https://www.newsfromthestates.com/article/six-gop-states-send-more-1000-national-guard-dc-trump-crackdown)

Rep. Joe Morelle, a New York Democrat, asked Gen. Steven Nordhaus, chief of the National Guard Bureau, what assurances he could provide to Americans concerned about the deployment of troops at the polls.

“The National Guard, obviously, always follows the Constitution, law, policy and guidance, both at the federal and the state level,” Nordhaus said.

Federal law prohibits the deployment of the military to polling places unless necessary “to repel armed enemies of the United States” and violations are punishable by up to five years in prison.

Trump [has said](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/01/11/us/politics/trump-interview-transcript.html "(opens in a new window)") that he should have ordered the National Guard to seize ballot boxes during the 2020 election, which he falsely maintains was stolen. Steve Bannnon, a former Trump adviser, has publicly urged the president to send the military and Immigration and Customs Enforcement, or ICE, agents to patrol the polls.

Trump last year deployed National Guard members to [several Democratic-led cities,](https://www.newsfromthestates.com/article/trump-deploys-national-guard-troops-memphis-anti-crime-task-force) in some instances federalizing them against the will of governors, who typically command National Guard members. He also sent active-duty Marines [into Los Angeles.](https://lailluminator.com/2025/06/20/repub/national-guard/ "(opens in a new window)") Opponents of the deployments expressed fears that they represented a test run for intimidating voters.

While the deployment to the District of Columbia continues, Trump withdrew troops from other cities after the Supreme Court in December [left in place](https://www.newsfromthestates.com/article/us-supreme-court-defeat-trump-blocks-deployment-national-guard-chicago) a lower court decision barring a deployment in Chicago.

Rep. Betty McCollum, a Minnesota Democrat, questioned how long the D.C. deployment is sustainable. She also referred to reporting [by ABC News](https://abcnews.com/Politics/pentagon-plans-national-guard-dc-2029-2-us/story?id=131234530 "(opens in a new window)") that the Pentagon intends to keep troops in D.C. through the end of Trump’s term in January 2029.

“Picking up waste in the District of Columbia does not prepare anyone for conflicts that could arise in Europe, Asia and the Middle East,” McCollum said.
