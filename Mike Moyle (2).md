Mike Moyle is the longest-serving current Idaho House member (14th term, District 14, Star/Eagle), serving as House Majority Leader since 2006, who directed the non-adoption of Joint Rules at the December 2024 and 2025 organizational sessions and has never served on JFAC.

## Basic Info

- **District:** 14 (Star/Eagle, western Ada County)
- **Chamber:** House
- **Role:** House Majority Leader (2006–present)
- **Terms:** 14th (longest-serving current member)
- **Party:** Republican
- **Family:** Wife Janet; five children, ten grandchildren

## Career Sequence

1998 primary margin: 14 votes (Moyle 2,338 / Bivens 2,324). Pre-leadership sequence: rank-and-file → Caucus Chair (2002) → Asst. Majority Leader (2003) → Majority Leader (2006).

## JFAC and Rules Power

Never served on JFAC but controls JFAC co-chair selection as House Majority Leader. Directed the House's non-adoption of Joint Rules at December 2024 and December 2025 organizational sessions (Monks motion), confirmed to Clark Corbin as deliberate. Cited Joint Rule 11 and acknowledged two-thirds threshold required for formal amendment.

Eagle district (14) overlaps with JFAC House co-chair Josh Tanner's District 22A — part of a western Ada County power cluster also including Jason Monks (District 22B) and Wendy Horman (District 30A, Ammon).

## ITD / Hawkins

Intervened in Hawkins Companies v. Moyle as House Majority Leader with private counsel (Hilty, Bower, Haws & Seable). Accused the Little administration of collusion in amended filings (June 2024).
