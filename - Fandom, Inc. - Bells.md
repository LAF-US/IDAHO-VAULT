The **Bells** are the tools of a [necromancer](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Necromancer "Necromancer") or an [Abhorsen](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Abhorsen "Abhorsen"). They use remnants of power from the first [Seven Bright Shiners](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Nine_Bright_Shiners "Nine Bright Shiners") to control or affect the [Dead](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Dead "Dead") (and are sometimes used in non-necromantic situations as well). While a necromancer's bells have handles of ebony or jet and use pure [Free Magic](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Free_Magic "Free Magic"), an Abhorsen's bells are mahogany and combined with [Charter Magic](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Charter_magic "Charter magic"). They are carried in a [bandolier](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Bandolier "Bandolier") across the chest.[^1]

The [Abhorsen-in-Waiting](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Abhorsen-in-Waiting "Abhorsen-in-Waiting") may sometimes use [panpipes](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Panpipes "Panpipes") (which are considerably weaker, but safer) before the bells, with each pipe corresponding to a bell.[^2]

## List of Bells

### Ranna

Ranna is the Sleeper, the smallest bell, with a sweet and low sound, which brings sleep to those who hear it.[^1] [^2] [^3] It used to bind both [Mogget](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Mogget "Mogget") and [Kerrigor](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Kerrigor "Kerrigor") in [*Sabriel*](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Sabriel_\(Book\) "Sabriel (Book)") and was wielded by [Touchstone](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Touchstone "Touchstone") at the binding of [Orannis](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Orannis) [^4] in [Abhorsen](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Abhorsen_\(Book\) "Abhorsen (Book)"). We saw Ranna wielded by an enemy for the first time in *[Goldenhand](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Goldenhand_\(Book\) "Goldenhand (Book)")*, where a necromancer uses it to incapacitate [Ferin](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Ferin "Ferin"), [Astilaran](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Astilaran "Astilaran"), and [Young Laska](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Young_Laska "Young Laska").

### Mosrael

Mosrael is the Waker, a harsh and rowdy bell, which [brings dead bodies to life](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Dead_Hand "Dead Hand") as the wielder falls into [Death](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Death) in a see-saw effect, and in skilled hands it can be used to open locks, reveal hidden ways or secrets, or wake one from an unnatural sleep.[^1] [^2] [^3] [^5] It was wielded by [Sanar](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Sanar) and [Ryelle](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Ryelle) together [^4] (as their souls were intertwined) at the binding of [Orannis](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Orannis "Orannis"). It also opens a hidden door at the top of the [Long Cliffs](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Long_Cliffs "Long Cliffs") that leads to [Abhorsen's House](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Abhorsen%27s_House "Abhorsen's House").

### Kibeth

Kibeth is the Walker, a difficult and contrary bell of several sounds, which can make others go where you wish.[^1] However, it is wise to keep this bell firmly encased, for it is known to sound on its own and cause an inexperienced user to walk where they otherwise would not wish to.[^2] [^3] The [Disreputable Dog](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Disreputable_Dog "Disreputable Dog"), the remnant of Kibeth, stood for herself at the binding of Orannis.[^4]

### Dyrim

Dyrim is the Speaker, a musical bell with a clear and pretty tone, which allows speech or makes things mute, and can silence someone if their tongue moves too freely.[^1] [^2] [^3] It was wielded by [Ellimere](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Ellimere) at the binding of Orannis.[^4]

### Belgaer

Belgaer is the Thinker, a tricksome bell that seeks to ring of its own accord, which allows independent thought for the Dead, or erases memories.[^1] [^2] [^6] Wielded by [Sameth](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Sameth) at the binding of Orannis,[^4] it was used by Sabriel to free [Yrael](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Yrael "Yrael") /Mogget from his "millennia of servitude" after the binding of Orannis, and by [Lirael](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Lirael "Lirael") to restore the " [Clariel](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Clariel "Clariel") " fragment of [Chlorr of the Mask](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Chlorr_of_the_Mask "Chlorr of the Mask") in Death. Belgaer is the most scorned bell of Free Magic necromancers.

### Saraneth

Saraneth is the Binder, the deepest, lowest bell, which forces the listener to obey the wielder's will [^1] [^2] [^6]. It was wielded by [Sabriel](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Sabriel) at the binding of Orannis [^4] and used to [bind Mogget](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Mogget#Binding "Mogget") for thousands of years.

### Astarael

Astarael is the Weeper, the largest bell, which throws all who hear it, including the wielder, deep into Death.[^1] [^6] [^7] Wielded by Lirael at the binding of Orannis, it was used by [Terciel](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Terciel "Terciel") in a self-sacrifice to delay Kerrigor and his followers,[^8] and by Lirael twice to bring "Clariel" to the [Ninth Precinct](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Death#The_Ninth_Precinct "Death").

## Forging

Based on the account of [Idrach the Lesser Necromancer](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/An_Extract_of_the_Journal_of_Idrach_the_Lesser_Necromancer "An Extract of the Journal of Idrach the Lesser Necromancer"), the bells begin as ordinary silver bells that can be bought at any establishment. They become magical with Free Magic spells (and Charter Magic, in the case of the Abhorsen) and are finally heated red-hot and quenched in the waters of Death, at which point they become the necromantic instruments we know. Idrach forged Ranna, Mosrael, and Kibeth at the first, second, and fourth Gates respectively, since dangerous waves of the [Third](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Death#The_Third_Precinct "Death") made forging impossible. It's unknown which other precincts correspond to which bell.

Abhorsens usually inherit their bells, which tend to be centuries old, rather than forging them anew.
