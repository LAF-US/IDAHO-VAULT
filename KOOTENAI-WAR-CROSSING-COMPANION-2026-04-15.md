# Kootenai War Crossing Companion

This note accompanies `Kootenai War of 1974.md`.

## What The Note Seems To Do

The source cluster does not present the 1974 action as local color.
It presents the Bonners Ferry corridor as a public threshold where passage,
toll, land, and recognition became impossible to separate.

## Why It Matters Here

Earlier crossing records in the vault describe a settler apparatus:
Bonner's money,
Walton's labor,
fare,
charter,
and a name that stuck.

The Kootenai action did not repeat that apparatus.
It reversed it.

The 10-cent toll on Highway 95 was not proprietorship dressed up as destiny.
It was a sovereign people making jurisdiction audible at the same bottleneck.

## Field Note

A corridor can be priced in one century and answered back to in another.

That is why this war belongs beside the Bonner crossing notes.
It proves the road was never only the proprietor's story,
and that passage here has always been political before it was ever neutral.
