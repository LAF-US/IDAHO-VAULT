# Fact-Check: Gemini Conversation on Digital Sovereignty (2026-04-06)

**Source:** Gemini web conversation with Logan, relayed to Claude Code for verification.
**Topic:** Digital sovereignty, Idaho legislation, UCC Article 12, copyright, work-for-hire, NIL, digital ID, blockchain, public records.
**Verified by:** Claude Code (The Abhorsen) using web research, 2026-04-06.

---

## Summary

Gemini built a compelling conceptual framework around "Digital Sovereignty" — individual control over data, infrastructure, and identity. The *concepts* are valuable. Several *Idaho-specific legal citations* are fabricated or materially wrong. This file is the canonical correction record.

---

## RED FLAGS (material errors)

### 1. Idaho has NOT adopted UCC Article 12

**Gemini claimed:** Idaho "fully integrated the 2022 UCC Amendments" creating Controllable Electronic Records (CERs) as a new asset class.

**Reality:** Idaho has NOT adopted UCC Article 12. As of April 2026, approximately 24-33 states plus DC have adopted uniform Article 12 — Idaho is not among them. Idaho introduced HB 709 (2026, programmable money) and HB 702 (2026, Article 8 amendments), neither of which is Article 12.

**Impact:** This is the most dangerous error. Gemini's entire "Deed" / "CER" / "Control = Possession" framework for Idaho is built on this fabrication. The legal concepts exist in other jurisdictions — they do not apply in Idaho today.

### 2. H0078 did not "institutionalize" digital driver's licenses

**Gemini claimed:** H0078 and S1299 together "institutionalized the digital driver's license" effective July 1, 2026.

**Reality:** H0078 passed the Idaho House 37-33 (March 11, 2025) but stalled in Senate Transportation Committee (last action March 12, 2025). Never became law.

### 3. S1299 phone-search protection was stripped

**Gemini claimed:** S1299 "explicitly prohibits" law enforcement from searching your phone when showing a digital credential.

**Reality:** The original S1299 contained this provision ("information incidentally observed on a device shall not be used to establish probable cause"). It was removed by amendment before passage. The enacted law (signed March 31, 2026) does NOT contain phone-search protection. S1299 does prohibit requiring a digital ID and does prohibit tracking.

### 4. FAST Act (S1423) did not fully pass

**Gemini claimed:** Idaho "just passed the FAST Act" allowing stablecoin payments to vendors.

**Reality:** S1423 passed the Senate 22-13 (March 27, 2026) and was referred to House Commerce and Human Resources Committee. No confirmation of full passage or signing. "Passed" implies enactment — it passed one chamber.

### 5. State Board of Education Policy IV.C — wrong citation

**Gemini claimed:** Policy IV.C governs IdahoPTV intellectual property, claiming ownership of "material gathered in the course of producing."

**Reality:** The IP policy appears to be Section V.M., not IV.C. IdahoPTV's internal Production Staff Guidelines do contain the "material gathered" language. The quote is real; the policy number is wrong.

### 6. Idaho Code 74-102 — wrong section for personal notes exception

**Gemini claimed:** Idaho Code § 74-102 provides a "personal notes" exception for public officials.

**Reality:** The personal notes exception ("personal notes created by a public official solely for his own use shall not be a public record as long as such personal notes are not shared with any other person or entity") is in § 74-101(13), not § 74-102. Section 74-102 is the "Right to Examine" provision.

## YELLOW FLAGS (partially accurate, needs qualification)

### 7. IFF rating of SB 1299

**Gemini claimed:** IFF "rated this bill lower" after amendments removed the phone-search provision.

**Reality:** IFF gave SB 1299 a +1 rating and criticized the amendments, specifically naming the removal of the "incidentally observed" provision. However, the rating was NOT lowered — it remained +1.

### 8. "Saving College Sports Executive Order"

**Gemini claimed:** Signed "early 2026."

**Reality:** Actual title: "Urgent National Action To Save College Sports." Signed April 3, 2026 (3 days before this conversation). Operative sections take effect August 1, 2026.

### 9. Raw Story v. OpenAI

**Gemini claimed:** The case shows the CMI removal theory was rejected.

**Reality:** Dismissal (November 2024) was on standing (no concrete injury shown), not on the merits. In the parallel *Intercept Media v. OpenAI* case, Judge Rakoff denied OpenAI's motion to dismiss the same CMI theory — creating a split within S.D.N.Y.

### 10. Idaho Code § 31-2903

**Gemini claimed:** Enacted 2025, allows electronic documents to satisfy "original" requirements for recording.

**Reality:** The statute exists and says what Gemini claims, but it's been in Idaho Code for years as part of the Uniform Real Property Electronic Recording Act — not a 2025 enactment.

## CONCEPTS WORTH KEEPING (despite citation errors)

The following ideas from the Gemini conversation are conceptually sound even though their Idaho-specific legal grounding was fabricated:

- **Digital Sovereignty as a framework** (data ownership, infrastructure autonomy, institutional literacy)
- **Privacy-Publicity axis** as a tension in digital identity
- **Individual-Group dynamics** applied to legislative/institutional contexts
- **Work for Hire as the IP boundary** between employer and personal production
- **The "Subtle Knife" metaphor** — every digital interface leaks metadata; every window opened must be closeable
- **Selective Disclosure** — proving a fact without revealing the underlying data
- **The personal notes exception** in Idaho public records law (real, just miscited)

These deserve further research grounded in *actual Idaho law*. See: [[Digital Sovereignty]].
