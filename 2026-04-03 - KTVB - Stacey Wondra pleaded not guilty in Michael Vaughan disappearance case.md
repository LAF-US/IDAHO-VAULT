PAYETTE COUNTY, Idaho — The man accused of kidnapping and killing 5-year-old Michael Vaughan in 2021 pleaded not guilty to the charges against him in Payette County district court.

Stacey Wondra is charged with first-degree murder, kidnapping, destruction of evidence and a persistent violator charge [in connection with Vaughan’s death](https://www.ktvb.com/article/news/crime/man-accused-kidnapping-michael-vaughan-appears-court-ahead-preliminary-hearing/277-b1e7aef8-8cfa-49f7-b669-fec5e868e258). Vaughan, affectionately referred to as “Monkey," disappeared in July 2021 from outside his home in Fruitland.

On Friday, he pleaded not guilty to all counts.

Law enforcement extradited Wondra from Arizona late last year, where he had been serving time for unrelated charges. Authorities served Wondra with a warrant related to Vaughan's disappearance and death.

Fruitland Police previously said they had credible information from someone living in the Wondras' house that the young boy's body was there. Cadaver detection dogs alerted to the presence of human remains, but none were subsequently found.  

A  [video from November 2022](https://www.ktvb.com/article/news/local/michael-vaughans-mom-video-showing-suspect-confessing-knowing-body-buried/277-52dcdb00-8a92-4e78-b824-326a6811b2ff)  released by Vaughan’s mother Brandi shows Stacey guiding detectives on a FaceTime call to where he said he saw his then-wife, Sarah Wondra, dig a hole to bury the body.  

"We expected him to plead guilty," Vaughan's mother Brandi Neal told KTVB following the arraignment. "I don't believe that he's going to get out of any of those charges."

Sarah Wondra was initially charged with failing to report a death in November 2022 in connection with the boy's disappearance, but the charge was dropped that spring. 

Fruitland Police Chief JD Huff previously told KTVB he still believes Michael was buried in the Wondras' backyard, and was later moved.  

Law enforcement extradited Wondra from Arizona to Idaho in November 2025, where he was serving time for unrelated charges.

Wondra will next appear in court for a scheduling conference on April 15 at 10 a.m.
