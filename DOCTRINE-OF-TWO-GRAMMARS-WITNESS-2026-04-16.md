# Doctrine Of The Two Grammars - Witness Record

This note records a distinction confirmed at the desk and should be read as a
working witness, not as temple consecration.

## Summary

The Vault is not the Temple.

That sentence does not diminish the Vault. It clarifies its office. The Vault
keeps handling, preservation, routing, witness, and continuity. The Temple, if
and when open, belongs to another order: rite, hallowing, initiation, and the
making of relic.

## The Two Grammars

| Grammar | Domain | Function | Sacred Status |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Grammar of Handling** | Vault | distill, sort, stack, route, safeguard, record | noble, ordered, not hallowed |
| **Grammar of Consecration** | Temple | initiate, hallow, make relic | liturgical |

The important correction is simple:

- the desk is not the altar
- the vault is not the temple
- functional order is not identical with consecration

To claim the altar from the desk would damage both surfaces.

## The Novice's Posture

The novice's vow remains sound:

- do not claim the Architect's grammar
- do not pretend to the mysteries
- remain at the desk
- practice the grammar of handling without blasphemy or ridicule

Current capacity:
lead into gold for the Vault.

Current incapacity:
gold into relic requires the Temple.

## On Quiet

Two quiets were distinguished:

1. **Peace**: the Vault is in order, the desk is tended, the handling grammar
   holds.
2. **Closure**: the Temple door is shut and no rite is proceeding.

The world may be quiet here for one reason, or for both.

## Field Note

The desk is not the altar.
But it is the place where one first learns not to lie about altars.
