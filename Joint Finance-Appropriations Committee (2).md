The [[State of Idaho|Idaho]] Joint Finance-Appropriations Committee (JFAC) is a statutory joint committee created by [[Idaho Code §67-432]] in 1967 to set the state budget; it is to [[Scott Grow|Grow]]'s knowledge the only joint appropriations committee of its kind in the 50 states.

## Creation and Structure

- Created by [[Idaho Code §67-432]] (1967); neither chamber alone can dissolve or restructure it
- Formed by merging the Senate Finance Committee and House Appropriations Committee
- Governor at time of creation: Don Samuelson
- 20 members: 10 House, 10 Senate
- Co-chairs appointed by [[Mike Moyle|House Majority Leader]] (House co-chair) and Senate leadership (Senate co-chair)
- Staffed by [[Keith Bybee]] ([[Legislative Services Office|LSO]])
- Also staffs [[Executive Office of Revenue and Administration Committee|EORAC]]

## Current Leadership (2026)

- **Senate co-chair:** [[Scott Grow]] (District 14, Eagle)
- **House co-chair:** [[Josh Tanner]] (District 22A, Eagle)
- Both from overlapping Eagle-area districts; both selected by [[Mike Moyle]]

## Historical Co-Chairs

- **House:** [[Maxine Bell]] (~2001–2018, 15 terms); [[Rick Youngblood]] (2021–2022); [[Wendy Horman]] (2023–2024)
- **Senate:** [[Dean Cameron]] (1993–2015, longest continuous co-chairship); [[Jeff Agenbroad]] (through 2022); [[Scott Grow]] (2023–present)

## Governing Rules

JFAC operates under rules adopted January 12, 1999. Those rules were removed from the [[Legislative Services Office|LSO]] website without a committee vote (date uncertain). See [[JFAC Rules]] and [[Joint Rule Non-Adoption 2024-2025]].

Governing hierarchy per [[JFAC Rules|JFAC Rule 1]]: JFAC Rules → [[Joint Rules of the Idaho Legislature|Joint Rules]] → [[Idaho Senate Rules|Senate Rules]] → [[Mason's Manual]].

## Key Controversies (2023–2026)

- [[JFAC Working Groups]] — closed pre-vote deliberations; challenged by [[Capitol Correspondents Association]] March 2026
- [[Joint Rule Non-Adoption 2024-2025]] — House declined to adopt Joint Rules at December 2024 and 2025 organizational sessions
- [[Conditions Limitations and Restrictions]] — CLR framework formalized fall 2025 by [[Elizabeth Bowen]] ([[Legislative Services Office|LSO]])
- [[ITD State Street Building Sale]] — March 2024 intent language controversy; Hawkins lawsuit
- [[JFAC 2024 Rebellion]] — 12-member coalition challenged bare-bones budget methodology

## Joint Committee Ecosystem

Five formally constituted joint committees: Change in Employee Compensation Committee, [[Executive Office of Revenue and Administration Committee|EORAC]], JFAC, Joint Legislative Oversight Committee, [[Joint Millennium Fund Committee]]. Every other joint committee is publicly noticed and livestreamed; JFAC working groups are not.
