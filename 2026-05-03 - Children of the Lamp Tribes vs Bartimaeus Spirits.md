# Children of the Lamp Tribes vs Bartimaeus Spirits

## Core Comparison

The two series organize magical beings at different levels of abstraction.

**Children of the Lamp** organizes djinn as **tribes**: social and moral groupings with customs, rituals, leaders, taboos, and inherited alignment. The tribe is a civic unit. It tells you where the being stands in a culture, a duty structure, and a luck economy.

**Bartimaeus** organizes spirits as **classes / levels / individual names**: a taxonomy of being defined by power scale, form, and summoned relation to masters. The spirit is an ontological unit. It tells you what kind of entity it is, how it manifests, and how it behaves under binding.

So the difference is:

- **Tribe** = collective identity with law and belonging
- **Spirit class** = species/level identity with capability and form

## What Each System Optimizes For

Children of the Lamp optimizes for:

- inherited affiliation
- ritual and initiation
- political alignment
- family line and tribe leadership
- law around wishes, seals, and bindings

Bartimaeus optimizes for:

- hierarchy of spirits
- name, form, and level
- master / servant relation
- personal wit, memory, and survival
- the tension between essence and summonable manifestation

In short: one system is **tribal**, the other is **taxonomic**.

## The Abstract Shape

If abstracted, the two systems become different ways of encoding meaning:

1. **Children of the Lamp** encodes meaning through **belonging**.
   - who your people are
   - which side you are on
   - what rites you must pass through
   - what your tribe permits or forbids

2. **Bartimaeus** encodes meaning through **form and relation**.
   - what kind of spirit you are
   - what name you answer to
   - what level you occupy
   - who can summon, bind, or release you

That means the first system is closer to a **civic fantasy institution**, while the second is closer to a **summoning ontology**.

## Vault Relevance

For the vault, this distinction matters because it gives two different persistence models:

- A **tribe** maps cleanly to an institution, council, or guild-like surface.
- A **spirit** maps cleanly to a persona, runtime entity, or summonable capability.

So:

- `Marid / Jinn / Jann / Ifrit` are not just categories; they are governed collectives.
- `Djinn / Spirit / Level / True Name` are not just labels; they are operational identity axes.

The vault can use both models, but it should not collapse them:

- tribes are for **shared law and alignment**
- spirits are for **instance identity and binding**

## Practical Synthesis

Taken together, the series suggest a useful design rule:

> A stable system needs both a social class and a metaphysical class.

That is the useful abstraction:

- one axis for **belonging**
- one axis for **being**
- one axis for **binding**
- one axis for **power**

That is exactly the kind of split the Persona / Institution / Runtime work in the vault is already circling.
