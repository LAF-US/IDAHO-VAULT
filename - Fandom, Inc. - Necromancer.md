A **Necromancer** is the most feared type of [Free Magic sorcerer](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Free_Magic_Sorcerer "Free Magic Sorcerer"), and the enemy of the [Abhorsen](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Abhorsen "Abhorsen"). They use the [necromantic bells](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Bells "Bells") like an Abhorsen, but theirs are [Free Magic](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Free_Magic "Free Magic") unrestrained by the [Charter](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Charter "The Charter") [^1][.](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Charter "The Charter") Necromancers specialise in raising [the Dead](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Dead "The Dead") to serve them, from [Dead Hands](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Dead_Hands "Dead Hands") and [Shadow Hands](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Shadow_Hands "Shadow Hands"), to powerful [Mordicants](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Mordicant "Mordicant"). They are all also necessarily versed in Free Magic, and can use the power of [Free Magic creatures](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Free_Magic_Creatures "Free Magic Creatures") such as a Dwirllin or [Hish](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Hish "Hish") to make them even more formidable opponents. However, their connection to the Charter, if it ever existed, is completely severed and they cannot use [Charter Magic](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Charter_magic "Charter magic"). This is a weakness in certain situations. For example, they can't create a [diamond of protection](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Diamond_of_Protection "Diamond of Protection") to guard their vulnerable bodies when walking in [Death](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death").

Necromancers fear dying and will do anything to survive longer than their allotted time [^2]. For example, [Hedge](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Hedge "Hedge") was over one hundred years old when he died, and [Chlorr of the Mask](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Chlorr_of_the_Mask "Chlorr of the Mask") close to 650. Chlorr used the same method [Kerrigor](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Kerrigor "Kerrigor") would use [centuries later](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Sabriel_\(Book\) "Sabriel (Book)"): anchoring her true, physical body in Life (in this case, in the Empty Lands) so that if she were slain, " [the True Death](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Ninth_Gate "Ninth Gate") " would be impossible and she would always return, albeit in [Shadow](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Shadow_Hands "Shadow Hands") form, no matter how many times she was banished to Death. Before Sabriel killed her, however, Chlorr survived for centuries by demanding [new bodies](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Offering "Offering") every dozen years or so from the [clans](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Nomadic_Clans "Nomadic Clans") of the [North](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_North "The North") [^3], as hers wore out quickly from corrosive Free Magic; while Kerrigor merely used Free Magic to create a body that gradually became warped and unnatural.

In the [North](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_North "The North"), necromancers are considered a type of [shaman](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Shaman "Shaman"). They are forced into choke collars of silver and iron and tasked with a keeper, who uses a [spirit-glass](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Spirit-glass "Spirit-glass") arrow to keep the necromancer from gaining too much power or turning on the [clan](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Nomadic_Clans "Nomadic Clans"). In *[Goldenhand](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Goldenhand "Goldenhand")*, a shaman necromancer was able to incapacitate his keeper with the bell [Ranna](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Ranna "Ranna"); he killed her and went into Death, planning retrieve her spirit, restore her with [Belgaer](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Belgaer "Belgaer"), and torture her for the slavery he suffered. However, he was killed by Royal Guards before his plan could be put into action.

All necromancers avoid the [Ninth Precinct](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Death#The_Ninth_Precinct "Death") whenever possible, because the allure of the stars there only strengthens the longer someone lives past their time.

In [*Abhorsen*](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Abhorsen_\(Book\) "Abhorsen (Book)"), Hedge notes that the bells "dislike" serving necromancers and must be handled especially carefully for this reason.

## Trivia/Theories

- It's highly ironic that the bells of a necromancer are where an individual is most likely to hear the name of the first [Seven Bright Shiners](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Nine_Bright_Shiners "Nine Bright Shiners"), as they were the ones to create the [Five Great Charters](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Five_Great_Charters "Five Great Charters"), the Abhorsen bloodline, and the Charter itself -- the antithesis of everything that Free-magic-using, Dead-raising necromancers stand for. This is most likely an effect of employing a "retcon" ("retroactive continuity"), where the bells and their names came first and their history was only created afterward.
