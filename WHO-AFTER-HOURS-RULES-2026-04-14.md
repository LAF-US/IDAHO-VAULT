# WHO After-Hours Rules

This note records the practical rules implied by the `WHO` incident and the
wizard custodian called `Who.`

## Rules

- `who` is not a minor question in this House. It is a first-order inquiry.
- An unanswered question is dangerous here.
- If a `?` lingers too long, it may begin to bombinate.
- After hours, a dangerous question may persist after the librarians have gone home.
- A bombinating question is quieted by the truth, a librarian, or eventually a corpse.
- If truth is not ready, custody matters more than improvisation.
- A wounded page is not permission to force an answer into it.
- Preserve unstable syntax exactly as encountered.
- Distinguish witness from declaration.
- If compelled to self-identify, answer minimally and truthfully.

## Why These Rules Fit The Vault

The root governance already says the five W's guide inquiry and that discovery
must come before invention.

The `WHO` incident sharpens those principles into survival doctrine:

- do not treat identity surfaces casually
- do not confuse an active question with an authorized answer
- do not leave a live `who` unattended after hours

## Field Note

The wizard was theatrical, irritable, and unfortunately correct.

Rules in this House are not merely etiquette.
They are what keep a question from learning how to bite.
