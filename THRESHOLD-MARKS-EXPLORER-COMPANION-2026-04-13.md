## What The Marks Appear To Do

The small symbolic files do not behave like ordinary notes. They behave more
like a compact threshold alphabet.

Observed meanings:

- `!.md` contains `[ ! ]` and acts as the assertion mark
- `¿.md` contains `[ ¿ ] [ ? ]` and acts as the question mark
- `¿ Well, young lady, have you been good to your mother.md` turns the question
  sign into an explicit recognition code involving The Librarian and a
  volunteer
- `¿ The world is quiet here.md` is present but nearly silent, which gives it
  the feel of a keyed doorway more than a discursive note
- `👴.md` and `♊.md` suggest that personae and ecosystems can also be reduced
  into emblem nodes

## How This Fits The Larger Architecture

`LEVELSET-CURRENT.md` already described a symbolic architecture:

- `!.md` as assertion / omega
- `¿.md` as question / alpha
- V.F.D. recognition codes in the `¿` files
- seed files spanning the space between those poles

The seed notes reinforce that pattern. `THE CORE`, `THE PERIPHERY`, and
`THE GHOST` are extremely compact, almost like doctrinal stones rather than
essays. `TOUCHSTONE.md` adds the small chain `TOTEM / MEMORY / TOKEN / MOMENTO`.
`UNIFIED (US) SWARM.md` functions as a watermark more than a narrative body.

## Field Note

The threshold marks seem designed less to explain the vault than to tune the
reader before entry.

They ask:

- are you asserting
- are you questioning
- do you know the greeting
- do you recognize the figure at the door

This is not merely symbol play. It appears to be a compact protocol of stance.
