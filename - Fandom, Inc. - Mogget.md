**Mogget** is the bound form of [**Yrael**, the Eighth Bright Shiner](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Yrael "Yrael"). As Yrael, he refused to take sides in the original [binding](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Orannis%27_Binding "Orannis' Binding") of [Orannis](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Orannis "Orannis"), thinking he would lose part of himself either way. As such, the [Seven Bright Shiners](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Nine_Bright_Shiners "Nine Bright Shiners") bound him to forever serve the [Abhorsen](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Abhorsen "Abhorsen") as punishment.

## Binding

Yrael's binding is a red leather collar or belt with a miniature [bell](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Bells "Bells") attached. This collar is one of the last known [Wallmaker](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Wallmaker "Wallmaker") relics. The bell attached was originally [Saraneth](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Saraneth "Saraneth"), the Binder, but was replaced with a miniature [Ranna](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Ranna "Ranna") at the end of [*Sabriel*](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Sabriel_\(Book\) "Sabriel (Book)").

He usually appears as a small, white cat, but can also appear as an albino dwarf, although not in the presence of an Abhorsen unless he has his or her permission, ever since he was forbidden by Jerizael, the 48th Abhorsen. It is this form he appears in to [Terciel](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Terciel "Terciel"), with whom he shared a close relationship.

Mogget occasionally attempts to persuade other characters to remove his collar, which would set him free. Only one with Abhorsen blood can release him, however. When released, he assumes his true form as [Yrael](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Yrael "Yrael"), once seen as a tall, slender, man-like form of white, hot light and an incredibly huge mouth;[^1] once seen merely as an incredibly tall shining humanoid with no features.[^2] When he is free, he is usually bent on destroying the Abhorsen and all his/her descendants as an act of revenge for his enslavement.

Mogget's bonds can weaken over time, particularly if generations of Abhorsens ignore him. This was the situation in *[Clariel: The Lost Abhorsen](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Clariel:_The_Lost_Abhorsen "Clariel: The Lost Abhorsen")*, where Mogget was able to manipulate [Clariel](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Clariel "Clariel") into taking him to the Royal Palace in [Belisaere](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Belisaere "Belisaere"). He planned to kill the King and use [his blood](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Royal_line "Royal line") to break the [Great Charter Stones](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Great_Charter_Stones "Great Charter Stones"), which would free him and all other bound [Free Magic creatures](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Free_Magic_Creatures "Free Magic Creatures"). However, his plan failed and [Belatiel](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Belatiel "Belatiel") renewed Mogget's bonds.

Six hundred years later, after his assistance in the second binding of Orannis, Mogget was permanently freed and all his memories restored by [Sabriel](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Sabriel "Sabriel") wielding [Belgaer](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Belgaer "Belgaer").

## Current status

After his release, Mogget disappears to the North, but still returns occasionally to visit [Sameth](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Sameth "Sameth"), with whom he has a strange friendship. It is this friendship (and the promise of fish) that leads him to help in the fight against [Chlorr](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Chlorr "Chlorr") in [Goldenhand](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Goldenhand "Goldenhand"). He claims the event is none of his affair, but he displays himself as The Great [Athask](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Athask "Athask") Cat, totem to the clan which bears his name, which causes them to flee the battle. This additionally places his protection on Sameth and [Ferin](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Ferin "Ferin").

## Trivia/Theories

- In unbound form, Mogget has tried to kill Sabriel three times, according to Sameth. (We see two of these times in *Sabriel*.)
- Mogget's unique and ongoing friendship with Sameth might be due to Sam being a Wallmaker, as [Terciel](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Terciel "Terciel") thought Mogget had originally had some special connection to the Wallmakers.
- In addition to punishment, the Seven may have bound Mogget to protect Life as they had already done to so many other dangerous Free Magic creatures: he would be the only Bright Shiner remaining at full strength and he had already expressed his indifference to the welfare of living things.
- *Goldenhand* is the only book where Mogget is unbound throughout.
- Mogget is the only character to appear in every book in the [series](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Old_Kingdom_series "Old Kingdom series").
