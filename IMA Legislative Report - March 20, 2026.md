[**HB 860**](https://4rl8bkfbb.cc.rs6.net/tn.jsp?f=001BSH15BIY9dzUywYVNENQeYAG8fMFs6_rXKvAAZ-3ffWEmj3y6OYyOkJeE6NYWHb3R0klF-EsmdW9EWFcXouVGcNoXVSxqK8yzdz5LYK9XKxwq4uJms9L9RWvSkvsqsDacUzBnVtzBQ9iiER-W4_6u4P6GdMGYQOBYmwMMq76mn4x99A1CKMOWZniuOytpnjJr-SW5C1uNf1Zhpf_YMPnjA==&c=SRUb2mTd1RVxK5dXYlevFxIuSdQj3CHywJeo-GrPVX-skw_zcubljg==&ch=zY8UUBxhILeQxPBpAqujEfhvLXCNiKwGOP6k0wuwXCmK18WDLGE7tw==), legislation amending Idaho’s parental consent laws, passed the full House on March 16 with an overwhelming 68-0-2 vote. That momentum continued March 18, when the Senate State Affairs Committee held a hearing on the bill, led by Sen. Kelly Anthon (R-Rupert). ﻿  
﻿﻿  
﻿At the request of Sen. Anthon, the Committee voted to send HB 860 to General Orders of the full Senate for possible amendments. As part of that process, Sen. Anthon introduced two amendments, one of which would allow physicians to assess and treat drug addicted newborns they suspect are experiencing withdrawal – a top priority of the IMA. This clarification is important to ensure these infants can receive prompt, medically appropriate care without delay. Additionally, HB 860 contains changes that address several IMA priorities including: 

·     Allow for the treatment of minors seeking care in an emergency directly related to an allegation of a crime committed against them 

·     Allow pregnant minors to consent to their own care from the prenatal through peripartum period 

·     Contact to the Idaho Crisis and Suicide 988 Hotline for minors experiencing a mental health crisis, while also permitting follow-up contact within 48 hours to reassess their safety

·     A modified emergency standard to include addressing serious bodily harm to the child

·     Clear guidelines for blanket parental consent forms to allow treatment of minors

·     Prohibits disclosure of medical records if the records contain information related to physical abuse, abandonment, or neglect 

The Senate voted to apply the needed amendments on March 20. IMA sincerely thanks Sen. Anthon for working with us once again to help address the unintended consequences created in 2024.
