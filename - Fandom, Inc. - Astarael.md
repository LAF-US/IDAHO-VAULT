**Astarael** is the seventh of the [Nine Bright Shiners](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Nine_Bright_Shiners "Nine Bright Shiners"), and the final bell. In [The Beginning](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Beginning "The Beginning"), Astarael became part of [the Charter](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Charter "The Charter") as the Abhorsen bloodline. As a [bell](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Bells "Bells"), Astarael is the Weeper, who throws all who hear it, including the wielder, deep into [Death](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death"). It was wielded by [Lirael](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Lirael "Lirael") at the binding of [Orannis](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Orannis "Orannis").

![Astarael in the well, by Bre Galloway.](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Astarael_by_bre_galloway.jpg)

Astarael in the well, by Bre Galloway.

Some vestige of her power and presence remains in the [Old Kingdom](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Old_Kingdom "Old Kingdom") in the tunnels below the [Abhorsen's House](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Abhorsen%27s_House "Abhorsen's House"). When seen in *[Abhorsen](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Abhorsen_\(Book\) "Abhorsen (Book)")* under the well, she is an impossibly tall, pale woman. The River of Death follows in her wake, and all [Charter magic](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Charter_magic "Charter magic") fails in her presence.

This remnant of Astarael traps [Mogget](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Mogget "Mogget") / [Yrael](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Yrael "Yrael") and communes with him after [Lirael](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Lirael "Lirael"), [Sameth](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Sameth "Sameth"), and the [Disreputable Dog](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Disreputable_Dog "Disreputable Dog") flee. Mogget later says she gave him "a second chance."

In *[Goldenhand](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Goldenhand_\(Book\) "Goldenhand (Book)")*, Lirael has to ring Astarael twice in order to bring " [Clariel](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Clariel "Clariel") " to the Ninth Gate, as the spirit fragment otherwise couldn't move, being chained to the riverbed by [Chlorr of the Mask](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Chlorr_of_the_Mask "Chlorr of the Mask").

## Bell Sound

Astarael begins softly, like a tuning fork lightly struck, but grows, like a pure note blown by a trumpeter of inexhaustible breath, until there is nothing but the sound of the bell in the minds of those who hear it.[^1]

## Trivia

- There is some question of how Astarael gave herself to the Charter. In *[Lirael](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Lirael_\(Book\) "Lirael (Book)")*, the [Disreputable Dog](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Disreputable_Dog "Disreputable Dog") (or Kibeth), after being questioned by Lirael, reveals that the Five of the [Seven](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Nine_Bright_Shiners "Nine Bright Shiners") disappeared completely into the Charter, while the other Two (Kibeth being one) chose to live on in Life in some fashion. Then, in *[Nicholas Sayre and the Creature in the Case](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Nicholas_Sayre_and_the_Creature_in_the_Case "Nicholas Sayre and the Creature in the Case")*, the [Hrule](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Hrule "Hrule") calls the [Abhorsens](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Abhorsen "Abhorsen") "Astarael's get," implying that Astarael did join bodily with the Charter in [The Beginning](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Beginning "The Beginning"), and made the Abhorsen Bloodline. This would mean there is still one of the Seven in Life. Her well at Abhorsen's House can also only be opened by her "children": those of the Abhosen bloodline.
- It is may be that even Shiners who gave themselves completely over to the Charter can "exist" as echoes or presences.
- Astarael's eternal mourning, combined with her scent of rosemary (meaning "fidelity in love") and amaranth ("the flower that never fades"), suggests she might be mourning the separation from, or the death of, one she loved.
- As we are told Astarael is forever separate from the godlike Charter, she may mourn this, especially if this happened because she was the Shiner to split Orannis.
- Astarael's vestigal form suggests she was female.
