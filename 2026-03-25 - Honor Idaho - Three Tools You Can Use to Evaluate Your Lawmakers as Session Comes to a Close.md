As the legislative session wraps up, something predictable happens.

The speeches get louder. The mailers get shinier. The social media posts bend the truth to win over unsuspecting Idahoans.

And yet, the one thing voters actually need becomes harder to find: the truth.

Not the spin. Not the press releases. Not the carefully worded statements about “fighting for Idaho values.” Just a simple answer to a simple question:

Did your lawmaker actually vote for limited government?

If you care about that question, then the good news is this: you don’t have to guess. There are now a few tools that cut through the noise and show you what really happened.

Let’s walk through three of the most useful ones.

**First, the Idaho Freedom Foundation’s Freedom and Spending Indexes.**

If you’re looking for a gold standard, this is it.

These indexes don’t rely on rhetoric or personality. They measure votes. Specifically, they track how lawmakers vote on issues tied to limited government, individual liberty, and fiscal responsibility.

What makes this tool especially valuable is how closely it aligns with the Idaho Republican Party platform. In other words, it doesn’t just tell you how someone voted, it shows you how those votes stack up against the principles many Republican voters say they believe in.

The Spending Index adds another layer that’s often overlooked. It highlights who is consistently voting for bigger government spending and who is resisting it. If limited government matters to you, the budget is where the rubber meets the road.

It’s worth mentioning that IFF analysts rate hundreds of bills each year, giving Freedom and Spending Index followers a wider view of trends than other scorecards that might only analyze 30 or 40 handpicked bills.

You can explore both indexes at [IdahoFreedom.org](https://idahofreedom.org/), and it’s worth your time. This is one of the clearest ways to separate messaging from measurable behavior.

**Second, the Idaho GOP Platform Scorecard.**

The Idaho GOP Platform Scorecard uses four artificial intelligence models to evaluate legislation against the actual Idaho GOP platform. Then it tracks how lawmakers vote on those bills.

Think about that for a moment. Instead of relying on interpretations or selective examples, this tool systematically compares votes to stated party principles.

So if a lawmaker says they support the platform, this scorecard gives you a way to verify that claim.

You can find this tool at [IDGOPScorecard.com](https://idgopscorecard.com/). It’s a straightforward, data-driven way to see who is walking the talk.

**Finally, the Votes with Democrats scorecard.**

Now, this one tends to get attention, and for good reason.

This report tracks how often Republican lawmakers vote with Democrats. And while some crossover is expected, especially on routine or broadly supported issues, patterns matter.

If a lawmaker occasionally votes across the aisle, that’s part of the legislative process. But if it becomes frequent or consistent, that tells a different story.

This scorecard doesn’t speculate. It doesn’t accuse. It simply documents. Because when you see the data laid out clearly, you can decide for yourself whether those voting patterns reflect your expectations for conservative representation.

You can review this at [VoteswithDems.com](https://voteswithdems.com/). (This will become fully available after the 2026 session concludes.)

At the end of the day, this is really about something bigger than politics.

It’s about trust.
