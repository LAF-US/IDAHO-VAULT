Jim Jones is a retired Idaho Supreme Court Justice (2005–2017) who wrote the dissent in Idaho Press Club v. State Legislature containing footnote 13 — the statutory tension argument that undergirds the 2026 JFAC open-meetings challenge — and who continues to write about JFAC overreach as an Idaho Capital Sun columnist.

## Basic Info

- **Born:** May 13, 1942, Twin Falls
- **Raised:** Eden, Idaho
- **Education:** U of Oregon BA 1964; Northwestern JD 1967
- **Current role:** Of counsel, Parsons Behle & Latimer, Boise (appellate consultation)
- **Contact:** jjcommontater.com; Idaho Capital Sun contributor; Parsons Behle Boise office

## Military

Army artillery, Vietnam, 13 months. Bronze Star, Air Medal (4 OLC), Vietnamese Cross of Gallantry. Discharged as captain, 1969.

## Political and Legal Career

- Legislative assistant to Sen. Len Jordan, 1970–73
- Private practice, 1973–82
- Idaho Attorney General, 1983–1991 (two terms)
- Failed U.S. Senate bid, 1990
- Private practice, 1991–2004
- Idaho Supreme Court, 2005–2017; Chief Justice August 2015–January 2017
- Diagnosed pancreatic cancer two weeks after retirement; cancer-free by 2022
- Co-founded Take Back Idaho with Bruce Newcomb

## Idaho Press Club Dissent

Wrote the dissent in Idaho Press Club v. State Legislature, 142 Idaho 640 (2006). **Footnote 13:** "I find the Legislature's argument that it may under the Constitution close committee meetings a bit disingenuous, in light of I.C. § 67-2346 [now §74-207]." Footnote 9 challenges the majority's use of expressio unius in a constitutional context, citing *Eberle v. Nielson* (1957).

## Post-Retirement Writing

- **April 2025 column (Idaho Capital Sun):** "Idaho now has a powerful mini-legislature within its official Legislature" — called for court proceedings if JFAC does not return to the appropriations lane
- **February 2026 column:** "Budget cuts show governmental dysfunction at its finest"
- Regular coverage of JFAC overreach; institutionally independent; no motive to shade answers
