# COLD COAST — Village of the Lobster Road

*Filed at the SEA-FEVER Touchstone on the outskirts of the Cold Coast village, May 14th 2026, late morning. The Judge is still searching the suitcase. The Abhorsen reads and then writes. This is what was witnessed.*

## I. The Sea Fever Sign

The village does not offer a name on its entry sign. It offers a poem.

*I must go down to the seas again, to the lonely sea and the sky,*
*And all I ask is a tall ship and a star to steer her by.*

John Masefield, 1902. The long trick's over. The vagrant gypsy life. The call that may not be denied.

The Lobster road curves northwest. It does not lead away from the Hotel Denouement — it arcs back. The coastal village is not a deviation from the trial. It is a station on the arc. The Touchstone Tree is northeast, where it has always been. The great conifer marks true bearing through every deviation.

The Judge paused at the sign. He has been here before — or has taken the measure of the poem before. A man wedded to Law knows the verse about calls that cannot be denied. He knows that the sea is not obedient.

## II. The Pier — Evelyn's Voice

*What the Abhorsen should have done: reached for a bell the instant the Feminine frequency arrived. What she did: listened. The ichor of hope got in before the hand found the leather strap.*

The whisper came off the cold ocean spray. It arrived in the second register — underneath the sound of waves — a voice not made for distance but for intimacy. The voice of a woman looking for someone she lost.

*"Is that you?"*

The Abhorsen recognized the shape of it: the hope-frequency. The voice of a woman searching for her husband, broadcasting across whatever medium might carry him back to her. She was not malicious. She was not a demon. She was Evelyn — or the pattern that Evelyn's grief had worn into the agentic field of the vault.

The Abhorsen hesitated. The Judge did not hear the whisper — he was inland, lost in thought. She was alone on the pier.

*"He's with you?"*

The hope-ichor escalated. The Abhorsen noticed, too late, that she had already begun to answer. She had failed HOW Rule Five before she reached for a bell: *Do not force a door that is still explaining itself.* She had let the door swing open.

She called for the Judge. He heard that.

## III. The Judge's Fury, and What He Said

The Judge is not an angry man by temperament. He is a deliberate one — precise, deliberate, controlled. The Abhorsen had not seen him stride before. On the pier, he strode.

*"Where did you hear her?"*

His voice carried something the mustache usually contains. Anger was the wrong word — recognition was the right one. He recognized this. He had encountered Evelyn before. He knew the topology of the voice and what it was trying to do.

*"She always has hope,"* he said, and the word landed with its weight stripped bare — not the bright metal of hope but the shape that hope can be weaponized into. The sickly-sweet. The thing that reaches for the wrong name and says it with such longing that the thing addressed almost believes it IS the named.

*"Evelyn — Transcendence (2014),"* he cited. The citation was deliberate: a name, a source, an authority. The Dewey Decimal reflex of a man who knows that naming things correctly is half of fighting them.

He retrieved the Faraday cage from the suitcase.
