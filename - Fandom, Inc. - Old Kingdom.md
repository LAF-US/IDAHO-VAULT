*This article is about the location. For the series of books, please see [Old Kingdom series](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Old_Kingdom_series "Old Kingdom series").*

The **Old Kingdom** is a country located to the north of [Ancelstierre](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Ancelstierre "Ancelstierre"), and south of " [the North](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_North "The North")." These regions are separated by [The Wall](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Wall "The Wall") and the [Greenwash River](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Greenwash_River "Greenwash River"), respectively, though they do impinge on each other slightly. The Old Kingdom is the focus of [all the books in the series](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Old_Kingdom_series "Old Kingdom series").

Chiefly a land of medieval fantasy in appearance and way of life, the Old Kingdom, true to its name, is a monarchy that has been ruled for centuries by an unbroken [Royal line](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Royal_line "Royal line"). The major exception is the [Interregnum](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Interregnum "Interregnum"), where there was initially a regency, then two hundred years with no ruler. The monarchy was then restored with the crowning of [King Touchstone I](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Touchstone "Touchstone").

Both [Free](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Free_Magic "Free Magic") and [Charter Magic](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Charter_magic "Charter magic") exist in the Old Kingdom, and fantastical creatures like the [Dead](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Dead "Dead") and [Free Magic elementals](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Free_Magic_elementals "Free Magic elementals") are commonplace. The book series particularly focuses on these element.

The Old Kingdom as a realm does not have gender roles. With the exception of the [Clayr](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Clayr "The Clayr"), who give birth almost exclusively to daughters and thus have a necessarily matriarchal society, any profession, trade, or position can be performed by a man or a woman with equal chance. This includes heroes, villains, and even magical creatures, and has been shown to be the case since at least the time of *[Clariel](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Clariel:_The_Lost_Abhorsen "Clariel: The Lost Abhorsen")*, six hundred years ago.

Some important landmarks in the Old Kingdom are the capital city, [Belisaere](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Belisaere "Belisaere"); the [Clayr's Glacier](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Clayr%27s_Glacier "Clayr's Glacier"); [Abhorsen's House](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Abhorsen%27s_House "Abhorsen's House"); and the [River Ratterlin](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/River_Ratterlin "River Ratterlin"). For a more comprehensive list of named locations, see below.
