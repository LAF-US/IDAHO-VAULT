**Mevolent** was an incredibly powerful [Elemental](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Elemental "Elemental") and was one of the very few to turn evil. He lead [a faction](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Mevolent%27s_Forces "Mevolent's Forces") during [The War](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/The_War "The War") against the [Sanctuary](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/The_Irish_Sanctuary "The Irish Sanctuary") with the goal of bringing the [Faceless Ones](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Faceless_Ones "Faceless Ones") back to this reality. Mevolent is mentioned often during the books. [Derek](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Derek_Landy "Derek Landy") has stated that Mevolent is indeed dead,[^1] though he appears in an alternate reality where he won the war and enslaved mortals.

## Biography

### History

Before his rise to power he served under [Arthur Dagan](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Arthur_Dagan "Arthur Dagan") 's grandfather and somehow came across [The Unnamed](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/The_Unnamed "The Unnamed") who would later become his master. Mevolent was a dedicated follower of the Faceless Ones, and attempted to open a portal and allow them entry back to Earth. His army was lead by his [three generals](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/The_Three_Generals "The Three Generals"). Mevolent engaged the Sanctuary starting the war. Although equally matched at the beginning Mevolent's side began to lose. In a desperate attempt Mevolent ordered an attack against the leaders of the Sanctuary. [Nefarian Serpine](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Nefarian_Serpine "Nefarian Serpine") succeeded in killing [Skulduggery Pleasant](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Skulduggery_Pleasant "Skulduggery Pleasant") and many other leaders fell with him. They paraded the bodies around demoralizing the Sanctuary and with the leaders dead. Mevolent's side acquired more followers and took the upper hand until Skulduggery came back from the dead. The battle shifted with Skulduggery's return. He later pursued [Quintin Strom](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Quintin_Strom "Quintin Strom") around the world around the time of The Corpse Attack in Denmark. In 1861 he was rumoured to be injured and had also gone into hiding. In [Skulduggery Pleasant: Midnight](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Skulduggery_Pleasant:_Midnight "Skulduggery Pleasant: Midnight") it is revealed he was killed by [Caisson](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Caisson "Caisson") who stabbed him multiple times and then used his power to drain away his life essence.

  
When Mevolent realises he is losing [the war](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/The_War "The War"), he orders a last strike against the leaders of his opposition. Many of these people, including [Skulduggery Pleasant](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Skulduggery_Pleasant "Skulduggery Pleasant") are killed and burned on pikes for all to see. This creates a sense of fear that Mevolent uses to begin turning the tide of [the war](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/The_War "The War").

### Mevolent's dimension

#### Pre Kingdom of the Wicked

At an unknown time he took control of the dimension, he later started purges against the [Warlocks](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Warlock "Warlock"), the [Witches](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Witch "Witch") and many more who refused to bow down to him.

#### Kingdom of the Wicked

![](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/skulduggery/images/4/4b/Three_Generals.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20170510171029)

Mevolent's three generals

Mevolent made his first actual appearance in the books in [his palace](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Mevolent%27s_Palace "Mevolent's Palace") in the [Alternate Dimension](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Alternate_Dimension "Alternate Dimension") visits by means of the [Dimensional Shunter](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Dimensional_Shunter "Dimensional Shunter") [Silas Nadir](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Silas_Nadir "Silas Nadir"). It was revealed by [Baron Vengeous](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Baron_Vengeous "Baron Vengeous") that he chose to die each day, upon himself and Valkyrie witnessing his death by several [Redhoods](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Redhoods "Redhoods"). It was claimed that he did this in order to "master death".

Mevolent attempts to find out Valkyrie's connections with [the Resistance](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/The_Resistance "The Resistance"), and doesn't believe her when she states that she doesn't know anything about them. He is later seen at his castle when Skulduggery, Valkyrie and Nefarian Serpine steal the Sceptre of Ancients.

When Valkyrie later returns as [Darquesse](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Darquesse "Darquesse"), they engage in battle during the fight with [the Resistance](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/The_Resistance "The Resistance"). Although he is ultimately defeated when Darquesse hits him with a horse, Mevolent exhibited extreme combat skills, defeating and decapitating Darquesse temporarily, almost killing her entirely. Though after she puts her head back onto her body she manages to knock him out by slamming a horse into him.

#### The Dying of the Light

" *You are not Darquesse* "

— Mevolent speaking to Valkyrie, *[The Dying of the Light](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/The_Dying_of_the_Light "The Dying of the Light")*

Mevolent made his appearance when Skulduggery and Valkyrie enter the alternate dimension whilst looking for an escaped Ravel. He then tries to take Darquesse on with [a new weapon](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Magic-Sucker "Magic-Sucker"), and nearly succeeds with the attempt. However, Darquesse fled. Later, Valkyrie goes up against Mevolent to trick him into thinking Darquesse had returned. He dropped the Magic-Sucker and was about to use the God Killer Sword to kill her before she was teleported away, as he realised she was not Darquesse.

Those not originally from the dimension fled, and he is not mentioned for the rest of the book.

#### Seasons of War

Mevolent is the main antagonist of the story, plotting to invade the main dimension to escape the Draugar who've infested the planet, turning it's people into more of themselves. He captures the main protagonists and has them hung up above while he does paperwork. He is eventually backstabbed by Lord Vile, who reveals himself to be the source of the Draugar, though he's eventually revived by followers from the main dimension. He manages to trick Valkyrie using his reflection to destroy the [Sceptre of the Ancients](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/The_Sceptre_of_the_Ancients "The Sceptre of the Ancients") and after his love Serafina Day is near fatally wounded, agrees to stay with her in the Eternity Gate so that they can spend the rest of eternity together in her dying moments.

## Magic

![The Mevolent character card from the Skulduggery Pleasant website.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/skulduggery/images/1/10/Mevolent-character-card.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/162?cb=20200129191407)

The Mevolent character card from the Skulduggery Pleasant website.

Mevolent was an Elemental. He was one of the few that turned evil. His mastery over the elements was unprecedented and absolutely incredible, and he had equal skill with all of them. He demonstrated his mastery over air by expanding the air in Darquesse's throat, causing it to burst and also the ability of flight.

Due to the fact that his underlings included Nefarian Serpine, Baron Vengeous and the immensely powerful Lord Vile, Mevolent is assumed to be a sorcerer of unimaginable strength. When she sees his magic, Darquesse claims that, apart from Argeddion and herself, he is most likely the most powerful sorcerer in existence. It is likely that if Mevolent was to discover his true name he would surpass both Darquesse and Argeddion, likely being capable of taking multiple Faceless One's head on.

During his battle with Darquesse, Mevolent shows incredible speed - moving at high speed to go to one place and back almost instantly - and strength - strong enough to lift a large slab of concrete, and rip off Darquesse's head - fighting techniques and sword mastery. As an [Elemental](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Elemental "Elemental"), Mevolent should possess no healing ability, yet was able to regenerate and continue fighting after having his bones crushed by Darquesse, showing that his decision to die once a day has some tangible benefits. Although he did not possess the power of knowing his [True Name](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/True_Name "True Name"), Mevolent managed to fight on-par with, and even briefly take an advantage over, Darquesse for the majority of the battle, decapitating her and causing other serious injury, Mevolent would have in fact won if he decided to destroy Darquesse's head instead of leaving it on the ground.

## Appearance

￼He is 8 feet tall, has blonde short hair, his skin has a yellow shade. His body described as lean, but in SoW he showed great strength and agility in combat even without his magic. He is almost always seen wearing black armour with spikes on his helmet and arms, with the helmet resembling a screaming face.  

## Relationships

· [Nefarian Serpine](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Nefarian_Serpine "Nefarian Serpine"): Nefarian was Mevolent's most trusted general. His right hand. Nefarian organized many of mevolent's attacks during the war. He is one of the people who really knows Mevolent, being able to predict his behavior and reasoning.

· Abyssinia: Mevolent in his childhood was trained by Abyssinia's father (The Unamed). Mevolent killed Abyssinia's father and family. She was the only survivor. When Mevolent started the war, she enlisted in the ranks of his army with the aim of killing him. She formed alliances like the Diablerie to get closer to Mevolent's trust. At some point she had sex with Mevolent and became pregnant in 1735 (Caisson).

· [Valkyrie Cain](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Valkyrie_Cain "Valkyrie Cain") / [Darquesse](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Darquesse "Darquesse"): She and mevolent have had several clashes in the books. Mevolent has as the priority to destroy Valkyrie because he sees her as the only opponent who could pose a real danger to him.

· [Damocles Creed](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Damocles_Creed "Damocles Creed"): Not much interaction has been seen between them, but Creed disagrees with the way Mevolent preached the word of the Faceless Ones. It seems that Damocles knows Mevolent well enough to know his weakness (as seen in SoW)

· [Serafina Dey](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Serafina_Dey "Serafina Dey"): Serafina is Mevolent's wife. They married in 1865. We don't know how they met.

In the other dimension, Mevolent is a widower because Serafina is death, and in the main dimension is Serafina the widow because Mevolent is deceased. In SoW, Mevolent is able to meet Serafina again after starting his invasion. At the end of SoW, Damocles Creed (Serafina's older brother) stabs her in the neck in a desperate attempt to stop Mevolent. He abandons his invasion and his chance to win to save her, tells her that he loves her and enters with her in The Eternity Gate.

## Personality

Mevolent is a rather violent and cruel individual as shown in *Kingdom of the Wicked*. Though his speech and demeanour may seem calm with no malicious intent, his actions and fighting skills as well as his methods of torture betray his almost normal appearance. He was obviously extremely charismatic and a brilliant leader, being able to persuade so many sorcerers to his cause, including the likes of Nefarian Serpine and Baron Vengeous.

## Trivia

- Derek said that *"He loved puppies. He thought they tasted really nice."* Although, it is entirely possible, and extremely likely, that he just made this up on the spot.
- Derek has stated that Mevolent's favourite music is Chopin's "Funeral March" closely followed by "Do You Want To Build A Snowman," [^2] however, this was certainly no more than a joke.
- In Mevolent's first appearance, he heavily implies that he has eaten newborns before, but only those that have had it coming, though this was most likely meant in morbid jest.
- The extreme power that Mevolent was supposed to have wielded has also led to speculation that he both knows, and has sealed, his True Name. However, this theory is disproven in *Kingdom of the Wicked*, wherein Darquesse cannot sense any trace of knowledge about Mevolent's True Name within him.
- There will be more information given on Mevolent's death in the main dimension and on his victory in the alternative one during the course of Phase 2.[^3]
	- Alternative Mevolent may come to the main dimension and try to conquer it as well.[^4]
- Mevolent is more powerful than [Lord Vile](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Lord_Vile "Lord Vile").[^5]
