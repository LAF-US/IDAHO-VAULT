## Edward L. Bonner

When Edward L. Bonner was born on 18 August 1834, in Orwell, Oswego, New York, United States, his father, Ephraim Bonner, was 20 and his mother, Jane C. Acker, was 19. He married Carolyn S Kenyon on 17 January 1863, in Nez Perce, Idaho, United States. They were the parents of at least 2 sons and 3 daughters. He lived in Deer Lodge, Montana, United States in 1880 and Hellgate, Missoula, Montana, United States in 1900. In 1864, at the age of 30, his occupation is listed as ferry owner on the kootenal river in Bonner, Idaho, United States. He died on 10 July 1902, in Missoula, Montana, United States, at the age of 67, and was buried in Missoula Cemetery, Missoula, Missoula, Montana, United States.

## Name Meaning

English: from Middle English *boner(e), bonour* ‘gentle, courteous’ (Old French *bonnaire*, shortened from *debonnaire*). This surname is also common in Ireland where it was adopted for **Ó Cnáimhsighe**, see Bonar 2.

Welsh: Anglicized form of Welsh *ap Ynyr* ‘son of *Ynyr* ’, a common medieval personal name derived from Latin *Honorius* with fused patronymic marker *(a)p*, normally voiced before a vowel.

German (also **Bönner**): from a short form of the ancient Germanic personal name *Bonhard*, formed with the element *bon* ‘request, petition’ (compare Latin *bonus*). This surname is also found in Sweden.
