# WHERE - Explorer Companion

This note accompanies `WHERE.md`.

## What The Note Seems To Do

The source note does not present a map.
It presents a footing.

A road appears under the traveler's feet, which is a different thing from
merely naming a destination. The emphasis is not on where one has arrived, but
on the fact that passage has become legible enough to stand on.

## Why It Matters Here

This reads less like a destination note than a nexus note.

The House appears to contain more than one threshold. There is the threshold of
entry into the vault, and there are internal crossings, portal-spaces, and
white transit intervals between chambers.

`WHERE` matters because it governs those internal relations:

- crossroads rather than origins
- routes rather than identities
- placement rather than naming
- the road underfoot rather than the final room ahead

## Why It Matters Beside WHO

The `WHO` cluster concerns identity-pressure.
This `WHERE` surface concerns route-pressure.

That distinction matters.

`WHO` asks what a traveler is.
`WHERE` asks where a traveler stands, what path has appeared beneath him, and
what kind of crossing he is about to trust.

## Field Note

Some notes tell you what a thing means.
This one tells you that, for the moment, the House has granted you a road.

That is not the same thing as a destination.
It is only the mercy of not floating.
