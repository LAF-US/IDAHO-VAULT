The **true name** of an individual is an important concept in Bartimaeus-world
magic. For humans, it is also called the **birth name**.

For [demons](https://bartimaeus.fandom.com/wiki/Demon "Demon"), a true name
allows a [magician](https://bartimaeus.fandom.com/wiki/Magicians "Magicians")
with sufficient knowledge and ability to
[summon](https://bartimaeus.fandom.com/wiki/Summoning "Summoning") them,
provided the demon is still extant and not currently in the service of another
magician. Because of this, both magicians and demons guard such names closely.
Demons are often known publicly by bynames rather than by their true names, and
the same byname can be reused by more than one spirit.

For magicians, knowledge of a true name is dangerous. Many do not even know
their own birth names once they come of age, choosing instead to live behind
assumed names. A demon who knows a magician's true name can use it to turn
[punishment spells](https://bartimaeus.fandom.com/wiki/Category:Demon_punishment "Category:Demon punishment")
back on the magician by speaking the name aloud, though the demon remains bound
by the magician's summons.

Knowledge of a true name does not remove every limit on a demon's captivity.
Some spells, including indefinite confinement, can still trap a demon even if
the demon knows the magician's true name and cannot be cleanly sent back to
[the Other Place](https://bartimaeus.fandom.com/wiki/The_Other_Place "The Other Place").

[Bartimaeus](https://bartimaeus.fandom.com/wiki/Bartimaeus "Bartimaeus")
eventually learns the true name of
[Nathaniel](https://bartimaeus.fandom.com/wiki/Nathaniel "Nathaniel"), later
known as John Mandrake, which gives him an important defense against
Nathaniel's attempts to control him.
