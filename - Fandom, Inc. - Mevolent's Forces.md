The forces of [Mevolent](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Mevolent "Mevolent") were the sorcerers who actively supported Mevolent and his goal of returning the [Faceless Ones](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Faceless_Ones "Faceless Ones") to Earth. Though these forces were largely disbanded following the murder of Mevolent by the three Irish Elders, they were granted immunity from prosecution for their past actions by [the Truce](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/The_Truce "The Truce"). [Ghastly Bespoke](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Ghastly_Bespoke "Ghastly Bespoke"), at [the Requiem Ball](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/The_Requiem_Ball "The Requiem Ball"), summarises the reason for their defeat as being that, as disciples of the Faceless, they were unable to endanger themselves as it would threaten their chances of being present during the return of their Gods.

The best known, and most feared, of Mevolent's forces were [the Three Generals](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/The_Three_Generals "The Three Generals"). This unit consisted of [Baron Vengeous](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Baron_Vengeous "Baron Vengeous"), [Lord Vile](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Lord_Vile "Lord Vile"), and [Nefarian Serpine](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Nefarian_Serpine "Nefarian Serpine"), and came into being a few years after the plot to kill [Skulduggery Pleasant](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Skulduggery_Pleasant "Skulduggery Pleasant"). Also fighting for Mevolent were [the Diablerie](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/The_Diablerie "The Diablerie") and a range of other assorted figures.

- Nefarian Serpine was a senior member of Mevolent's side from the outbreak of [the War](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/The_War "The War"). Within the first century of conflict he descended upon [the Necromancer Temple](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/The_Temple "The Temple"), wishing to learn of an ancient technique he believed could kill Skulduggery Pleasant. The cleric sent to treat with him, however, was [Auron Tenebrae](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Auron_Tenebrae "Auron Tenebrae"), a man who had seen Pleasant's natural aptitude for [Necromancy](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Necromancy "Necromancy"). Serpine's frequent discussion of his plan to kill Pleasant induced the former to involve a catch in the technique, meaning Pleasant would survive the procedure. When the war turned against Mevolent in 1700 Serpine masterminded a series of murders and the kidnapping of Skulduggery Pleasant's wife and child, carried out by [China Sorrows](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/China_Sorrows "China Sorrows"). Having lured the detective into combat, he killed Pleasant's wife and child in front of him before torturing Pleasant to death. However, due to Tenebrae's meddling, even when the flesh was burnt from Pleasant's bones, he lived on as the skeleton detective. Having extricated his remains from a pike outside Serpine's castle, Pleasant returned to the fray but would later relapse to Necromancy under the name of [Lord Vile](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Lord_Vile "Lord Vile"), channelling his magic through the [armour](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Lord_Vile%27s_armour "Lord Vile's armour") given to him by [Abyssinia](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Abyssinia "Abyssinia"). Later in the war, Serpine killed [Larrikin](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Larrikin "Larrikin") with the technique, the latter having jumped in front of his comrade, [Dexter Vex](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Dexter_Vex "Dexter Vex"). After Mevolent's death and Vengeous's arrest, Serpine fled to the American West and found sanctuary in a Necromancer Temple, incurring the rivalry between Pleasant and the leading cleric there, [Solomon Wreath](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Solomon_Wreath "Solomon Wreath"). he later became a signatory of the Truce.
- Baron Vengeous joined the War as leader of the Diablerie, making full and judicious use of his combat discipline. His fierce loyalty to Mevolent and the Faceless Ones inclined him to later join the Three Generals. Near the end of the War, he began work on [the Grotesquery](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/The_Grotesquery "The Grotesquery"), ordered by Mevolent to animate the remains of a Faceless One. At some point following Pleasant's return, Vengeous was arrested by the eponymous detective; he was not granted freedom by the truce, either due to his crimes or the hazards of his work on the Grotesquery.
- Lord Vile was the name taken by Skulduggery Pleasant after his defection to Mevolent; he entered the latter's forces anonymously after a brief training period at the temple of Auron Tenebrae. The armour he wore was given to him by Abyssinia, his lover during this period, and it was how he channelled his power. When he entered the War, Mevolent's forces were suffering a second decline at the hands of the Sanctuaries; the work of Vile and Abyssinia, however, quickly reversed the course of the War, annihilating multiple forces. Vile, initially a conspirator with Abyssinia, stabbed her during her plot to murder Mevolent; this act finally convinced him to return to the side of the Sanctuary forces. In deference to his actions in this period, he disowned his family crest.

The three generals contributed significantly to the war effort and spearheaded most of the major operations of the War. Both Serpine and Vengeous would later return to plot the reentry of the Faceless Ones.

The Diablerie were an elite group of combat-based Adepts and Signum Linguists. They were led, for the majority of the War, by [China Sorrows](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/China_Sorrows "China Sorrows"). Members included: [Baron Vengeous](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Baron_Vengeous "Baron Vengeous"), [Jaron Gallow](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Jaron_Gallow "Jaron Gallow"), [Abyssinia](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Abyssinia "Abyssinia"), [Murder Rose](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Murder_Rose "Murder Rose") and [Gruesome Krav](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Gruesome_Krav "Gruesome Krav").

- China Sorrows was an extremely capable Signum Linguist; raised in a devout family, she entered the War on Mevolent's side despite the urging of her brother, [Mr Bliss](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Mr._Bliss "Mr. Bliss"). As leader, she spearheaded the raid on the Marseiles sanctuary, the kidnapping of Skulduggery Pleasant's wife and child and the elimination of Abyssinia, working alongside [the Dead Men](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/The_Dead_Men "The Dead Men"). After the second of these missions, she and Mevolent personally inscribed a suicide sigil that would let her evade Pleasant's wrath. She defected before the War ended but was still hunted in the aftermath by her student, Jaron Gallow.
- Baron Vengeous briefly utilised his effectiveness in combat as a member of the Diablerie he moved on however, first to the Three Generals, then to his work on the Grotesquery.
- Jaron Gallow was a competent Signum Linguist trained by China Sorrows; after the former's defection, he became leader of the Diablerie and started a blaze in Naples intended to kill his former mentor. the fire killed 83 people and ruined China's favourite shawl. After the War, he was approached by [Batu](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Batu "Batu").
- Abyssinia was a member of the Diablerie in the middle of the War, having secured Pleasant's defection. she rose to favour with Mevolent and became his mistress, bearing him the child [Caisson](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Caisson "Caisson"). She eventually conspired to overthrow him, working with her lover Lord Vile; Vile's betrayal, however led to her crippling. She would be healed by her father, [the King of the Darklands](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/The_Unnamed "The Unnamed"), and fought against both Mevolent and the Sanctuary forces with her newfound power. Eventually, she was subdued by the Dead Men and Diablerie working in concert.
- Murder Rose was a well-known adept whose skill in combat was legendary; she would typically kill her enemies against orders, entering a frenzy. However, when tasked with the abduction of Skulduggery Pleasant's child, she successfully took the infant alive.
- Gruesome Krav was a notoriously effective sorcerer, he was seemingly an older member of the group, his age potentially on par with that of Baron Vengeous.

The Diablerie largely laid the way for Mevolent's forces, weakening the defences of the Sanctuaries, such as at Marseilles, before the great assault. There is little record of them coming into contact with the Dead Men, except for the termination of Abyssinia. None except Vengeous were detained following the signing of the Truce.

Various miscellaneous sorcerers were also part of Mevolent's forces, few fit into any named faction.

- [Dreylan Scarab](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Dreylan_Scarab "Dreylan Scarab") was an assassin in the employ of Mevolent, he had a passing knowledge of symbol magic and used a special weapon in order to assassinate his foes. He fled to America after [Thurid Guild](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Thurid_Guild "Thurid Guild") framed him for the murder of [Esryn Vanguard](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Esryn_Vanguard "Esryn Vanguard"), an act actually carried out by the Exigency Department. There, he met with the woman affectionately known by her son as Momma Sanguine, who would give birth to a son: [Billy-Ray Sanguine](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Billy-Ray_Sanguine "Billy-Ray Sanguine"). He would then be incarcerated for 200 years, and his release was not supplied in the terms of the Truce, this was arguably due to his status as a non-believer.
- [Arthur Dagan](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Arthur_Dagan "Arthur Dagan") was the grandson of Mevolent's master, the Unnamed, and was an especially pious disciple of the Faceless Ones. His company, [Dagan Logistics](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Dagan_Logistics "Dagan Logistics"), supplied Mevolent's forces using the Invisible Railway. The funds Dagan gathered from these activities were used to finance Mevolent's war effort.
- [Burgundy Dalrymple](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Burgundy_Dalrymple "Burgundy Dalrymple") was a master swordsman, his magic was weak and so he preferred to fight other sorcerers in a bound environment.
- [Serafina Dey](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Serafina_Dey "Serafina Dey") was the wife of Mevolent, and a powerful sorcerer, though never directly involved, her whims and desires often shaped the war effort. She, and the other members of the Unveiled faction bar [Damocles Creed](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Damocles_Creed "Damocles Creed"), developed a deep enmity with [Abyssinia](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Abyssinia "Abyssinia").
- [Eliza Scorn](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Eliza_Scorn "Eliza Scorn") was a devout follower of the Faceless Ones who participated in the War, her actions are unknown other than that she was persistently outdone by [China Sorrows](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/China_Sorrows "China Sorrows").
- [Doctor Nye](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Doctor_Nye "Doctor Nye") was a [Crenga who](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Crenga "Crenga") specialised in the Medical Arts and was a leading surgeon. It gained infamy for its cruel experiments on captured sorcerers, attempting to locate the human soul. At some point in the War, it was mutilated, having its nose cut off. It potentially also served as medical officer to Mevolent, similarly to its role in the Alternate Universe.

## History

Mevolent's forces were on the offensive for the first part of the War, they punished Grand Mages like [Trebuchet](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Trebuchet "Trebuchet") who allied themselves with [Meritorious](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Eachan_Meritorious "Eachan Meritorious") through daring operations such as the Marseilles Raid. Undertakings such as the raid on the Prussian Necromancer Temple were key in forcing the Necromancer Order out of The War effort. However, despite these early successes, their fortune began to run dry: [Quintin Strom](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Quintin_Strom "Quintin Strom") 's army had some success, as did [Mantis](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Mantis "Mantis") and Ghastly's mother. The Dead Men, especially, proved themselves to be a formidable annoyance. Mevolent seemingly began to contemplate peace: he listened to the teachings of Esryn Vanguard and thought about ending the War. However, Vanguard was killed by Thurid Guild of the Exigency Department, and attention returned to winning the War.

Serpine, one of Mevolent's foremost lieutenants, settled upon a plan to disrupt the workings of the Dead Men: by killing its de facto leader: [Skulduggery Pleasant](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Skulduggery_Pleasant "Skulduggery Pleasant"). He learnt the Necromancy technique of the red-right hand, under the guidance of [Auron Tenebrae](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Auron_Tenebrae "Auron Tenebrae"). [China Sorrows](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/China_Sorrows "China Sorrows") was able to capture Pleasant's family, delivering them to Serpine: the Detective followed the bait into Serpine's trap, leading to his "death". The same week, Serpine had masterminded the assassinations of various other members of the opposing alliance. Serpine almost succeeded in killing Pleasant but, due to Tenebrae's treachery, Skulduggery's soul, and magic, remained. Pleasant returned to the fray. However, Serpine's actions led to Pleasant developing a blind appetite for revenge: he took the name of Lord Vile and entered the Necromancer Order: forging a suit of armour that would hide his identity on the battlefield. Though proclaimed as the Death Bringer, Vile had little interest in enacting the passage, and joined Mevolent's side.

This officially marked the formation of the Three Generals, and was, seemingly, another turning point in the War. Mevolent had three extremely capable and powerful subordinates, Vile, especially, was feared for his ferocity on the field of battle: the Dead Men were one of the few units to encounter him and survive. Several key sorcerers, including Ghastly's mother, were easily killed by Vile. Larrikin, a member of the Dead Men was killed by Serpine, but again his scheme to disrupt the Dead Men failed: many of them rallied around [Larrikin's](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Larrikin_Fetter "Larrikin Fetter") death. [Abyssinia](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Abyssinia "Abyssinia"), a sorcerer who had earned the favour of [Mevolent](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Mevolent "Mevolent"), and fathered him an illegitimate son, plotted with Vile to kill [Mevolent](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Mevolent "Mevolent"): Vile betrayed her in the midst of this scheme, and she fled. The Diablerie and the Dead Men formed a truce in order to hunt Abyssinia down. China Sorrows was captured but persuaded Abyssinia to hand herself in for the safety of [Caisson](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Caisson "Caisson"), the son she had birthed with Mevolent. Caisson would be adopted by China, and fall in love with one of [Serafina](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Serafina "Serafina") 's handmaids: Solace, a daughter of China.

However, Pleasant relinquished his blind vengeance, and decided to concentrate his efforts on killing Serpine, he returned to the Dead Men and hid Vile's armour.

In 1861 [Mevolent](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Mevolent "Mevolent") died on the battle field fighting the Meritorious, Tome, and Crow. Vengeous was arrested by Pleasant soon after. Serpine found sanctuary in an American Necromancer Temple, allowing him to weather out the rest of the War: however his newfound sanctuary allowed the Dead Men to return to action.

Serpine came out of hiding as the most senior remaining figure in the war effort but decided to consent to a truce: giving him time to secure more power. By the end of the War he was well-respected by several figures, including Eliza Scorn.
