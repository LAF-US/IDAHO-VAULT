On March 4, 2026, Capitol Correspondents Association president Kevin Richert and Idaho Press Club president Melissa Davlin sent a formal open-meetings complaint letter to JFAC co-chairs Scott Grow and Josh Tanner, triggering a statutory response deadline of approximately March 18, 2026.

## The Letter

- **Date:** March 4, 2026
- **Signatories:** Melissa Davlin (Idaho Press Club president), Kevin Richert (CCA president)
- **Addressees:** Scott Grow and Josh Tanner (JFAC co-chairs)
- **CC:** Keith Bybee (LSO)
- **Statutory citations:** Idaho Code §74-201, Idaho Code §74-207, Senate Rule 20, House Rule 26

## Statutory Timeline

Under Idaho Code §74-208:
- 14-day cure period begins from written notice
- Statutory response deadline: ~**March 18, 2026**
- Silence = denial
- Civil penalties: $250 (unknowing), $1,500 (knowing), $2,500 (repeat knowing within 12 months)
- Penalties are personal to each member
- AG enforcement only — no private right of action

## Editorial Framing

The letter was coordinated by Logan Finney (Idaho Reports), who drafted the CCA briefing document and member email. Idaho Reports / IPTV cannot editorially own the story due to its status as a state agency under SBOE oversight. Primary story carrier: Clark Corbin (Idaho Capital Sun).

## CCA Membership Context

Approximately a dozen CCA members were briefed ahead of the letter. The Capitol Correspondents Association is the press credentialing body for the Idaho Legislature. Kevin Richert is CCA president; Melissa Davlin is not a CCA officer but signed as IPC president.
