The **Five Great Charters** refers to the five beacons of [the Charter](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Charter "The Charter") that infuse the [Old Kingdom](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Old_Kingdom "Old Kingdom") with [Charter Magic](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Charter_magic "Charter magic") and constrain the presence of its opposite, [Free Magic](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Free_Magic "Free Magic"). The Five Great Charters were originally all bloodlines, but the [Wallmakers](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Wallmaker "Wallmaker"), who accounted for two of the five, put their power into inanimate objects. Thus, the Five Great Charters are the [Royal bloodline](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Royal_line "Royal line"), the [Abhorsen](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Abhorsen "Abhorsen") bloodline, the [Clayr](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Clayr "Clayr") bloodline, the [Charter Stones](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Charter_Stones "Charter Stones"), and finally, [the Wall](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Wall "The Wall").

As [Sabriel](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Sabriel "Sabriel") was raised in [Ancelstierre](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Ancelstierre "Ancelstierre"), she was unaware of the Charters and their significance, and once she entered the Old Kingdom, there was a binding spell placed on speaking of them. At the fishing port of [Nestowe](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Nestowe "Nestowe"), a young girl named Aline sings Sabriel a rhyme that explains the Great Charters, though vaguely.

*Five Great Charters knit the land*

*Together linked, hand in hand*

*One in the people who wear the crown* \[[Royals](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Royal_line "Royal line")\]

*Two in the folk who keep the Dead down* \[[Abhorsens](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Abhorsen "Abhorsen")\]

*Three and Five became stone and mortar* \[[Charter Stones](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Great_Charter_Stones "Great Charter Stones") and [the Wall](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Wall "The Wall"), respectively\]

*Four sees all in frozen water* \[referring to the [Sight](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Sight "Sight") of the [Clayr](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Clayr "The Clayr")\]*.*[^1]

Each of the three bloodlines has special attributes or abilities.

1. The Royal Line is preternatural in their ability to gain and keep loyal followers. Their voices also hold great power, to the extent the aged and weak "absentee King" [Orrikan](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Orrikan "Orrikan") was able to compel [Mogget](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Mogget "Mogget") to answer his question in *[Clariel](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Clariel:_The_Lost_Abhorsen "Clariel: The Lost Abhorsen")*. This is also the bloodline infamous for [berserks](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Fury "Fury").
2. The Abhorsen line has a "death-sense," the ability to feel when an animal or human has died. They almost always have dark, usually black, hair, and dark eyes. Their skin, if not already pale, will become extremely so if they walk in [Death](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death"). One of their number will of course become *the* Abhorsen, tasked with banishing [Dead](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Dead "Dead") and [Free Magic creatures](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Free_Magic_Creatures "Free Magic Creatures"), while another will become the [Abhorsen-in-Waiting](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Abhorsen-in-Waiting "Abhorsen-in-Waiting").
3. The Clayr nearly all have nut-brown skin and blond, almost white, hair. They are also overwhelmingly female. The Clayr possess the Sight, the ability to see into the future.

[Orannis](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Orannis "Orannis") himself reveals more about the Five Great Charters when speaking through [Nick](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Nicholas_Sayre "Nicholas Sayre"):

"*... I'll sing you a song of the long ago --*

*Seven shine the shiners, oh!*

*What did the Seven do way back when?*

*Why, they wove the Charter then!*

*Five for the warp, from beginning to end.*

*Two* [^2] *for the woof, to make and mend..."* [^3]

### Trivia/Theories

- One of the Great Charters has changed from "stone and mortar" back into a bloodline as well, in [Prince Sameth](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Sameth "Sameth"). He is the only person of the great Charter bloodline of the [Wallmakers](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Wallmaker "Wallmaker") currently known, or who has existed for hundreds of years. His status as a Wallmaker is apparently purely spontaneous and should be impossible, with no direct family history. It may have occurred simply because a Wallmaker would be needed against Orannis.
- Many fans believe [Mosrael](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Mosrael "Mosrael") gave itself to the Clayr bloodline (as their Sight is said to "awaken") and [Dyrim](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Dyrim "Dyrim") gave itself to the Royal bloodline (as diplomacy and power of personality is inherent in them). However, neither of these are confirmed. [Saraneth](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Saraneth "Saraneth") was also thought to have been the source of the Abhorsens, until *[Lirael](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Lirael "Lirael")* and " [Nicholas Sayre and the Creature in the Case](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Creature_in_the_Case "The Creature in the Case") " revealed it to be [Astarael](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Astarael "Astarael"). This is so far the only connection known for certain.
- There is a theory that the power inherent in each **Great Bloodine** is of the same strength regardless of the number of the Blood. For example, the power of the numerous Clayr is distributed among them all, making each individual Clayr relatively weak in their ability, i.e. the Sight. In contrast, [Kerrigor](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Kerrigor "Kerrigor") was the only member of the Royal line left besides [Touchstone](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Touchstone "Touchstone") (and the latter was in suspended animation), which would help explain how extraordinarily successful he was at gaining followers.
- Many assume that [Kibeth](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Kibeth "Kibeth") and Astarael were not part of the Great Charters, as remnants of those two exist and five of the Nine Great Shiners who had committed themselves to become the Charter were supposedly completely consumed by doing so. However, Astarael seems to have given the entirety of herself to the Charter as the originator of the Abhorsen bloodline, and an "echo" of her remains. It's possible the other Seven can also appear as remnants and just haven't yet.
- The Shiners, particularly Kibeth, are certainly not above punning. While 'woof' is certainly a valid alternative weaving term to 'weft', it's quite suspicious that Kibeth is one of the primary candidates for the remaining two.
- Unifying the above theories, [Ranna](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Ranna "Ranna") gave itself to the [Wallakers](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Wallmaker "Wallmaker") and more specifically the [Great Charter Stones](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Great_Charter_Stones "Great Charter Stones"). This would help explain how [Touchstone](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Touchstone "Touchstone") was able to repair them using his blood. [Mosrael](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Mosrael "Mosrael") gave itself to the [Clayr](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Clayr "The Clayr"). [Kibeth](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Kibeth "Kibeth") remained outside the **Great Charters** as a remnant. [Dyrim](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Dyrim "Dyrim") gave itself to the [Royal Family](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Royal_line "Royal line"). [Belgaer](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Belgaer "Belgaer") gave itself to the [Wallmakers](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Wallmaker "Wallmaker"), and by extension the [Charter Stones](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Great_Charter_Stones "Great Charter Stones") and the [Wall](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Wall "The Wall"). However, Belgaer returned to a bloodline with [Sameth](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Sameth "Sameth"). [Saraneth](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Saraneth "Saraneth") would be implied to also be a remnant that did not give itself to the [Charter](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Charter "The Charter"). [Astarael](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Astarael "Astarael") gave itself to the [Abhorsens](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Abhorsen "Abhorsen"). Alternatively, as a remnant of Astarael is met underneath [Abhorsen's House](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Abhorsen%27s_House "Abhorsen's House"), then it would follow that Saraneth gave itself to the Abhorsen and Astarael remained. Further, the rhyme told to [Sabriel](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Sabriel "Sabriel") indicates that it was Ranna that was given to the Royal Line and Dyrim that was unaccounted for and thus would have given itself to the Great Charter Stones. Either of these are possible based upon the information available.
