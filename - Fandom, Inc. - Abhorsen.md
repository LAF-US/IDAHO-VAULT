**The Abhorsen** are those of the [Five Great Charters](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Five_Great_Charters "Five Great Charters") hereditary bloodline whose charge is maintaining the border between [Life](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life") and [Death](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death"). However, inheritance is not always direct: the next Abhorsen could be a niece, cousin, sibling, or other relative of the current Abhorsen, rather than their child.

They are the only [Charter](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Charter "The Charter") bloodline whose contributor is known: [Astarael](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Astarael "Astarael"). The bindings on her well can only be removed with the breath "of her children," those with Abhorsen blood. Additionally, the [Hrule](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Hrule "Hrule") in " [Nicholas Sayre and the Creature in the Case](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Creature_in_the_Case "The Creature in the Case") *"* refers to Abhorsens as "Astarael's get" ("get" being an archaic term for "offspring").

The Abhorsen combines [Charter Magic](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Charter_magic "Charter magic") and [Free Magic](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Free_Magic "Free Magic") in their [bells](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Bells "Bells") to compel and control the [Dead](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Dead "Dead"), righting the wrongs created by [necromancers](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Necromancer "Necromancer") or others who pervert the nature of Life and Death. The arrival of a new Abhorsen or [Abhorsen-in-Waiting](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Abhorsen-in-Waiting "Abhorsen-in-Waiting") is signalled by the arrival of the bells, necromantic, Free-Magic instruments infused with Charter magic. As Abhorsens are servants of [the Charter](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Charter "The Charter"), they bear uncorrupted baptismal [Charter Marks](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Charter_Marks "Charter Marks") on their foreheads, unlike Free Magic necromancers who have long been severed from the Charter.

Up until the binding of [Orannis](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Orannis "Orannis"), every Abhorsen had been served by [Yrael](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Yrael "Yrael") / [Mogget](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Mogget "Mogget").

The Abhorsen stronghold, called [Abhorsen's House](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Abhorsen%27s_House "Abhorsen's House"), is located on an island in the [Ratterlin](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/River_Ratterlin "River Ratterlin") near the [Long Cliffs](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Long_Cliffs "Long Cliffs"). The House lies in close proximity to an enormous waterfall; the associated rapids complement the magical wards of the House in keeping the Dead and other dangerous entities from accessing it. Only the Abhorsen can break into the barrier separating Life and Death at Abhorsen's House, but never do so, as thousands of Dead enemies lie in wait there, hoping for just such an occurrence,

The Abhorsen share certain physical characteristics. They usually inherit pale skin, which then becomes paler when leeched of its colour from the waters of death. The Abhorsen also have dark, usually black, hair.

## Symbol

Silver key(s) on a dark blue field.

## Trivia/Theories

- In [Goldenhand](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Goldenhand_\(Book\) "Goldenhand (Book)") it is mentioned that the Abhorsen's lineage used to be more plentiful (Vancelle says in Chapter 20, on page 209: "... all from the days when the Abhorsens were more populous, and a score or more might visit at the same time.)
- Nearly all Abhorsens have names ending in -iel or -ael. This may be a tribute to, or from faded memories of, the seventh Bright Shiner, Astar **ael**, who began the Abhorsen bloodline. It also may be the equivalent of a "family name," which Old Kingdom denizens don't have.
- After the death of Sabriel's father, she was the only member of the Abhorsen line left, as Lirael had not yet been born. Even [Touchstone](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Touchstone "Touchstone") had [Kerrigor](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Kerrigor "Kerrigor") sharing his blood. Hence, when Sabriel died and there was no one else to be Abhorsen, the spirits of past Abhorsens "broke the rules" and brought her back to Life until the bloodline could continue.
- The origin of the name "Abhorsen" in the book's world is unknown, but Nix may have chosen the name referencing "Abhorson," the executioner in Shakespeare's *Measure for Measure*. In *[Terciel and Elinor](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Terciel_and_Elinor_\(Book\) "Terciel and Elinor (Book)")*, it is said that the title Abhorsen is thought to be the name of the first of their line.
