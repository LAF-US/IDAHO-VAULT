In the Bartimaeus universe, it is regularly reported that the earthly realm
consists of seven or more separate but overlapping **planes** on which varying
levels of magical activity can be perceived.

According to Bartimaeus, the more powerful a spirit is, the more planes it can
typically view. Most humans can see only the first plane, and sometimes the
second under certain circumstances, while those with unusual magical
[resilience](https://bartimaeus.fandom.com/wiki/Resilience "Resilience") may
be able to perceive planes 1-7. Magicians often wear lenses that let them see
the second and third planes, and a little of the fourth.

Lower-level spirits, such as imps and
[foliots](https://bartimaeus.fandom.com/wiki/Foliots "Foliots"), can typically
see something like planes 1-5. That means anything with the same appearance on
those planes, but not on the higher ones, can hide its true shape from them.

Cats can see two planes.

Higher classes of being, such as djinn, afrits, and marids, can see planes 1-7,
with some claiming access to even more. Bartimaeus treats such claims with
skepticism, suggesting that only planes 1-7 are generally considered important.

The existence of additional planes is briefly hinted at. Bartimaeus believed
that the
[Seven-League Boots](https://bartimaeus.fandom.com/wiki/Seven-League_Boots "Seven-League Boots"),
a kind of [talisman](https://bartimaeus.fandom.com/wiki/Talismans "Talismans"),
were activated and operated through the imprisonment of one or more spirits
with access to a theoretical **eighth plane**. That higher plane would explain
how the wearer of the boots could travel extremely long distances in very short
periods of time.

Bartimaeus notes that he knows little about such matters, and he does not say
much more about them.
