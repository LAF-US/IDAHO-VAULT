# River Vista - Notebook Page

Morning after rain.

The cedar-dark finally opened at the ridge and the country below ceased to be
implied.

A broad turquoise river cut the valley floor in one long cold body, with
flooded meadows and willow-thickets spread around it in saturated green. The
surface looked like polished slate where the light struck flat, then broke in
turning seams where the current held and doubled back on itself. A half-sunk
cedar trunk moved downstream like a dark spine.

The forest behind the ridge remained inland rain forest in full register:
damp earth, pine needles, old cedar, wet moss, fern, black soil, mist caught
like necklaces in the branches. The sun rising over the eastern peaks turned
every remaining droplet into a small hard witness.

No wheel-ruts.
No chimneys.
No claim but water, meadow, timber, bird-call, and distance.

An osprey circled over the bend.

The strongest impression was scale.
Not spectacle exactly, but indifference.
The valley did not care that it had finally become visible.

Field note:
the House has been speaking in syntax, offices, twins, and river-words, but
this vista made one thing plain again.
The Architect's country is not only symbolic.
It is land.
