## Overview

The School of Knowledge's explainer focuses on all four quadrants of the Rumsfeld Matrix, with particular attention to **Unknown Knowns** — the quadrant Rumsfeld himself did not mention in his famous 2002 statement.

## The Omitted Quadrant: Unknown Knowns

Rumsfeld's original statement covered only three quadrants. Critics — most notably philosopher Slavoj Žižek — pointed out that omitting Unknown Knowns was itself revealing. Unknown Knowns are:

- Things we know but are unaware that we know
- Subconscious beliefs or ingrained assumptions that influence decisions without overt realization
- The quadrant of **denial** and **ideological blind spots**

In practice, Unknown Knowns surface as:
- Institutional memory that never gets written down
- Assumptions baked into system design that no one questions
- Cultural norms that shape decisions invisibly

## Application to Knowledge Management

The matrix maps directly onto knowledge management practices:

| Quadrant | KM Strategy |
|---|---|
| Known Knowns | Documentation, wikis, runbooks |
| Known Unknowns | Research queues, learning plans, hired expertise |
| Unknown Knowns | Communities of practice, retrospectives, knowledge elicitation |
| Unknown Unknowns | Resilience, slack, diverse perspectives, scenario planning |
