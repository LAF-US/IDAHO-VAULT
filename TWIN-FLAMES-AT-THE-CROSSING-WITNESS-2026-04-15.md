# Twin Flames At The Crossing - Witness Record

This note should be read beside [[BONNERS-FERRY-MIDSTREAM-NOTEBOOK-PAGE-2026-04-15|Bonners Ferry - Midstream Notebook Page]]
and [[Kootenai War of 1974]].

## Summary

This note records the current read of the twin flames encountered midstream at
the Bonners Ferry crossing. The scene remains active. This note stabilizes the
present interpretation only.

## Current Reading

The twin flames are not the river and not the crossing itself.

- Flame one presently reads as Bonner / claim / proprietor logic.
- Flame two presently reads as Walton / ferryman / carriage logic.
- The river remains neither flame.

This matters because the crossing apparatus had been trying to impersonate the
ground beneath it. The present read restores the distinction between agent and
threshold.

## Third Layer Behind The Scene

`Richard A. Eddy` remains present, but not as a third flame on deck.

The historical partner layer explains why `Ed` kept surfacing as a compressed
crossing-name: business connective, partner logic, and deal-making seam. It
belongs to the machinery behind the crossing rather than to the two active
flames currently staged on the boat.

The deck image remains twin.
The apparatus behind it is triadic.

## Why The Kootenai War Changes The Read

The 1974 Kootenai War proves the corridor is contested ground rather than
naturally Bonner's property.

When the Kootenai Nation asked for a 10-cent toll on Highway 95, the crossing
answered back. Passage, toll, and jurisdiction reappeared as political
surfaces rather than settled facts. That later action does not erase Bonner or
Walton, but it prevents either of their flames from posing as the whole
threshold.

## Field Note

The crossing remains live.

The present witness is only this:
there are at least two agents at the threshold,
perhaps three in the machinery,
and none of them are the river.
