## Six Investigative Threads

1. **Deliberation without disclosure** — Working groups are formalized deliberation under Idaho Code §74-202 regardless of quorum. Members are answering the wrong legal question.
2. **The rules vacuum** — No formally adopted rules govern JFAC. If old rules remain (the stronger parliamentary position), they still don't authorize working groups.
3. **Power concentration** — Two co-chairs from the same Eagle district, controlled by a Majority Leader who has never served on JFAC. The western Ada County power cluster.
4. **The deliberative shell game** — Pre-FAC + working groups + public hearing means the public sees only the third step in a sequence where decisions are already substantially made.
5. **Asymmetric information among members** — Members not on specific working groups don't know what was decided there. Melissa Wintrow: "there's a big knowledge gap for legislators." [TRANS]
6. **The no-quorum defense is the wrong argument** — Every member relies on quorum; none addressed Idaho Code §74-202. The Idaho Attorney General Open Meetings Manual sub-quorum evasion doctrine covers the structural pattern.
