The **Latin-1 Supplement** (also called **C1 Controls and Latin-1 Supplement**) is the second [Unicode block](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicode_block "Unicode block") in the [Unicode](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicode "Unicode") standard. It encodes the upper range of [ISO 8859-1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISO_8859-1 "ISO 8859-1"): 80 (U+0080) – FF (U+00FF). [C1 Controls](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C0_and_C1_control_codes "C0 and C1 control codes") (0080–009F) are not graphic. This block ranges from U+0080 to U+00FF, contains 128 characters and includes the [C1 controls](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C1_controls "C1 controls"), [Latin-1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin-1 "Latin-1") [punctuation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Punctuation "Punctuation") and [symbols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symbols "Symbols"), 30 pairs of [majuscule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Majuscule "Majuscule") and [minuscule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lower_case "Lower case") [accented](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Accented "Accented") [Latin characters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin_characters "Latin characters") and 2 mathematical operators.

The C1 Controls and Latin-1 Supplement block has been included in its present form, with the same character repertoire since version 1.0 of the [Unicode Standard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicode_Standard "Unicode Standard").[^6] Its block name in Unicode 1.0 was simply **Latin1**.[^7]

## Subheadings

The C1 Controls and Latin-1 Supplement block has four subheadings within its character collection: C1 controls, Latin-1 Punctuation and Symbols, Letters, and Mathematical operator(s).[^8]

### C1 controls

The C1 controls subheading contains 32 supplementary control codes inherited from [ISO/IEC 8859-1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISO/IEC_8859-1 "ISO/IEC 8859-1") and many other 8-bit character standards. The alias names for the C0 and C1 control codes are taken from [ISO/IEC 6429:1992](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=ISO/IEC_6429:1992&action=edit&redlink=1 "ISO/IEC 6429:1992 (page does not exist)").[^8]

### Latin-1 punctuation and symbols

The Latin-1 Punctuation and Symbols subheading contains 32 characters of common international punctuation characters, such as the inverted question and exclamation marks, a middle dot, and symbols such as currency signs, spacing diacritic marks, vulgar fractions, and superscript numbers.[^8]

### Letters

The Letters subheading contains 30 pairs of majuscule and minuscule accented or novel Latin characters for western European languages, and two extra minuscule characters ([ß](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%9F "ß") and [ÿ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C5%B8 "Ÿ")) not commonly used as the first letter of words.[^8]

### Mathematical operator

The Mathematical operator subheading is used for the multiplication and division signs.[^8]

## Number of symbols, letters and control codes

The table below shows the number of letters, symbols and control codes in each of the subheadings in the C1 Controls and Latin-1 Supplement block.

| Type of subheading | Number of symbols | Range of characters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **C1 controls** | 32 control codes | U+0080 to U+009F |
| **Latin-1 punctuation and symbols** | 32 punctuation and symbols | U+00A0 to U+00BF |
| **Letters** | 30 pairs of [majuscule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Letter_case "Letter case") and [minuscule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Letter_case "Letter case") [accented](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Accented "Accented") Latin characters, and two extra minuscule characters | U+00C0 to U+00D6, U+00D8 to U+00F6 and U+00F8 to U+00FF |
| **Mathematical operators** | The U+00D7 × MULTIPLICATION SIGN and U+00F7 ÷ DIVISION SIGN symbols. | U+00D7 and U+00F7 |

## Emoji

The Latin-1 Supplement block contains two [emoji](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emoji "Emoji"): U+00A9 and U+00AE.[^9] [^10]

The block has four [standardized variants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Variant_form_\(Unicode\) "Variant form (Unicode)") defined to specify emoji-style (U+FE0F VS16) or text presentation (U+FE0E VS15) for the two emoji, both of which default to a text presentation.[^11]

| --- | --- | --- |
| base code point | © | ® |
| base+VS15 (text) | ©︎ | ®︎ |
| base+VS16 (emoji) | ©️ | ®️ |
