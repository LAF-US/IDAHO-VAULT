*Hawkins Companies v. Moyle* (Docket 51788-2024) challenged JFAC's use of intent language to block the ITD State Street Building Sale; the Idaho Supreme Court dismissed for lack of standing in August 2024, leaving the constitutional question open.

## Background

Hawkins Companies, Pacific Companies, and FJM filed suit April 2024 after JFAC intent language blocked their $51.7M purchase of the ITD State Street campus. Argued the intent language was a "shadow amendment of general law" violating the single-subject rule and the separation of powers.

## Dismissal

Dismissed August 2024. Justice Zahn wrote the opinion: ITD and the Department of Administration would likely have had standing but did not sue. The constitutional question — whether JFAC can use intent language to override a statutory grant of executive-branch authority — was expressly left open.

## Intervenors

Mike Moyle intervened as House Majority Leader with private counsel (Hilty, Bower, Haws & Seable). AG Labrador argued moot. Governor Little's private attorney conceded budgets may be unconstitutional.

## Significance

Establishes that who sues matters as much as what happened. The constitutional question about JFAC intent language overriding statute remains unresolved and will arise again when CLR language in operative bill sections is challenged.
