**Necromancy** is the [Adept](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Adept "Adept") discipline that allows its practitioners to harness the energy of the dead, manipulating it so that they can control darkness and shadows.

## Power

[Necromancers](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Necromancer "Necromancer") harness the living energy of the dead to control shadows and darkness. Some abilities that can be achieved are making them into solid projectiles, shadow blasts, whips and tendrils and many other things. Other techniques include the one that [Nefarian Serpine](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Nefarian_Serpine "Nefarian Serpine") uses, which is placing power in his hand, rendering it skinless and bloody, and using it to make people suffer agonizing torture and then death whenever he points at them. The purple vapor that Serpine can produce is Necromancy. They can use their power when there is no death present, but it is when there is nearby death that their power is at its strongest. [Valkyrie](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Valkyrie_Cain "Valkyrie Cain") has shown extremely powerful Necromancy in [Mortal Coil](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Mortal_Coil "Mortal Coil"), due to the fact that when she was dead in [Nye](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Nye "Nye") 's workplace, she used Necromancy and was perhaps the most powerful depiction of it, because she harnessed her own death energy. This means dead people are the most powerful Necromancers. They can also shadow-walk, which is a lesser form of [teleportation](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Teleportation "Teleportation"). Through manipulation of shadows, powerful necromancers are also able to fly by using shadows to support them. This has been shown by [Lord Vile](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Lord_Vile "Lord Vile") in *[Death Bringer](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Bringer "Death Bringer")*. Powerful Necromancers such as [Melancholia St Clair](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Melancholia_St_Clair "Melancholia St Clair") and [Lord Vile](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Lord_Vile "Lord Vile") have shown the ability to create a bubble of death, in which all life force within the aptly named death bubble are sucked towards the user as it contracts, making them stronger. This is similar to the [Warlock](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Warlock "Warlock") technique of sucking out souls.

### Objects/Weapons

![Fan Art of 's Necromancy ring](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Valkyrie%27s_Necromancer_Ring.png)

Fan Art of Valkyrie Cain 's Necromancy ring

It is frequently stated that it is the Necromancer way to place their power into an object. However, while the power allows anyone to use it, the darkness can only yield to people who have its consent to use it. [Baron Vengeous](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Baron_Vengeous "Baron Vengeous") had to wear special garments when wearing Vile's armour because the power was raw enough to "sear his flesh and boil his blood." When Valkyrie uses Wreath's cane, tendrils of shadows coiled around her hand and it said that "if Wreath had not given it voluntarily, those shadows would tighten and turn her bones to dust." The objects are also able to be destroyed but when they are, the power is not lost as it flows back into the person whose power was in the object. An exception to this is Melancholia, whose power is so strong she does not need an object to store it in.

## Trivia

It is stated by [Dr.Nye](https://skulduggery.fandom.com/wiki/Nye "Nye"), that Necromancy magic can be dangerous and unstable when it is used by something that is already dead.
