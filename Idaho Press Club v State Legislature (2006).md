In *Idaho Press Club v. State Legislature*, 142 Idaho 640 (2006), the Idaho Supreme Court held that Art. III §12 of the Idaho Constitution does not require legislative committees to be open to the public, but left the statutory open-meetings obligation under §67-2346 (now Idaho Code §74-207) untouched.

## Holding

The constitutional route to forcing open committee meetings is foreclosed. The majority held that "each house" and "committee of the whole" in Art. III §12 does not extend to legislative committees (expressio unius). The statutory route — Idaho Code §74-207 — was not addressed and remains available.

## Parties and Counsel

- Idaho Press Club represented by Givens Pursley (Allen Derr; Debora Kristensen argued)
- Legislature defended by AG Lawrence Wasden (James Carlson argued)
- IPC lost and paid costs

## Jim Jones Dissent

Jim Jones dissented. Footnote 13: "I find the Legislature's argument that it may under the Constitution close committee meetings a bit disingenuous, in light of I.C. § 67-2346 [now §74-207]." This is the statutory tension argument that undergirds the 2026 CCA open-meetings challenge. Footnote 9 challenges the majority's use of expressio unius in a constitutional context, citing *Eberle v. Nielson* (1957).

## Significance for 2026 Investigation

The 2006 loss explains why IDOG / Betsy Russell frames transparency arguments in terms of "the spirit of the law" rather than explicit statutory accusation — IPC paid costs. Jones's dissent footnote 13 is the statutory route still open; Jones wrote it, is still active, and is still writing about JFAC overreach as of 2026.
