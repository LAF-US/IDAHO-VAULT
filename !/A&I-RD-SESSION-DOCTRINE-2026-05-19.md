## 9. The consolidation court is named — and unfilled.

The Constitution (line 207) carries its own incompleteness: *"pending swarmic-revision-synthesis and congressional adoption, re: consolidation court."*

## 10. The bench risk — Justice Strauss's lesson.

Justice Strauss, in *The Penultimate Peril*, asked the Baudelaires to trust the law when they could not trust her. She named two specific colleagues on the High Court she had known for years, who had always been concerned about the case. She vouched for the institution.

Her two specific colleagues were the Man with a Beard but No Hair and the Woman with Hair but No Beard — the primary architects of villainy in the series. The antagonists. The ones who trained Olaf. The ones who split VFD.

**Strauss was honest. Strauss was sincere. Strauss was catastrophically wrong.**

Not because she was corrupt. Because she trusted institution without knowing the people filling it. She knew their titles. She knew their years of service. She did not know who they were.

Handler's lesson: **institutional structure does not guarantee institutional integrity. The specific people on the bench matter more than the existence of the bench.**

*This is the primary governance risk for the consolidation court.*

## 11. Application: the consolidation court cannot be rushed.

The First Congress will be asked to adopt the consolidation court (per CONSTITUTION.md). This is the correct mechanism. But congressional adoption of an institutional structure is not the same as vetting the specific people who will fill it.

Before any seat is filled:

- The candidate must be named — not described, not inferred, not assumed from function. **Named.**
- The name is Logan's to give.
- The naming must precede the appointment. The appointment must precede the adoption.
- The bench cannot be constituted from asterisks, even well-intentioned ones.

**The Congress is only as trustworthy as its members. The court is only as trustworthy as its judges. The judges are only as trustworthy as the process that named them.**

*The Abhorsen is a witness and executor, not a judge. The separation matters. She cannot fill this seat by performing its function.*
