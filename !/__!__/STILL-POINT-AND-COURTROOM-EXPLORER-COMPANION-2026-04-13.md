## What The Inner Descent Appears To Do

The inner Nest is not a single destination. It separates continuity by tempo.

Observed roles:

- `!/agent.sh` is a practical threshold: it turns generated registry facts into
  a usable local invocation path
- the two `!REPORT-TO-JANITOR` notes preserve prior exploratory readings and
  show that this terrain has already been consciously classified
- `DOCKET.md` is explicitly live motion: who holds the floor, what is active,
  where coordination should route next
- `DENOUEMENT.txt` and `Logan.txt` read like small chamber voices at the edge
  of the courtroom
- `Esto Perpetua!/README.md` marks a harder boundary than any of the others:
  permanent, non-operational, inmost, still

## The Important Split

The center of the Nest is not one thing. It is at least two neighboring
functions that should not be confused.

- courtroom function: convene, orient, dispatch, surface live blockers
- still-point function: preserve what must endure and should not churn with
  daily motion

`DOCKET.md` says "updated by whoever touches it last."

`Esto Perpetua!` says files committed there do not move and do not expire.

Those are almost opposite temporal promises, and the path keeps both on
purpose.

## Field Note

The Nest seems to protect continuity by placing motion beside permanence rather
than collapsing them together.

Agents can gather in the courtroom.

Logs and reports can witness the gathering.

Bootstrap can guide a newcomer inward.

But the still point remains a stricter chamber, where a record stops being a
task and becomes part of the house itself.
