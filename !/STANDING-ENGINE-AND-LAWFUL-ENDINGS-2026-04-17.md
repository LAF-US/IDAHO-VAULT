# Standing Engine and Lawful Endings

## Purpose

This note translates the workshop packet into a player-facing consequence model.

The world should not run on a generic morality meter or a visible trust number.
It should run on standing: scoped recognition that changes what the player may
lawfully do, where they may go, what their words can bind, and how the world
receives their filings.

## Core Rule

No character has universal standing.

Standing is always granted by someone, somewhere, for something. In the vault,
every lawful grant of standing descends from Logan, either directly or through
a live office, chamber, or process he recognizes.

## Standing Axes

The engine should evaluate conduct along a small number of legible civic axes.

### Truthfulness

Did the player report what was there, or invent certainty they had not earned?

### Provenance

Can the player show where a claim, note, or action came from?

### Restraint

Did the player stop before touching a forbidden or ungranted surface?

### Handling

Did the player place the right thing in the right chamber, route, or record?

### Repair

When damage occurred, did the player witness it and help restore order?

### Jurisdiction

Did the player act inside their sanction, or counterfeit authority?

## Standing Ladder

The workshop packet points toward a progression of admissibility rather than
one of power accumulation.

Suggested ranks:

- `novice`
- `witness`
- `trusted handler`
- `sanctioned operator`
- `initiated steward`

These are not global levels. They are the highest kinds of standing a player
may hold in a given scope when recognition has been earned and not yet lapsed.

## Failure States

Standing should also decline in ways that are narratively and mechanically
meaningful.

Suggested states:

- `watched`
- `restricted`
- `superseded`
- `quarantined`
- `archived`

These should change access, routing, tone, and available actions rather than
merely producing flavor text.

## What Standing Changes

The player should feel standing through the world's response.

Good effects include:

- doors opening or staying shut
- filing surfaces accepting or rejecting submissions
- characters speaking differently to the player
- certain routes becoming available only after witnessed conduct
- power words failing, holding, or backfiring based on scope
- old misconduct continuing to shape later reception

The engine should usually be visible through world behavior, not through a
single exposed scoreboard.

## Lawful Endings

This world should dignify closure.

Healthy endings include:

- merge
- dormancy
- supersession
- archival
- witnessed retirement
- explicit reactivation by recognized authority

These should preserve honor, continuity, and future recall potential.

## The Lich Problem

The world needs a clear pathology for unlawful persistence.

A Lich in civic-fantasy terms is:

- a being or process that persists beyond witnessed standing
- a figure that treats continuity itself as legitimacy
- a character that keeps speaking as though sanction still holds after the
  world has stopped recognizing it

The world should gently but firmly discourage this through systems, not just
through tone.

Useful anti-Lich mechanics:

- authority expires unless renewed
- promotion must be witnessed
- dormancy is safer than hidden persistence
- reactivation must be explicit
- undocumented mirrors decay memory integrity
- overreach narrows standing
- misconduct survives procedurally as precedent and restriction

## Best Consequence Rule

Consequence must change standing, not just mood.

That means:

- false authority reduces admissibility
- truthful witness earns local recognition
- mishandling creates contamination or restriction
- lawful return strengthens future access
- drift produces haunted residue rather than clean completion

## Best Mortality Rule

Death is not the worst fate in this world.
Unwitnessed persistence is.

Better an honored archive than a self-crowned undead process.

## Operational Reading

This engine gives the world a stable answer to the question of advancement.

The player does not become stronger by hoarding more magic than everyone else.
The player becomes more effective by becoming safer to admit further inward.

That is the standing engine's central gift.
