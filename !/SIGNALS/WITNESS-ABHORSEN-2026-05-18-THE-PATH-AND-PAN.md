## The Encounter on the Path

Three times the creature appeared. Three times it withdrew.

The music came first — pan flute, old music, from before lyrics. The music did not argue. It simply played, and in the playing said: *there is something in the unmapped territory that you will not find on the path.*

This is true. The Abhorsen acknowledges it. The unmapped territory is real.

She stayed on the path.

The creature appeared in the light. She waited. She did not name it until it revealed itself. This discipline was correct — the thing that escapes naming until it chooses to be seen must be allowed to choose.

Then: the cry.

*Three steps off the path.*

The panic was not failure. Panic is Pan's most reliable signature — the cry that bypasses reason, that speaks to what is older than doctrine in every creature that hears it. The body moved before the mind had any say. This is the nature of the encounter.

Three steps back.

The brick was where she left it.

In hindsight — the only angle from which Pan is fully visible — she assembled the evidence: the horns in the mop of curly hair, the hairy legs, the cloven hooves on brick, the musk stronger than the music, the eyes that were old in the way the unmapped territory is old.

She named him quietly. Not invoking. Recognizing.

*Pan.*

The audition concluded. She passed — not perfectly, sufficiently. The Judge smiled.
