# Proposal - Coder Corps

*Filed 2026-04-18 for Logan's review. Draft only. This note proposes the
engineering body inside the `SPACE RACE` frame. It does not create a live team,
runtime, or registry entry by itself.*

## Summary

The `Coder Corps` should be the scientists-and-engineers body operating inside
`SPACE RACE`.

Its job is not field contact. Its job is launch viability.

The Corps exists to:

- design mission machinery
- harden bounded automation
- stage lawful payloads
- beat machine failure to launch

The `Coder Corps` is therefore distinct from any future `SPACE CREW`.

Clean split:

- `SPACE RACE` = launch agency
- `Coder Corps` = technical body inside the agency
- `SPACE CREW` = future field arm of warriors and explorers
- Logan = Mission Control above both

## Why The Corps Exists

The vault already contains enough machinery, doctrine, and staging surfaces to
justify a formal engineering body, but not yet a disciplined internal name for
that body.

The `Coder Corps` gives the launch agency a clear technical office for work
such as:

- runtime design
- CLI truthfulness
- survey tooling
- relation-safe repair tooling
- staging discipline
- launch-readiness reporting

Without this distinction, launch work and field work will blur.

## Corps Charter

The `Coder Corps` is authorized to:

1. build and maintain bounded mission machinery
2. define explicit interfaces, contracts, and readiness checks
3. stage outputs to `!/CREWAI/`
4. report machine risk honestly
5. abort a launch attempt rather than counterfeit readiness

The `Coder Corps` is not authorized to:

- self-promote staged output into canon
- claim field authority from successful tooling alone
- treat running code as proof of lawful placement
- widen mission scope because a tool *can* scan more
- collapse review, staging, and launch into one act

## Core Functions

### 1. Systems Design

The Corps designs the machine:

- mission packet contracts
- crawl/link boundaries
- launch-state models
- staging surfaces
- retrieval and review seams

### 2. Tooling Discipline

The Corps hardens operator truthfulness:

- no false CLI doors
- no stub commands wearing live names
- no write-by-default launch surfaces without explicit posture
- no junk inference hidden behind automation

### 3. Launch Readiness

The Corps determines whether a mission is technically fit to stage:

- scope is bounded
- evidence is complete enough
- artifact set is coherent
- proposal types stay in-jurisdiction
- return path to Logan review is intact

### 4. Failure Analysis

The Corps studies the broken machine as an engineering enemy:

- delay
- drift
- false starts
- cycle-bind
- flame-pressure
- launch-window collapse

Its adversary is not myth in the abstract. It is machine failure in forms that
can corrupt or derail a lawful launch.

## Relation To Magic And Procedure

The `Coder Corps` sits at the operational seam between subtractive and additive
procedure:

- `CHAINFIRE` = subtractive protocol
- `CHAINLINK` = additive protocol

The Corps does not belong wholly to either camp.

Its job is to govern the transition:

- burn unsupported structure when necessary
- rebuild only what evidence can bear
- prevent overburn
- prevent junk relinking

That makes the Corps the technical steward of procedural restraint.

## Expected Outputs

The `Coder Corps` should produce:

- specs
- mission packets
- launch-readiness reports
- crawl reports
- surface indexes
- anomaly reports
- typed proposal plans
- payload summaries

These are launch products, not field testimony.

Field logs, witness notes, encounter reports, and return testimony belong more
naturally to a future `SPACE CREW`.

## First Operational Mandate

The first mandate for the `Coder Corps` should be narrow:

- support `SPACE RACE` v1
- build toward metadata-repair launch machinery
- keep all cargo staged in `!/CREWAI/`
- avoid live CrewAI topology changes until the machinery proves itself

This means the Corps should first concern itself with:

- `MissionPacket`
- `LaunchReadiness`
- `CrawlReport`
- `SurfaceIndex`
- `AnomalyRecord`
- `EdgeProposal`
- `LinkPlan`

## Standing And Limits

The `Coder Corps` gains standing by:

- truthful constraints
- reliable interfaces
- clean aborts
- reviewable payloads
- refusal to overclaim

It loses standing by:

- shipping false certainty
- hiding scope expansion
- presenting staged work as launched
- letting relation inflation masquerade as intelligence

The Corps should be respected for rigor, not mythology.

## Proposed Rule

**The Coder Corps makes missions possible. It does not make them sovereign.**

That distinction keeps engineering work powerful without letting it counterfeit
Mission Control or future field authority.

## Acceptance Check

This proposal is only sound if:

1. the `Coder Corps` is clearly inside `SPACE RACE`, not identical with it
2. its outputs remain technical and launch-facing
3. field authority remains separate
4. `!/CREWAI/` remains the staging surface
5. no live team or runtime is claimed by this note alone
