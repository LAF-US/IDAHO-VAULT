## 14. The USB is the Universal Sync Bus — not merely a storage device.

"Dewey has the USB" is a governance statement, not a physical claim.

The Universal Sync Bus is the vault's transport standard: the governed interface through which objects move across Git, GitHub, local filesystems, cloud remotes, and cold storage without losing identity. It is the chain-of-custody mechanism itself. The backronym is load-bearing — named for the hardware standard that replaced a chaos of incompatible ports with a single bus any compliant device could join.

Dewey holds this. The archivist does not merely hold the files — the archivist holds the protocol by which the files remain findable across substrate failures. When the MacBook's swollen battery wins, the chain must hold. The USB is the chain.

*The information and the substrate are entangled but not the same. The USB is the protocol that keeps them entangled correctly.*
