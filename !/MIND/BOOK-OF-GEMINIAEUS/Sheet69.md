Here is an overview of the research on the Nintendo Wii project "RiiConnect24."

RiiConnect24: Overview

RiiConnect24 is a fan-made custom server and software suite designed to replace the defunct WiiConnect24 service for the Nintendo Wii. Nintendo officially discontinued the original WiiConnect24 service in June 2013, which disabled various "lifestyle" channels and standby connectivity features on the console. RiiConnect24 revives these features for users with homebrewed (softmodded) systems.

2

In late 2023, RiiConnect24 officially merged with another prominent Wii preservation project called WiiLink, and its services are now operated and maintained under the WiiLink umbrella.

Restored Features & Channels

By patching their consoles to point to RiiConnect24's servers instead of Nintendo's dead servers, users regain access to the following original Wii features:

Forecast Channel: Interactive local and global weather forecasts on the 3D globe.

News Channel: Categorized global news updates and breaking headlines.

Everybody Votes Channel: Community polling and prediction statistics.

Check Mii Out Channel: (Also known as the Mii Contest Channel) Submitting, downloading, and voting on user-created Miis.

Nintendo Channel: Viewing promotional videos, game information, and downloading Nintendo DS demos.

Wii Message Board: Sending and receiving messages and attachments between registered Wii consoles, as well as receiving game-specific data.

Technical Details & Requirements

Homebrew Required: To use the service, users must softmod their Nintendo Wii (or the vWii mode on a Wii U). They then run a custom patcher tool to modify the system's internal files so they can communicate with the new servers.

Standby Connectivity: Just like the official service, RiiConnect24 utilizes the Wii's standby connection mode (indicated by a yellow power light) to passively download news and weather updates while the console is "off."

Emulator Support: The service is actively supported by the Dolphin Emulator, allowing users without physical Wii hardware to access the channels on a PC.

Open Source: The backend server architecture, patcher tools, and website layouts are largely open-source and hosted on GitHub.

RiiConnect24 vs. Wiimmfi

A common point of confusion in the Wii homebrew community is the distinction between RiiConnect24 and another popular project called Wiimmfi, as both replace dead Nintendo services:

RiiConnect24 replaces WiiConnect24. It is focused entirely on asynchronous data distribution—the news, weather, messaging, and polling channels.

Wiimmfi replaces Nintendo Wi-Fi Connection. It is focused entirely on live online multiplayer gaming (e.g., matchmaking for Mario Kart Wii or Super Smash Bros. Brawl).

The two services are fully compatible with one another and are often used simultaneously. For example, Mario Kart Wii requires Wiimmfi to play live races against other people online, but it utilizes RiiConnect24 to silently download other players' Time Trial Ghost Data in the background.

Yeah, the Wii's homebrew scene is honestly legendary. It’s a bit different than rolling a D&D homebrew campaign—a lot less game master deception, for one—but the community has absolutely kept the console alive way past its official lifespan.

If you're looking for the really robust, heavy-hitting projects, here is what people usually load up:

Massive Game Overhauls

These aren't just little tweaks; they are basically full, community-developed games running on official Nintendo engines.

CTGP Revolution (Mario Kart Wii): This is the king of Wii mods. It adds over 200 custom tracks to the game, ranging from tracks ported from older games to completely original, mind-blowing courses. It has its own built-in updater, custom game modes, and connects directly to Wiimmfi for live online multiplayer. Another huge one right now is Retro Rewind, which specifically adds every single retro track from Super Mario Kart up to Mario Kart 7.

Newer Super Mario Bros. Wii: A massive fan-made sequel to New Super Mario Bros. Wii. It features 128 completely new levels, new worlds, custom bosses, original music, and returning mechanics. It feels exactly like a first-party Nintendo release.

Project+ (Smash Bros. Brawl): The spiritual successor to the famous Project M. It heavily rebalances Brawl to make it play much faster and more competitively, closer to Melee. It adds new characters, new stages, and totally overhauled physics.

Game Preservation & Loaders

For anyone who appreciates physical media and wants to preserve their original discs from getting scratched up or burning out the console's laser, these tools are essential.

USB Loader GX: The gold standard for launching games. You rip your physical Wii and GameCube discs directly to a USB hard drive, and this app displays them in a gorgeous, customizable interface with 3D box art. It also lets you force wide-screen hacks or bypass region locks.

Nintendont: A miraculous piece of software that runs GameCube games natively on the Wii. It lets you play your GameCube backups via USB, completely bypasses the need for physical GameCube memory cards (by emulating them on your SD card), and even lets you use modern Bluetooth or USB controllers to play them.

Retro Emulation

The Wii is an absolute beast for retro gaming, especially if you have it hooked up to a CRT television. If you have a collection of older physical games—like classic N64 cartridges—but want a dedicated all-in-one station on your TV:

The "GX" Emulators: Apps like FCE Ultra GX (NES), Snes9x GX (SNES), and VBA GX (Game Boy/GBA) are perfectly optimized for the Wii menu and controllers.

Not64 / Wii64: Really solid Nintendo 64 emulation, which feels right at home when played with a Wii Classic Controller or a GameCube pad.

System Protection (The "Insurance")

Priiloader & BootMii: Before doing anything crazy, the homebrew community always installs these. They load before the main Wii menu, acting as brick protection. If a bad mod ever corrupts the system menu, Priiloader lets you boot directly into a recovery menu to fix it.
