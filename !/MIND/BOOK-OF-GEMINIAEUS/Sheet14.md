Conversation with Gemini

You said

I have a Specialized brand e-bike. We bought a cart to tow with it. How do I mount it?

Gemini said

That's an exciting new way to use your e-bike! Mounting a tow cart, or trailer, typically involves the rear axle of your bike, and the correct steps largely depend on the type of axle your Specialized e-bike has and the hitch mechanism of your cart.

Specialized e-bikes, especially modern models, often use a thru-axle, which may require a special adapter.

Here are the general types of rear axles and the corresponding mounting methods:

1. Thru-Axle (Most Common on Modern E-Bikes)

Identification: A thick, unthreaded rod that slides through the hub and frame dropout, secured by threading into the opposite dropout or by a nut/lever on the non-drive side.

Mounting Method: You will almost certainly need a thru-axle adapter specifically designed to work with your trailer hitch.

Measure: You need to know the diameter, length, and thread pitch (e.g., 12mm×1.0, 12mm×1.5) of your current thru-axle.

Purchase Adapter: Replace your existing thru-axle with a trailer-compatible thru-axle (e.g., from brands like Robert Axle Project or Burley). This new axle has a coupling point (a flat side or a stub) to attach the trailer hitch.

Installation: Carefully follow the instructions for the new axle, ensuring it is tightened to the correct torque specification (usually found on the axle or in your bike manual).

2. Quick-Release Axle

Identification: A thin skewer with a lever on one end and a nut on the other.

Mounting Method: The trailer hitch plate is typically designed to fit directly onto the skewer.

Remove Skewer: Open the lever and remove the nut and spring from the opposite side, then slide the quick-release skewer out.

Install Hitch: Place the trailer hitch plate onto the skewer, ensuring it fits flush in the frame dropout (you may need to remove one of the small skewer springs).

Re-install: Re-insert the skewer through the axle, re-install the spring and nut, and close the quick-release lever, checking that it is very tight.

3. Bolt-On / Solid Axle (Less Common on E-Bikes)

Identification: A solid axle secured by two nuts on either end.

Mounting Method:

Loosen Nut: Remove the nut on the side you want to mount the hitch (usually the non-drive side).

Install Hitch: Slide the hitch plate onto the exposed axle end.

Secure: Re-tighten the nut securely.

Crucial Next Steps & Safety:

1. Identify Your Axle: Determine whether your Specialized e-bike uses a thru-axle or a quick-release/solid axle.

2. Consult the Manuals: Always follow the installation instructions provided by both your cart manufacturer and your Specialized e-bike model for towing compatibility and weight limits.

3. Check for an Adapter: If you have a thru-axle, you likely need a specific adapter. Check the cart manufacturer's website or an adapter specialist like Robert Axle Project, mentioning your Specialized e-bike model and year.

4. Security: Regardless of the type, always ensure the safety strap from the trailer arm is wrapped around your bike's frame as a backup.

Why the Thru-Axle is the Main Challenge

The main complication is that a standard trailer hitch is not compatible with a thru-axle. Your current axle is the structural bolt holding your wheel on, and it does not have a mounting point for the trailer hitch.

1. Measurement is Key: You must replace your current thru-axle with a trailer-specific thru-axle adapter (like those made by The Robert Axle Project or the trailer manufacturer). To do this, you need to know three critical measurements of your current axle:

Diameter (likely 12mm)

Total Length (e.g., 175mm)

Thread Pitch (e.g., 1.0, 1.5, or 1.75)

2. Torque is Crucial: The most important part of this installation is re-installing the new axle adapter to the correct torque specification. This is a safety issue; if the axle is too loose, the wheel can shift or come off. If it's too tight, you can damage the frame threads. A quality torque wrench is essential for this step.

Recommendation

Since this installation directly involves the wheel axle and is a key safety point (especially when towing), I recommend the following:

Option 1: If you have a torque wrench and are comfortable looking up the specs and following the adapter's instructions, go DIY. The process itself is not overly difficult, but requires precision.

Option 2: If you do not have a torque wrench or are unsure about finding the correct axle specs, take it to your Specialized dealer or a trusted bike shop. They will be able to correctly measure your axle, order the right part, and install it to the precise torque specification quickly and safely. This gives you the best peace of mind.
