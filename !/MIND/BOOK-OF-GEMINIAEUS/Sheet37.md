In Derek Landy's Skulduggery Pleasant series, the "Three Names" framework is the foundation of the world’s magic system. It is built on the idea that names are not just labels but ties to an individual's soul and the source of magic itself.

The system is designed around a hierarchy of vulnerability and power:

1. The Given Name

This is the name your parents give you at birth.

The Power: If a sorcerer knows your given name, they can exert limited control over you—similar to hypnosis. They can make you fall asleep, forget things, or walk to a specific location.

The Risk: It cannot force you to do anything against your basic instincts (like self-harm), but it makes you a "puppet" to those with the knowledge. This is why mages are so secretive about their birth names.

2. The Taken Name

This is a name you choose for yourself to "seal" your given name.

The Purpose: Once you adopt a taken name, your given name loses its power over you. It acts as a magical shield.

The Culture: Most sorcerers choose names that reflect their personality or aspirations (e.g., Skulduggery Pleasant, Ghastly Bespoke, Valkyrie Cain).

The Rule: Traditionally, once a name is "taken," it cannot be changed. It becomes your official identity in the magical world.

3. The True Name

This is the "real" you—the name written into the fabric of the universe (and the Book of Names).

The Ultimate Power: Knowing your own True Name grants nearly god-like power, as it provides a direct, unhindered connection to the source of magic.

The Ultimate Risk: If someone else learns your True Name before you "seal" it, they gain total control over your mind and body.

Sealing: To prevent this, sorcerers can have their True Name "sealed" (sometimes through a grizzly surgical process). Once sealed, the name cannot be used by others to control you, but you can still access its power.

Examples: Only a few True Names are revealed in the series, such as Darquesse (Valkyrie’s True Name) and Argeddion.
