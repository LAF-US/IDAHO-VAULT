How can I turn on call recording

Gemini said

Turning on call recording depends heavily on your device brand, operating system, and location. Because of privacy laws, this feature is restricted or works differently in various regions.

Here is how to enable it for the most common platforms:

1. Google Phone App (Most Androids & Pixel)

If your phone uses the standard Google Phone app, the feature is built-in but only appears if your carrier and region support it.

Open the Phone app.

Tap the three-dot menu (top right) and select Settings.

Tap Call recording.

Under "Always record," select Numbers not in your contacts or Selected numbers.

Note: In many regions, the app will play an audible announcement: "This call is now being recorded," to notify both parties.

2. Samsung Galaxy Devices

Samsung often includes a native recorder in its own "Phone" app that doesn't always notify the other party (depending on local laws).

Open the Samsung Phone app.

Tap the three dots > Settings.

Tap Record calls.

Toggle on Auto record calls. You can choose to record all calls, unsaved numbers, or specific numbers.

3. iPhone (iOS 18 and later)

Apple recently introduced native call recording. If you are on the latest software:

During an active call, look for the Record icon (a small waveform/circle) in the top left corner of the call screen.

Tap it to start recording.

Important: iOS will automatically notify the other person with a voice prompt that the call is being recorded.

The transcript and audio will be saved to your Notes app.

4. Third-Party Apps

If your phone doesn't have a native option, you can use apps from the App Store or Play Store (e.g., Cube ACR or Rev Call Recorder). However, these often require a subscription and may use a "three-way call" workaround to record.

[!IMPORTANT] Legal Disclaimer: Call recording laws vary by state and country. Some areas require "One-Party Consent" (only you need to know), while others require "All-Party Consent" (everyone on the line must agree). Always check your local regulations before recording.
