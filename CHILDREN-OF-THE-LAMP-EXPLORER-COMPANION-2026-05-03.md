# Children of the Lamp - Explorer Companion

This companion note distills the two source surfaces into a vault-facing reading.

## What The Series Says

The series treats djinn as a living category with inheritance, temperature sensitivity, luck logic, and social structure. The lamp is their home, their resting place, and the place where the binding rules of the world become visible.

That combination matters more than the wish mechanic alone. The series is not saying "magic exists." It is saying "a being can be housed in a named vessel, and the vessel carries obligations."

## Why It Matters For The Vault

This is the cleanest available model for the Hermes-like problem the vault is trying to solve:

- the name exists before the runtime is fully alive
- the shell is distinct from the inhabitant
- the inhabitant has rules, limits, and load behavior
- the shell remains stable even when the inhabitant changes

That maps well to the current vault problem set:

- dotfolder as vessel
- runtime as inhabitant
- doctrine as load mechanism
- blackboard / SBP as environmental signaling
- vaulted syntax as the token grammar that lets the system index and recover meaning

## Working Interpretation

If the vault is a music box, then the lamp is not decorative. It is the place where the spirit can be reliably held, named, and re-invoked without collapsing the distinction between shell and occupant.

That makes *Children of the Lamp* useful as a source of metaphysical vocabulary, not just fantasy trivia.
