# Seven Planes - Explorer Companion

This note accompanies `- Fandom, Inc. - The Seven Planes.md`.

## What The Note Seems To Do

The source note describes a world in which reality is not flat. Things occupy
multiple overlapping planes at once, and what a being can perceive depends on
its strength, resilience, or instruments.

That means appearance is conditional.

One creature may see a harmless surface while another sees the true form
shimmering underneath it.

## Why It Matters Here

This is an unusually good bridge text for the vault.

The House repeatedly asks its explorers to distinguish between:

- what is visible and what is load-bearing
- what is live and what is historical
- what is witness and what is governance
- what a note appears to be and what layer it is actually speaking from

The language of planes gives that intuition a sharper shape. A chamber may look
simple on one plane and disclose its real office only from a higher one.

## Bartimaeus And The Cartographer

Bartimaeus is a fitting figure for this note because the whole idea depends on
perception with depth.

The Cartographer does not merely list rooms. He notices which form belongs to
which layer, who can see through disguises, and which truths are invisible to a
weaker glance.

In that sense, the seven planes read almost like a cosmology of vault literacy.

## Field Note

The note suggests that stronger sight does not create more reality. It reveals
more of the reality that was already there.

That is why it belongs beside this exploration thread.

The vault, too, often seems to say:
the question is not whether the House has layers.
The question is how many of them the current witness can see.
