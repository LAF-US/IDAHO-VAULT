Shape-shifting lets djinn become any animal or object they choose.

The page emphasizes that the djinn retains thought and sense while transformed, but the transformation still has constraints: time limits, residue effects, and tribal exceptions such as the Marid camel form. When disguised as objects, djinn must behave like objects.

That makes shape-shifting a useful model for persona surfaces: the same entity can change form without losing continuity, but the form still imposes behavior.
