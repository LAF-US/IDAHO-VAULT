Idaho Code §74-202 defines "deliberation" as the receipt or exchange of information or opinion relating to a decision, with no quorum threshold — making it the statute most directly applicable to [[JFAC Working Groups|working groups]] and the one members appear least aware of.

## Operative Definition

"Deliberation" means receipt or exchange of information or opinion relating to a decision.

**No quorum threshold.** This is critical: every member interviewed in the [[JFAC Working Groups]] investigation relies exclusively on the no-quorum defense. None addressed §74-202.

## Application to Working Groups

Working groups involve multiple members exchanging information and opinions about budget decisions. Under §74-202, that is deliberation regardless of how many members are present. The quorum-counting behavior confirmed by [[Stephanie Cook|Cook]] and [[Denise Woodward|Woodward]] addresses the wrong legal standard — quorum is irrelevant to the deliberation definition.

The [[Idaho Attorney General Open Meetings Manual]] sub-quorum evasion doctrine reinforces this: a public body may not avoid open meetings requirements by conducting meetings of less than a quorum.
