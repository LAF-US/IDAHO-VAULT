# Digital Sovereignty

Control over one's digital existence — data, infrastructure, and identity.

---

## Definition

The ability to have authority and autonomy over your digital records, platforms, and persona. Not a single law or technology but a framework spanning legal, technical, and philosophical dimensions.

Three layers (conceptual — originated in Gemini conversation 2026-04-06, fact-checked):

1. **Data Ownership** — Local-first storage; files as property, not platform licenses
2. **Infrastructure Autonomy** — Owning the "land" your data sits on (domains, servers, repos)
3. **Institutional Literacy** — Understanding how groups (legislatures, corporations, platforms) manage individual data
