**Mosrael** is the second of the [Nine Bright Shiners](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Nine_Bright_Shiners "Nine Bright Shiners"). In [The Beginning](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Beginning "The Beginning"), Mosrael became part of the [Charter.](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Charter "The Charter") As a [bell](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Bells "Bells"), Mosrael is called the Waker, who [brings dead bodies to life](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Dead_Hands "Dead Hands") as the wielder takes their place in [Death](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death") in a see-saw effect. Additionally, in well-trained hands, a "mere whisper" can be used to "reveal the hidden, to open locks and ways, and indeed, wake those whose sleep was not of the ordinary kind.". Exceptional care needs to be taken, and even careful hands, it will still have a strong tendency to push the wielder into Death [^1] It was wielded by [Sanar](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Sanar "Sanar") and [Ryelle](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Ryelle "Ryelle") together at the binding of [Orannis](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Orannis "Orannis").

## Bell Sound

Mosrael has a sound like tens of parrots screeching, a noise that bursts into the air and weaves itself into the wind, multiplying into the scream of a thousand birds.[^2]

## Trivia

- Mosrael is the favored bell of [necromancers](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Necromancer "Necromancer"), though the [Abhorsens](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Abhorsen "Abhorsen") very rarely use it.
- Mosrael is heard four times in the series, save for the binding of Orannis: first when [Sabriel](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Sabriel "Sabriel") is trying to open the door in the [Long Cliffs](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Long_Cliffs "Long Cliffs") toward [Abhorsen's House](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Abhorsen%27s_House "Abhorsen's House"), second when she uses it in tandem with [Kibeth](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Kibeth "Kibeth") to free her father in Death, thirdly by the necromancer who pursues [Ferin](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Ferin "Ferin"), and lastly by [Terciel](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Terciel "Terciel") to open the hidden door in the Long Cliffs [^1].
- Mosrael is one of three bells [Idrach](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Idrach "Idrach") forges in Death.
