# Fact-Check Report: Gemini Claims Verification

**Date:** 2026-04-06
**Analyst:** Claude Code (The Abhorsen)
**Method:** Web search verification against primary sources (legislature.idaho.gov, TSA.gov, WhiteHouse.gov, law.cornell.edu, etc.)

---

## CLAIM 1: Idaho H0078 (2025) & S1299 (2026) — Digital Driver's License

**Gemini's claim:** Bills that "institutionalized the digital driver's license" and authorized ITD to issue digital IDs compliant with ISO standards, effective July 1, 2026. S1299 described as a "limitation" bill prohibiting government from requiring digital ID and preventing law enforcement phone searches when showing digital credential. Passed March 2026.

**Verdict: PARTIALLY ACCURATE**

- **H0078 (2025):** Real bill. Passed the Idaho House 37-33 on March 11, 2025. Authorizes ITD to issue electronic driver's licenses, compliant with AAMVA and ISO/IEC 18013-5. Implementation target is July 1, 2026. **However, H0078 did NOT pass the Senate as of the last recorded action (March 12, 2025 — referred to Senate Transportation Committee).** Calling it "institutionalized" is misleading if it never became law in the 2025 session.
- **S1299 (2026):** Real bill. It IS a limitation bill on digital ID. Passed Senate 35-0 on March 19, 2026, passed House 70-0 on March 26, 2026, and was **signed by the Governor on March 31, 2026**. The claim that it "passed March 2026" is **verified**.
- **Law enforcement phone search provision:** The ORIGINAL bill contained language stating "information incidentally observed on a device shall not be used to establish probable cause or justification for further search or seizure." However, this provision was **removed by amendment** before passage. Gemini describing SB 1299 as "preventing law enforcement phone searches" is **inaccurate for the enacted version**.

**Corrections needed:** H0078 appears to have stalled in the Senate (not enacted). S1299 passed but the phone-search protection was stripped by amendment.

## CLAIM 2: TSA $45 Fee

**Gemini's claim:** $45 fee for travelers without REAL ID-compliant card.

**Verdict: VERIFIED**

TSA introduced a $45 fee option (TSA ConfirmID) for travelers without REAL ID-compliant identification, effective February 1, 2026. The fee covers a 10-day travel window. This is confirmed by TSA.gov press releases from December 2025 and January 2026. The REAL ID Act enforcement began May 7, 2025.

**No corrections needed.**

## CLAIM 3: Idaho FAST Act (S1423) — Stablecoin Vendor Payments

**Gemini's claim:** Passed March 2026, allowing Idaho to pay vendors in stablecoins.

**Verdict: PARTIALLY ACCURATE**

- S1423 is real. It establishes the Financial Accountability Stablecoin Transaction (FAST) Act.
- It **passed the Senate** Third Reading on March 27, 2026 (22-13).
- However, it was then **referred to the House Commerce and Human Resources Committee**. There is no confirmation it completed the full legislative process or was signed into law.
- The bill's scope: It defines and governs stablecoins in Idaho, referencing the federal GENIUS Act. The characterization as "allowing Idaho to pay vendors in stablecoins" may be an oversimplification — need to verify bill text for exact scope.

**Corrections needed:** Claiming it "passed" without noting it only passed one chamber (as of available records) is misleading. Final disposition unclear.

## CLAIM 4: UCC Article 12 Amendments — Idaho Adopted CERs

**Gemini's claim:** Idaho adopted the 2022 UCC Amendments creating "Controllable Electronic Records" (CERs) as a new asset class. Claims Idaho has "fully integrated" these.

**Verdict: LIKELY FABRICATED**

Idaho has **NOT** adopted the uniform UCC Article 12. According to multiple legal tracking sources (Paul Hastings, ABA, Uniform Law Commission), Idaho is specifically identified as a state that has pursued **non-uniform amendments** to its UCC regarding digital assets, rather than adopting the standard Article 12 framework. As of the search results, approximately 24-33 states plus DC have adopted the uniform Article 12 — Idaho is not among them. Idaho introduced HB 709 (2026) regarding programmable money and HB 702 (2026) on Article 8 amendments, but these are not the same as adopting Article 12.

**Corrections needed:** Idaho has NOT "fully integrated" UCC Article 12. It has gone a different route with non-uniform amendments. This is a significant factual error.

## CLAIM 5: Idaho Code 31-2903 (2025) — Electronic Document Recording

**Gemini's claim:** Law stating any requirement for "original" or "paper" document for recording (like a land deed) is satisfied by an electronic document.

**Verdict: VERIFIED**

Idaho Code 31-2903 ("Validity of Electronic Documents") is part of the Uniform Real Property Electronic Recording Act. It states: "If a law requires that a document be an original, be on paper or another tangible medium, or be in writing as a condition for recording, the requirement is satisfied by an electronic document satisfying this chapter." Also covers electronic signatures, notarization, and acknowledgments.

**Note:** The "(2025)" date is misleading — this statute has been in Idaho Code for years (part of the Uniform Real Property Electronic Recording Act adoption). It was not newly enacted in 2025. Gemini may be conflating the code year with an enactment date.

## CLAIM 6: Idaho Freedom Foundation Rating of SB 1299

**Gemini's claim:** IFF rated SB 1299 lower after amendments because a provision was removed that would have banned police from using "incidentally observed" info on phone as probable cause.

**Verdict: PARTIALLY ACCURATE**

IFF gave SB 1299 a **+1 rating** (positive). IFF DID explicitly state that the Senate amendment "made the bill worse" and DID identify the removal of the "incidentally observed" provision as a key loss. However, the rating itself was **not lowered** — it remained at +1. IFF also noted other negative amendment effects: statutory damages ($500-$2,500) were eliminated, civil penalties were removed, and personal liability protections were added for government employees.

**Corrections needed:** IFF criticized the amendments but did NOT lower the rating. The characterization of a rating reduction is inaccurate.

## CLAIM 7: House v. NCAA Settlement — $20.5 Million Revenue Sharing

**Gemini's claim:** $20.5 million annual revenue sharing for power conference schools.

**Verdict: VERIFIED**

The House v. NCAA settlement was approved June 6, 2025. The annual revenue-sharing cap starts at approximately **$20.5 million per school** for 2025-26, increasing approximately 4% annually over the 10-year term. The cap is calculated as 22% (some sources say 22.5%) of Power Five schools' average athletic revenue. 319 Division I schools opted in. The settlement also includes ~$2.8 billion in back damages.

**Minor note:** The $20.5M is a per-school cap, not total. Gemini's phrasing "for power conference schools" is correct but could be clearer.

## CLAIM 8: NIL Go Platform

**Gemini's claim:** NCAA platform requiring Division I athletes to report third-party NIL deals worth $600+ within five business days.

**Verdict: VERIFIED**

NIL Go is a real platform created by the **College Sports Commission** (not the NCAA directly) with support from Deloitte. It requires Division I student-athletes to report third-party NIL deals valued at $600 or more within 5 business days of execution. The College Sports Commission reviews these deals for "valid business purpose" compliance.

**Minor correction:** NIL Go is operated by the College Sports Commission, not the NCAA itself, though the CSC was created as part of the House v. NCAA settlement framework.

## CLAIM 9: Idaho Has No State-Level NIL Statute

**Gemini's claim:** Idaho has no state-level NIL statute as of April 2026.

**Verdict: VERIFIED**

Multiple sources confirm Idaho has no state NIL law. Idaho athletes operate under NCAA interim policies (effective July 1, 2021) and now under the House settlement framework and CSC rules, not state legislation. Idaho is among the states with no proposed NIL legislation.

**No corrections needed.**

The executive order exists but is titled **"Urgent National Action To Save College Sports"** (not "Saving College Sports"). It was signed by President Trump on **April 3, 2026** — which is very recent, not "early 2026." The order addresses transfers, eligibility, NIL regulation, and threatens federal funding for non-compliance. Operative sections effective August 1, 2026.

## CLAIM 12: Idaho Code 74-101(13) — Public Records Definition

**Gemini's claim:** Definition of public records as things "prepared, owned, used or retained" by an agency.

**Verdict: VERIFIED**

Idaho Code 74-101(13) defines "public record" as: "any writing containing information relating to the conduct or administration of the public's business prepared, owned, used or retained by any state agency, independent public body corporate and politic or local agency regardless of physical form or characteristics." The subsection number (13) is confirmed correct.

**No corrections needed.**

## CLAIM 13: Idaho Code 74-102 — "Personal Notes" Exception

**Gemini's claim:** "Personal notes" exception for public officials.

**Verdict: PARTIALLY ACCURATE (wrong section)**

The personal notes exception exists but is found in **Idaho Code 74-101(13)**, not 74-102. Section 74-101(13) states: "personal notes created by a public official solely for his own use shall not be a public record as long as such personal notes are not shared with any other person or entity." Section 74-102 is the "Right to Examine" provision, not the personal notes exception.

**Corrections needed:** The exception is in 74-101(13), not 74-102.

## CLAIM 14: 17 U.S.C. 101 — Work for Hire Duration and Termination

**Gemini's claim:** Copyright duration of 95 years from publication or 120 from creation for works made for hire. Termination rights: "None" for work for hire vs. 35-year reclamation for individual works.

**Verdict: VERIFIED**

- The definition of "work made for hire" is in 17 U.S.C. 101. The **duration** is governed by 17 U.S.C. 302(c): 95 years from publication or 120 years from creation, whichever expires first.
- Termination rights under 17 U.S.C. 203 do NOT apply to works made for hire. Individual authors can terminate assignments after 35 years; work-for-hire authors cannot.

**Minor note:** The duration is technically in Section 302(c), not Section 101. Section 101 provides the definition. But the substance is correct.

## CLAIM 15: ISO 18013-5 — Standard for Idaho Digital IDs

**Gemini's claim:** ISO 18013-5 is the standard Idaho is using for digital IDs.

**Verdict: VERIFIED**

ISO/IEC 18013-5:2021 defines specifications for mobile driving licences (mDLs). Idaho's H0078 specifically references compliance with "ISO/IEC 18013-5" and AAMVA standards. The standard was published September 30, 2021 and governs secure storage, transmission, and validation of driver's licenses on mobile devices.

**No corrections needed.**

## CLAIM 16: Raw Story v. OpenAI — CMI Removal Case

**Gemini's claim:** Dismissed late 2024, upheld through 2025, regarding removal of Copyright Management Information.

**Verdict: PARTIALLY ACCURATE**

- The case was **dismissed in November 2024** by Judge Colleen McMahon (S.D.N.Y.) on standing grounds — plaintiffs could not demonstrate concrete injury from CMI removal.
- The dismissal was **upheld on reconsideration** in early 2025 (Raw Story failed to convince the judge to reconsider).
- However, a **split emerged** in S.D.N.Y.: In *Intercept Media v. OpenAI*, Judge Rakoff **denied** OpenAI's motion to dismiss similar CMI claims, creating a circuit split within the same district.
- Saying it was "upheld through 2025" is accurate for the Raw Story case specifically but omits the important context that the legal theory succeeded in the parallel Intercept Media case.

**Corrections needed:** The dismissal was on standing, not merits. The legal theory (CMI removal) was NOT definitively rejected — it succeeded in the Intercept Media case in the same court.
