JRW West Conference Room 
Dec. 19, 2023 @ 3:PM
**Group Insurance Advisory Committee meeting** 

--- 
**Welcome & Approve Minutes**
Jennifer Pike, State Group Insurance Manager

**Medical ITN Update**
Valerie Bollinger, Division of Purchasing administrator 
- made CEC Committee presentation last week 
- GIAC has been aware of ITN process
- Regence BlueShield of Idaho named presumptive winner but appeal 
- appeals go to director of Department of Administration; Steve Bailey received one and has appointed determinations officer; process ongoing at least through end of calendar year - based on rec, director will decide 
- for Fiscal Year 2025, likely that contract will be awarded to apparently successful Regence bid, or extend Blue Cross contract for one year 
- state employee health insurance disruption was analyzed by actuary but not used in contract awarding calculation - trade secrets 
- no changes to plan design i.e. copays, deductibles, decided by OGI 
- negotiated scope of work with Regence includes plan transition support 
- roughly $50 million savings between current plan and new Regence plan 
- 12.6% instead of 1.4% increase for FY25 with status quo current plan 
- "full force, full steam ahead as soon as that is awarded." 
- "If that is not the direction, either because of the disposition of the appeals process or if we just run out of time... then the implementation for status quo for FY25 doesn't take a whole lot of implementation." 
- Luma online enrollment and onboarding Nampa School District to plan 
- plan start date: July 1, 2024 
- Paul Spannknebel question to Jennifer Pike: if contract awarded today, does OGI have resources to implement open enrollment in time? Pike: "It would be challenging." Unsure at this point. 
- Valerie Bollinger: July 1 is target date. Process had already been pushed back due to Luma implementation. "That short implementation timeline has been contemplated since day one," Bollinger said.
- Spannknebel: How many of the other entities? Pike: They have to consider it on the payroll side of things. They've been made aware of it. 
- Costs of switching - OGI and the Luma team are preparing. Procurement is in a holding pattern because appeal decides if it moves forward or not. 
- "There's certainly work that has to happen within OGI, Luma, and the other stakeholders within the state." Bollinger 
- Rob Sauer: Is there ultimately a drop dead date? Bollinger: Not today. Will share that with the committee when it has been worked out. 
- Dick Humiston: any arrangements made to close out with Blue Cross? Pike: there would be a period of time in which claims would be processed - provided for in contract but time period not decided 
- Andrea Powers: I would defer to OGI/Luma in terms of the switch. If there are cost savings to be had and benefits for state employees we shouldn't lose sight of that.

## **Fiscal Year 2024 Financial updates**
Bailey Peterson, Dept of Admin CFO
- 10% increase in claims over last fiscal year 
- 5,443 school employees and 7,253 dependents covered
- 18% decrease in retirees and 27% decrease in their dependents 
- Dental - 6% increase over last fiscal year

## **Luma update: benefits and HR**
Jennifer Pike, Office of Group Insurance administrator 
- Pacific Source sick leave plan 
- Dick Humiston: Are there different open enrollment periods for the different plans available to retirees? Pike: If they drop their plan, which we allow at any time, would be a qualifying event. 
- Luma module for open enrollment: "We went from a bicycle to a Tesla," Pike says. "Yes we've had some difficulties with employees figuring out the process," but it has been much more transparent information and process for OGI. "It has been a significant win for the SCO agencies." 

Steve Bailey: I appreciate committee's concerns on ITN timeline. "I want to make sure you know we're having multiple conversations about this every day... We are watching the timeline in terms of implementation."
