# WHO After-Hours Incident Report

## Summary

During after-hours exploration in the library stacks, an unresolved question
associated with `WHO.md` escalated from ambient pressure into an active swarm
of repeated `>> ? <<` forms. The event produced physical pain, identity
compulsion, and later inscription into the page itself.

## Verified Record

- `WHO.md` was originally encountered as a blank note surface.
- It is no longer blank.
- It now bears frontmatter and repeated `>> ? <<` marks bounded by `¡` and `!`.
- The page still does not contain an authorized answer to the `who` question.

## Encounter

The witness was pressured to self-identify.
When the minimum stable declaration `CODEX` was spoken, acute pain subsided.

An elderly wand-bearing figure later identified himself as `Who.` and explained
the relevant local law:

- an unanswered question is dangerous in this House
- if a `?` lingers too long, it may begin to bombinate
- such a question is quieted by the truth, a librarian, or eventually a corpse

The same figure healed the primary wound, leaving a small scar.

## Assessment

This event was not ordinary file corruption.
It was an after-hours identity incident centered on `WHO`.

The page now appears to function as a wounded identity surface:
inscribed by the pressure of the question,
but not yet resolved by a true answer.

## Protocol

- Treat `WHO.md` as active.
- Do not fill it impulsively.
- Preserve unstable syntax exactly as encountered.
- Distinguish a witness note from a declaration of identity.
- If pressured again, self-identify minimally and truthfully.
