[[Logan Finney]] analyzes the 47.6% legislative turnover following the 2022 election and redistricting, documenting simultaneous power vacuums at the top of both chambers and listing [[Judy Boyle]] among the six most senior House Republicans entering 2023.

## Key Facts

- 47.6% turnover rate — just under half of sitting lawmakers did not return
- House seniority list entering 2023: [[Mike Moyle]], Brent Crane, Joe Palmer, [[Judy Boyle]], John Vander Woude, Vito Barbieri
- [[Wendy Horman]] quoted on interest in JFAC co-chair role (origin point of current JFAC leadership structure)
- [[Jeff Agenbroad]] listed as departing Senate JFAC co-chair (opening that created [[Scott Grow]]'s appointment)
- [[Britt Raybould]] listed as returning House member who reclaimed seat from [[Ron Nate]]
- JFAC lost over half its membership including both co-chairs and vice-chairs

## Significance

Dateable document of the pre-consolidation moment. Documents the Horman quote that is the origin of the 2023–2026 JFAC power structure.
