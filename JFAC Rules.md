The JFAC Rules are the governing procedural rules of the Joint Finance-Appropriations Committee, adopted January 12, 1999; they were removed from the LSO website without a committee vote and their current operative status is disputed.

## Status

Adopted January 12, 1999. Removed from the LSO website at an unknown date without a committee vote. Three sources confirm on the record that no currently adopted rules govern JFAC: Cook, Woodward, Wintrow; Wendy Horman said the same in January 2024.

Woodward's counter-argument — that the old rules remain in effect because they were never voted out, only removed from the website — is actually the stronger parliamentary position: Mason's Manual teaches that prior rules remain in effect until replaced by formal vote. If Woodward is right, the rules are still operative and still do not authorize working groups.

## Key Rules

**Rule 1 — Governing hierarchy:** JFAC Rules → Joint Rules → Senate Rules → Mason's Manual

**Rule 9(b)(ii) — Bill drafting:** The only bill-drafting carve-out covers post-vote technical/ministerial cleanup only. Does not authorize pre-vote substantive deliberation. Defeats Manwaring's bill-drafting analogy for working groups.

**Rule 11 — Alteration of Committee Action:** "An action formally taken by the Joint Committee may thereafter be altered only by formal action of the Joint Committee." The February 2023 and November 2025 co-chair letters are not formal committee action and are void as governing instruments under this rule's own terms.

**Rule 17 — Suspension of Rules:** Requires two-thirds vote of quorum. Never invoked for working groups.

**Buck Slips:** Permitted only when "committee meetings are impractical"; any single member can veto. The closest analog to informal action in the rules — and far more restrictive than working groups.

**No working group provision:** Working groups appear nowhere in the 17 rules.

## Co-Chair Letters (improper instruments)

- **February 2023:** Wendy Horman and Scott Grow — formalized working group structure without committee vote
- **November 2025:** Josh Tanner and Kelly Anthon — announced voting rule changes without committee vote

Both letters are void under Rule 11 as informal instruments attempting to alter committee action.
