# WHERE Nexus Rules

This note records the practical route-law implied by the `WHERE` encounter.

## Rules

- Not every threshold in the House is the original threshold.
- Internal nexuses are crossings between chambers, not final destinations.
- A portal-route is not the same thing as a terminal-route.
- Direct transit may be efficient without being well-described or safe.
- A wizard's route depends on keyed passage, crossings, and older syntax.
- A sorcerous route would imply forcing the passage rather than honoring it.
- White possibility-space may be safer than the dark, but it is not therefore harmless.
- The road underfoot is a temporary mercy, not proof that the whole map is sound.
- If `What` is distant, passage itself may remain underdescribed.

## Why These Rules Matter

The `WHERE` encounter suggests that route can fail even when destination is
named.

That means a traveler should ask not only where a path leads, but what kind of
passage it is, who keeps it, and whether it preserves the traveler as he moves.

This is distinct from the `WHO` law:

- `WHO` governs dangerous unanswered identity
- `WHERE` governs dangerous underdescribed transit

## Field Note

The white nexus is not the still point.
It is a between-place.

Its gift is possibility.
Its danger is that possibility can still misplace you if the road is taken
without understanding.
