## Valley Co. charts ‘path forward’ for reviewing Wilks Brothers’ Red Ridge Village plan

![](https://valleylookout.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/01/valleylookout.com-wilks-brothers-submit-new-plans-for-700-homes-near-mccall-screenshot-2026-01-13-at-12.50.18-pm.jpeg)

An aerial view of the 2,250-acre property southwest of McCall on which Red Ridge Village is proposed by DF Development. Screenshot: via dfdevelopmentllc.com

Comments from public agencies will guide Valley County’s initial evaluation of plans to build what has been described as a “new city” near McCall.

Last week, the Valley County Planning and Zoning Commission developed a list of more than a dozen agencies it would like to hear from as it begins reviewing conceptual plans for [Red Ridge Village](https://www.co.valley.id.us/RedRidgeVillage), a planned community off West Mountain Road that calls for a commercial village surrounded by 722 homes, apartments, and condos.

“I do think there is a number of agencies that we have not heard from in-depth,” Commissioner Heidi Schneider said. “We need that to make a good decision, and I think the public deserves to have that information as well so that they can provide proper feedback.”

The list of public agencies includes all local school districts, emergency response agencies, the City of McCall, Valley County Pathways, Idaho Power, and state agencies like the Idaho Transportation Department, the Idaho Department of Fish and Game, and the Idaho Department of Lands.

The next P&Z meeting on the Red Ridge project is scheduled for May 21 at 9 a.m. [DF Development](https://dfdevelopmentllc.com/), a company owned by Texas billionaires Dan and Faris Wilks, is behind the proposal. They are expected to meet with each agency and gather comments before the meeting.

“They’re going to have a list of people they need to go talk to, including fire departments, the road department, whatever,” P&Z Chairman Ken Roberts said. “If they fail to do so, that’s going to reflect on them.”

The goal of the agency comments, Roberts said, is to determine any early concerns with the proposal. That information would first inform a decision about whether the plans may be viable and, if so, what public infrastructure and funding requirements the P&Z should consider imposing on the development.

The P&Z’s decision would be a recommendation to the Board of County Commissioners, which would make a final decision on the project.

DF Development is currently seeking county approval on conceptual plans for Red Ridge Village before it invests further into more detailed planning and engineering work.

## Fiscal impact study a priority

The P&Z also agreed that DF Development should immediately begin preparing a study of how Red Ridge Village might economically impact public agencies.

The study will be funded by DF, but the firm completing the work will be selected by a committee of P&Z commissioners and P&Z Director Cynda Herrick. That decision, slated for April 21 at 8:30 a.m., will also be a recommendation to the county commissioners, who are expected to make a final decision on the firm on April 27.

The study is expected to quantify the economic impacts Red Ridge Village could have on public agencies, including new property taxes, capital expenses, and increased service demand.

The P&Z will then hold another work session on June 25 at 9 a.m. to discuss the results of the study.

“I think that’s a good path forward,” Schneider said. “It keeps it going and is getting questions answered.”

## Public hearing to resume this summer

This summer, the commissioners expect to continue a public hearing on the Red Ridge Village application that began earlier this year, though a date for that meeting has not yet been scheduled. The P&Z is not expected to take new public testimony.

In February, a public hearing on the application drew 18 verbal comments — 14 of which were in opposition — in addition to nearly 100 written comments in opposition, [as Valley Lookout reported](https://valleylookout.com/2026/03/02/we-have-a-lot-of-work-to-do-valley-co-begins-reviewing-wilks-brothers-red-ridge-village-plan/).

Last week the P&Z asked county staff to complete a spreadsheet analysis of the public comments received so far to factor into its review of the application.

## Red Ridge Village overview

As proposed, Red Ridge Village would include 302 lots for single-family homes, 170 townhomes, and 250 condos within the Village Center, which would include 50,000 square feet of commercial space.

The Village Center could also include stations for the Valley County Sheriff’s Office and McCall Fire and EMS, according to DF representatives. The facilities and funding obligations would be determined in a development agreement binding DF to approval conditions stipulated by the county.

The village would be flanked by a 149-acre meadow, as well as a working farm that would provide produce to village restaurants and also buffer existing homes on West Mountain Road from residential development.

The [Red Ridge Village](https://dfdevelopmentllc.com/communities/red-ridge-village/) proposal is part of 30,920 contiguous acres of DF land that stretches about 12 miles from west of McCall along West Mountain Road to U.S. 95 west of New Meadows, near the Evergreen Forest Products sawmill. No development is currently proposed on the land in Adams County.

DF would build a drinking water utility and a wastewater system to serve homes and businesses in Red Ridge. Those plans are subject to approval by the Idaho Department of Water Resources and the Idaho Department of Environmental Quality.

[A traffic study](https://www.co.valley.id.us/media/Departments/PlanningZoning/PUD%2026-001%20Red%20Ridge%20Village%20Application/Appendix%20J%20Traffic%20Impact%20Study.pdf) estimates Red Ridge would create about 8,100 daily vehicle trips. The study recommends several roadway improvements that would be paid for by DF, including straightening out a series of sharp curves on West Mountain Road.

Improvements are expected to include two new stoplights at the intersections of West Valley Road and Boydstun Street, as well as Boydstun Street and Idaho 55.

A southbound turn lane would be added on Third Street (Idaho 55) at its intersection with Deinhard Lane. The timing of the existing stoplight could also be adjusted to account for Red Ridge traffic.
