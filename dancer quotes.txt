===== Hava Fisherman

Descriptive text: 

A woman with dark hair who uses a wheelchair sits for an interview. She wears a dark t-shirt with the art and logo of Open Arms Dance Project. White text on an orange background reads, “It’s very fun for me to dance. It’s just hard work.”

Caption: 

Hava Fisherman lives with cerebral palsy and other disabilities and uses a wheelchair. “I never thought I could dance,” she says. But when Megan Brandel, the founding artistic director of the @Open Arms Dance Project, choreographed pieces that included Hava, she changed her mind. “When I saw Megan, I wanted to join,” says Hava. She’s now been with the inclusive dance company for a decade. “It makes me happy that I’m dancing.” Learn more about Open Arms and watch Hava perform in a new @IdahoPTV video, “Flipping the Script.” Check it out at idahoptv.org/arts!

===== Gail Chandler Hawkins

Caption:
At 76, Gail Chandler Hawkins is the oldest member of the Open Arms Dance Project in Boise. The inclusive company welcomes dancers of all ages and abilities. In the 1970s, Hawkins herself taught dance to older people in Washington, D.C. when she was part of “Dance Exchange,” an innovative performing arts group founded by dancer Liz Lerman.

“I’ll never forget people just in their 60s saying to me, “I can’t dance. I’m too old,” Gails says. “Often we created their dances out of their life stories.”

Hawkins loves Open Arms for its similar philosophy. “What we do is adapt.  It’s OK if you can’t do it quite this way, then do it this way.”

“For me, the improvisation is a way of really getting deeply in touch with one’s experience and emotions, which are inside our bodies.”

Recently, Gail was diagnosed with pancreatic cancer. But she still performed at the group’s big concert in April. 

“I’ll be part of Open Arms as long as somebody can get me there,” she says. “It’s just something that really allows us to be who we are.”
