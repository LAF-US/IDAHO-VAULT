**Charter Stones** are large stone monuments "with Charter marks running like quicksilver through the stone, forming and dissolving, only to reform again, in a never-ending story that told of the making of the world." They serve as an easy access point to the [Charter](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Charter_magic "Charter magic") for Charter mages, and suppress [Free Magic](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Free_Magic "Free Magic"), the Dead and any entrance to [Death](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death"), but can be broken by Free Magic [sorcerers](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Free_magic_sorcerers "Free magic sorcerers") with the use of the blood of a Charter mage. If this is done, there is some damage to the physical stone, and the Charter marks freeze, appearing as motionless carvings. There is a pervasive aura of wrongness emanating from broken Charter stones, possibly only detectable by Charter mages, and the boundary between life and Death is easier to cross, instead of harder. Also, Charter Magic becomes more difficult to access, instead of easier.

All Charter Stones were originally created by the [Wallmakers](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Wallmakers "Wallmakers"). There are many throughout the Old Kingdom that strengthen the presence of the Charter, though during the Interregnum many were broken by necromancers loyal to the Greater Dead [Kerrigor](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Kerrigor "Kerrigor"). There are also Six [Great Charter Stones](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Great_Charter_Stones "Great Charter Stones") under the Royal Palace in the reservoir. Two of the Six stones were broken by Kerrigor, but they were mended by Touchstone. Many of the lesser stones were also repaired under his rule.

There are conflicting accounts, some saying that the lesser stones require only a normal Charter mage's blood to repair, while others say that Royal blood is required.
