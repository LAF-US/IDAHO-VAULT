[[Joint Finance-Appropriations Committee|JFAC]] uses informal sub-quorum working groups to conduct pre-vote deliberations on budget motions, a practice challenged under [[Idaho Code §74-207]] and [[Idaho Code §74-202]] by the [[Capitol Correspondents Association]] in a March 2026 letter.

## What They Are

Working groups are informal sub-committees of [[Joint Finance-Appropriations Committee|JFAC]] assigned by the co-chairs ([[Scott Grow]] and [[Josh Tanner]] as of 2026) to review specific agency budgets before the full committee vote. They have no basis in [[JFAC Rules]], [[Joint Rules of the Idaho Legislature|Joint Rules]], [[Idaho Senate Rules|Senate Rules]], or [[Mason's Manual]].

## Mechanics (on-record, 2026)

- Named members, assigned rooms, set schedule (confirmed by [[Keith Bybee]] document and [[Stephanie Cook|Cook]] on record)
- Members actively count attendance to avoid triggering a quorum
- Members may attend working groups they are not assigned to
- Products of working groups (draft motions, [[Conditions Limitations and Restrictions|CLR]] language) proceed directly to the floor without a public deliberation record
- No public notice, no agenda, no audio or video stream
- Non-public access confirmed: [[Melissa Wintrow]]: "probably not" when asked if public could attend

## Three-Tier Deliberative Structure

1. **Pre-FAC** (~8am day before public hearing) — co-chairs finalize motion language
2. **Working groups** (9:30–11am same morning) — produce draft motions and [[Conditions Limitations and Restrictions|CLR]] language
3. **Public JFAC hearing** (following day) — formal disposition of products already substantially decided

## Legal Problem

[[Idaho Code §74-207]] requires all meetings of any standing, special, or select legislative committee to be open to the public. [[Idaho Code §74-202]] defines "deliberation" as receipt or exchange of information or opinion relating to a decision — with no quorum threshold. Every member interviewed relies on the no-quorum defense; none addressed §74-202.

The [[Idaho Attorney General Open Meetings Manual]] addresses sub-quorum evasion directly: a public body may not avoid open meetings requirements by conducting meetings of less than a quorum. The working group structure achieves structural sub-quorum evasion through institutionalized sub-quorum gatherings with assigned members and set schedules.

[[JFAC Rules|JFAC Rule 9(b)(ii)]] provides the only bill-drafting carve-out, covering post-vote technical/ministerial cleanup only — not pre-vote substantive deliberation. No working group exception exists anywhere in the 17 JFAC rules.

[[Mason's Manual]] §650: subcommittees are the only valid parliamentary mechanism for delegating committee work.

## Formalization History

Working groups existed informally before 2023 but were formalized in a co-chair letter from [[Wendy Horman]] and [[Scott Grow]] in February 2023 — itself an improper instrument under [[JFAC Rules|JFAC Rule 11]], which requires that any alteration of committee action be made by formal committee action. A second co-chair letter from [[Josh Tanner]] and [[Kelly Anthon]] in November 2025 announced voting rule changes without a committee vote.

## CCA Challenge

The [[Capitol Correspondents Association]] sent a formal letter March 4, 2026 to co-chairs [[Scott Grow]] and [[Josh Tanner]] (CC: [[Keith Bybee]]), citing [[Idaho Code §74-201]], [[Idaho Code §74-207]], [[Idaho Senate Rules|Senate Rule 20]], and House Rule 26. Signed by [[Melissa Davlin]] ([[Idaho Press Club]] president) and [[Kevin Richert]] ([[Capitol Correspondents Association|CCA]] president). Statutory response deadline: ~March 18, 2026.
