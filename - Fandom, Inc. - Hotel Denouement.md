The **Hotel Denouement** was a hotel in the
[Land of Districts](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Land_of_Districts "Land of Districts")
and the primary setting of
*[The Penultimate Peril](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/The_Penultimate_Peril "The Penultimate Peril")*.
It functioned as a [V.F.D.](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/V.F.D. "V.F.D.")
safe place. A figure identified only as
["J.S."](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/J.S. "J.S.") invited volunteers and
other guests to the hotel; in the television adaptation, this is revealed to
be [Justice Strauss](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Justice_Strauss "Justice Strauss"),
who hoped to bring
[Count Olaf](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Count_Olaf "Count Olaf") to
justice.

## Description

The hotel was a nine-story structure on the edge of a lake, leaning so far
forward that it looked as though it might fall into the water. Its exterior
letters and numbers were written backward so that they would read correctly in
the lake's reflection. The building was covered with lilies and moss to mimic
the lake's surface.

Inside, the main lobby had a domed ceiling with an enormous clock at its
center. According to
[Dewey Denouement](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Dewey_Denouement "Dewey Denouement"),
he was the one who wound it. The hotel also concealed
[Dewey Denouement's underwater library](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Dewey_Denouement%27s_Underwater_Library "Dewey Denouement's Underwater Library")
beneath a nearby pond.

## Staff And Structure

[Frank Denouement](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Frank_Denouement "Frank Denouement")
and [Ernest Denouement](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Ernest_Denouement "Ernest Denouement")
managed the hotel and attended to guests. Dewey, their triplet brother, served
as a hidden sub-sub-librarian and caretaker of both the clock and the secret
library, which caused many people to assume Frank and Ernest were only twins.

The hotel was organized according to the
[Dewey Decimal System](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Dewey_Decimal_System "Dewey Decimal System"),
so floors, rooms, and even bells corresponded to subjects rather than ordinary
hotel numbering. In effect, the building functioned like a library disguised
as hospitality. The basement covered general works and employee quarters, while
the upper floors ranged through philosophy, religion, social sciences,
language, science, technology, arts, literature, and history/geography.
Specific numbers were assigned to particular guests or spaces, including `371`
for educational guests, `674` for lumber interests, and `954` for the Indian
restaurant.

## Destruction

At the end of *The Penultimate Peril*, the
[Baudelaires](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Baudelaire_Family "Baudelaire Family")
set the hotel on fire to signal that "the last safe place is safe no more" and
to delay Count Olaf from unleashing the
[Medusoid Mycelium](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Medusoid_Mycelium "Medusoid Mycelium")
in the lobby. The fire destroys the hotel and leaves the fate of many
occupants uncertain, though some characters survive in the television
adaptation.
