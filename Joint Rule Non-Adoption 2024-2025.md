At the December 2024 and December 2025 organizational sessions, the Idaho House — on a Monks motion directed by Mike Moyle — adopted House Rules only, omitting Joint Rules, leaving JFAC without a jointly adopted governing document.

## What Happened

At both the December 2024 and December 2025 Idaho Legislature organizational sessions, the House (on a motion by Jason Monks, directed by Mike Moyle) adopted House Rules only and declined to adopt Joint Rules. The Senate adopted both chambers' rules. Mike Moyle confirmed to Clark Corbin that this was deliberate, citing Joint Rule 11 and acknowledging a two-thirds threshold would be required for formal amendment.

## Significance

JFAC is a statutory joint committee created by Idaho Code §67-432. Its governing rules should be joint rules. By declining to adopt joint rules, Mike Moyle structurally subordinated the joint committee's procedure to the Speaker's preferences without a formal vote — the very kind of informal action that JFAC Rule 11 prohibits.

The JFAC Rule 1 fallback hierarchy (JFAC Rules → Joint Rules → Senate Rules → Mason's Manual) is itself drawn from rules whose operative status is now in question. If there are no valid JFAC rules, there is no Rule 1, and the working groups lack even the structural legitimacy that Rule 1 might otherwise provide.
