# Christian nationalism town hall set for next week

Moscow-PullmanDaily News

Listen to this article

<audio src="https://e-edition.lmtribune.com/neodirect/49/21829844?role=1" preload="none" controls="" style="display: block; width: 100%; max-width: 750px; margin: 0px auto;">Your browser does not support the audio element.</audio>

Members of Christ Church will participate in a town hall on Christian nationalism at 7 p.m., April 9, at the University of Idaho Administration Auditorium.

The event is organized by the Collegiate Reformed Fellowship, a University of Idaho student group.

Christ Church has been a vocal advocate of Christian nationalism and has a stated goal of making Moscow a “Christian town.”

According to a news release from the Collegiate Reformed Fellowship, the town hall will address questions about what a Christian nation or town would look like and how non-Christians would be treated in this scenario.

The public will be able to send questions to a text line available at the event.

The town hall will feature panelists Pastor Doug Wilson, Pastor Toby Sumpter and Pastor Jared Longshore.

Wilson and Christ Church have drawn national media attention for their controversial conservative views. For example, Wilson, Sumpter and Longshore were recently quoted in a CNN report saying they want to repeal women’s right to vote.
