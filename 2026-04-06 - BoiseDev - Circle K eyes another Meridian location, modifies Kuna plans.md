## Circle K eyes another Meridian location, modifies plans for Kuna station

![](https://boisedev.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/03_CircleK_Favor-2048x1365.jpg)

A Circle K location in Texas. Photo: Courtesy Circle K/BusinessWire

A gas and convenience store chain aiming to broaden its Treasure Valley footprint has taken another step in that direction.

Circle K submitted a pre-application in February for a potential gas station and convenience store at 1515 W. Ustick Rd., southeast of its intersection with N. Linder Rd. in Meridian.

The preapplication materials propose a gas station with five pump stations, a 3,264 sq. ft. fuel canopy, a 5,200 sq. ft. convenience store, and 22 parking spaces on the nearly 9-acre lot. An existing building on the property would be demolished, according to the conceptual site plan.

## Reentering the Idaho market

The Arizona-based chain operated 45 stores in Idaho until 1999 when Boise-based Jacksons Food Stores purchased them, [according to reporting from the Idaho Statesman](https://www.newspapers.com/article/the-idaho-statesman-jacksons-to-buy-45-c/132442789/).

The Ustick site pre-application is the latest in a series of exploratory inquiries in the Treasure Valley and Idaho in recent years.

![Senior Edge Legal 2026 - 2:1](https://static-v2.boisedev.com/assets/17651b97-0b36-4675-8bd6-7459914b4016.png)

Senior Edge Legal 2026 - 2:1

Last year, the City of Kuna’s planning and zoning commission approved an application for the company to build a 5,200 sq. ft. convenience store with six pump stations near the intersection of Meridian and Deer Flat roads, [as BoiseDev reported](https://boisedev.com/news/2025/02/24/kuna-pz-approves-design-for-new-circle-k-on-meridian-rd/). On March 10, the planning and zoning commission approved modifying the plans to increase the convenience store’s square footage to 5,685 square feet, according to the meeting minutes.

In early 2024, BoiseDev reported that Circle K submitted pre-application materials to develop a gas station [at the intersection of W. Overland and Ten Mile roads](https://boisedev.com/news/2024/04/04/circle-k-ten-mile-meridian/). That same year, the company also explored converting [two old Rite Aid locations in Boise](https://boisedev.com/news/2024/04/04/circle-k-ten-mile-meridian/) into gas stations. None of the three locations moved forward to formal applications.

Meanwhile, in Valley County, the Donnelly City Council approved plans in November 2024 for [a Circle K at the intersection of Highway 55 and Roseberry Rd](https://valleylookout.com/2024/11/19/tight-spot-valley-co-city-could-get-second-gas-station-with-restrictions/).
