**Captain Widdershins** is the captain of the *[Queequeg](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Queequeg "Queequeg")* and a [V.F.D.](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/V.F.D. "V.F.D.") Volunteer.

## Personality

He has a rather peculiar way of speaking, often making redundant comments and interjecting them with the word "Aye!"

He feels a great need to remain in control, objecting when anyone makes a statement that could be even slightly construed as a command.

He cares very much for Fiona and refuses to discuss any of V.F.D.'s secrets in front of her. This, he believes, will keep her from harm. It is clear that he knows many of said secrets, and it is known that he taught [Olivia Caliban (Madame Lulu)](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Olivia_Caliban "Olivia Caliban") how to read coded stains on maps.

## Biography

### Early Life

Widdershins mentions that he was friends with [Josephine Anwhistle](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Josephine_Anwhistle "Josephine Anwhistle") and that he patrolled the waters of [Lake Lachrymose](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Lake_Lachrymose "Lake Lachrymose") for years.[^1]

At some point, Widdershins was recruited into [VFD](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/V.F.D. "V.F.D."). He trained alongside [Lemony Snicket](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Lemony_Snicket "Lemony Snicket"), who attempted to lead a sort-of coup of the younger students that almost got him kicked out of the organization. Widdershins found this impressive.[^2] He also trained alongside [Olivia Caliban](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Olivia_Caliban "Olivia Caliban").[^1]

Widdershins tells his superiors that he's investigating in [Stain'd-by-the-Sea](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Stain%27d-by-the-Sea "Stain'd-by-the-Sea") in order to inform Snicket that his sister, [Kit](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Kit_Snicket "Kit Snicket"), was arrested. She and Snicket had planned to break into a museum together, but when Snicket was unable to arrive, she managed to steal the item but was caught trying to escape.

Snicket thanks him for telling him, and then asks him to identify a mysterious substance. Widdershins recognizes it as caviar, as [Gustav](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Gustav_Sebald "Gustav Sebald") loved the stuff.[^2]

While he does not appear later, [Ghede](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Ghede "Ghede") says he is in a submarine deep underwater while Snicket is on *The Thistle of the Valley.*[^3]

### Unknown Points in Time

Kit Snicket helped him build the *[Queequeg](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Queequeg "Queequeg"),* and he kept it up with a crew of two. The first member of the crew of two was his wife; when he married her, he became the stepfather of [Fernald](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Fernald "Fernald") and [Fiona](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Fiona "Fiona").

It is mentioned in *[The Grim Grotto](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/The_Grim_Grotto "The Grim Grotto")* that he helped burn down [Anwhistle Aquatics](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Anwhistle_Aquatics "Anwhistle Aquatics"). He later allowed [Jacques Snicket](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Jacques_Snicket "Jacques Snicket") to blame the arson entirely on his stepson. Fernald left him after this, and Widdershins raised Fiona on the *Queequeg,* telling her that her mother died in a manatee accident and that her brother disappeared. He discouraged Fiona's interest in mycology, wanting her to focus more on Herman Melville. He also refused to tell her many VFD secrets, such as what was inside the sugar bowl and what [the Great Unknown](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/The_Great_Unknown "The Great Unknown") was.

After the death of his wife, Jacques replaced her on the Queequeg's crew, followed by a woman "who turned out to be a spy", [Lemony Snicket](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Lemony_Snicket "Lemony Snicket"), and then [Phil](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Phil "Phil").

### Pre-The Grim Grotto

![Fiona, Captain Widdershins and Fernald.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/snicket/images/b/b1/WddershinsFamily.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20190109080841)

Fiona, Captain Widdershins and Fernald.

Captain Widdershins searched the wreckage of the [Baudelaire Mansion](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Baudelaire_Mansion "Baudelaire Mansion") and discovered the [Duchess of Winnipeg](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Duchess_of_Winnipeg "Duchess of Winnipeg") 's ring, only to lose it later in a storm at sea.[^4]

When Josephine's house collapsed, Widdershins and Fiona gathered all of her materials that they could before they could be destroyed by the lake.

Widdershins and Fiona followed the story of the Baudelaire Orphans- [Violet](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Violet_Baudelaire "Violet Baudelaire"), [Klaus](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Klaus_Baudelaire "Klaus Baudelaire") and [Sunny](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Sunny_Baudelaire "Sunny Baudelaire") - but Widdershins told Fiona that they weren't to do anything about it, and instead they had to search for the Sugar Bowl. Fiona then managed to track the Sugar Bowl to the Stricken Stream. Around that time, they stopped receiving telegrams from VFD.

The *Queequeg* fell into disrepair, due to being attacked by villains, leeches, sharks, realtors, pirates, girlfriends, torpedoes and angry salmon.[^1]

### The Grim Grotto

While investigating the Stricken Stream, Widdershins sees the Baudelaires clinging to a toboggan; after they give him the password of "The World is Quiet Here", he lets them into the submarine. Once there, he immediately puts them to work, telling Violet to fix the telegram machine, Klaus to study tidal charts with Fiona, and Sunny to cook with Phil.

![Captain Widdershins in the Russian illustrations.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/snicket/images/f/f7/Captain_Widdershins_russian.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20120822233423)

Captain Widdershins in the Russian illustrations.

At the end of the day, Widdershins asks for reports, and Violet informs him that the telegraph is not broken, and the machines on the other end must be the problem. Klaus and Fiona managed to trace the Sugar Bowl to a cave under Anwhistle Aquatics labelled GG. Before they can investigate further, Widdershins spots [Count Olaf](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Count_Olaf "Count Olaf") 's submarine on the sonar and orders everyone to shut off the submarine to avoid detection. Olaf's submarine only leaves when [the Great Unknown](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/The_Great_Unknown "The Great Unknown") appears and scares him off. Afterwards, Klaus realizes that GG is a cave, and Widdershins is so excited that he says if Klaus finds the sugar bowl, he can marry Fiona, and they can marry Violet off to Fernald when they find him. The Baudelaires are not too keen on this, and Fiona is embarrassed. Fiona figures out that GG is the Gorgonian Grotto, filled with a deadly fungus, but Widdershins is not deterred and sets a course anyway.

They arrive the next morning, but the cave's entrance is only big enough for the children to fit into. Widdershins suits up Fiona and the Baudelaires and send them off. While they're gone, he and Phil set up to celebrate Violet's fifteenth birthday, but then a woman arrives and convinces him and Phil to abandon the submarine and the children.

### After The Grim Grotto

In *[The Penultimate Peril](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/The_Penultimate_Peril "The Penultimate Peril")*, [Kit Snicket](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Kit_Snicket "Kit Snicket") mentions she intends to meet him at a certain clump of seaweed, and if she does, both of them will be at the [Hotel Denouement](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Hotel_Denouement "Hotel Denouement") on Thursday. She waterskis towards Captain Widdershins, then later, waterskis away from him.

According to Kit, she and Widdershins reunited with Fernald on Fiona aboard the *Queequeg*, when [Hector](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Hector "Hector") 's [Self-Sustaining Hot Air Mobile Home](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Self-Sustaining_Hot_Air_Mobile_Home "Self-Sustaining Hot Air Mobile Home") crashed into it. The [Great Unknown](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Bombinating_Beast "Bombinating Beast") appeared, and Widdershins chose to take his chances with it.[^4] It is unknown what happened to him after this; though it is unknown how accurate Kit's story is- and, if it is true, whether the Great Unknown saved or ate him and the other castaways- Snicket did say that Widdershins would never live to see Fiona's mycological studies put to good use.[^1]

## Netflix Series Divergent Canon

![Painting of Captain Widdershins](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/snicket/images/9/9b/43676278_2051458675166071_7695824114388303872_n.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20181027023424)

Painting of Captain Widdershins

Captain Widdershins does not physically appear in the TV except in a portrait and a family photograph. According to *[The Incomplete History of Secret Organizations: An Utterly Unreliable Account of Netflix's A Series of Unfortunate Events](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/The_Incomplete_History_of_Secret_Organizations:_An_Utterly_Unreliable_Account_of_Netflix%27s_A_Series_of_Unfortunate_Events "The Incomplete History of Secret Organizations: An Utterly Unreliable Account of Netflix's A Series of Unfortunate Events")*, he was removed because a major theme in season 3 is that next generation of V.F.D. must take up the torch in the absence of parental figures. Fiona mentions he left one day to answer a distress call from a manatee and never came back. Instead, Fiona has taken over his role.

After Fiona reunites with Fernald, they go on a mission to locate their missing stepfather. While having dinner with their stepfather's portrait, they hear his voice on a radio.

## Physical Appearance

He is described in the book as being an enormous man dressed in a shiny suit made of some sort of slippery-looking material with equally slippery-looking boots on his feet. On the front of the suit was a portrait of Herman Melville. Widddershins had no beard, merely a very long mustache curled up at both ends like a pair of parentheses.

In the Netflix adaptation, he does have a white beard was modeled after [Barry Sonnenfeld](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Barry_Sonnenfeld "Barry Sonnenfeld").

## Trivia

- "Widdershins" is a rare word for going counterclockwise, considered unlucky; it literally means "against the direction of the sun." [^5]
- A fan theory is that he is [Friday Caliban](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Friday_Caliban "Friday Caliban") 's father. In this speculation, the assumption can also be made that his first name may be [Thursday](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Thursday "Thursday"), and that he and [Miranda Caliban](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Miranda_Caliban "Miranda Caliban") were together and broke up, with Miranda presumably also being Fernald and Fiona's mother.
	- Fernald, Fiona and Friday are all names beginning with F, which could support this theory.
		- Both Widdershins and Miranda use the same story of the prospective parent being devoured by a manatee, possibly to hide the truth from their children. This is another connection that could support this theory.
		- Another fact supporting this theory is that most siblings in the series come in groups of three, and even on the rare occasions that only two siblings are known of, the existence of a third sibling is usually hinted at (such as the [White-Faced Women](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/White-Faced_Women "White-Faced Women")). The only known siblings in groups of two (aside from Fiona and Fernald) are from *[All the Wrong Questions](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/All_the_Wrong_Questions "All the Wrong Questions")*: [Pip](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Pip_Bellerophon "Pip Bellerophon") and [Squeak Bellerophon](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Squeak_Bellerophon "Squeak Bellerophon"), [Kellar](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Kellar_Haines "Kellar Haines") and [Lizzie Haines](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Lizzie_Haines "Lizzie Haines"), [Zada and Zora](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Zada_and_Zora "Zada and Zora"), and [Tatiana](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Tatiana_Cozy "Tatiana Cozy") and [Treacle Cozy](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Treacle_Cozy "Treacle Cozy").
		- Something against this theory could be that [Kit Snicket](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Kit_Snicket "Kit Snicket") believes [Thursday](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Thursday "Thursday") is alive and well, and mentions dining with him the day before meeting the Baudelaires. The day before meeting the Baudelaires, Captain Widdershins was with them on his submarine, and by the time Kit was discussing Thursday, Kit believed the Captain to be either deceased or in mortal peril due to the crash of the *Queequeg* and arrival of the [Great Unknown](https://snicket.fandom.com/wiki/Bombinating_Beast "Bombinating Beast").
