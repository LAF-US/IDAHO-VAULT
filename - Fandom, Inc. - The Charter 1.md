**The Charter** is the quasi-divine transmutation of every concept in all creation into an infinite series (sometimes described as a "song" or "flow") of unique runes that can be drawn in the air, on the ground, or anywhere else; and that can be thought, spoken, sung, whistled, or even barked. It was created in [The Beginning](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Beginning "The Beginning") by the first [Seven Bright Shiners](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Nine_Bright_Shiners "Nine Bright Shiners"), [Free Magic beings](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Free_Magic "Free Magic") of unfathomable power: [Ranna](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Ranna "Ranna"), [Mosrael](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Mosrael "Mosrael"), [Kibeth](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Kibeth "Kibeth"), [Dyrim](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Dyrim "Dyrim"), [Belgaer](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Belgaer "Belgaer"), [Saraneth,](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Saraneth "Saraneth") and [Astarael](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Astarael "Astarael"). (A [necromancer](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Necromancer "Necromancer") 's [bells](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Bells "Bells") are named after them.) They wove their very selves into the Charter and made order out of the chaos that was [Free Magic](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Free_Magic "Free Magic").

### Charter Magic

Baptismal [Charter Marks](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Charter_Marks "Charter Marks") are necessary to experience the endless ocean of ideas and powers of the Charter, and the baptismal mark allows a person to use the Charter as well. Attack, protection, healing, assistance: any concept has its own Charter symbol that can be used, and will have more power if they are combined and sealed with a so-called "master mark." For example, when [Sabriel](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Sabriel "Sabriel") must calm panicked younger girls at [Wyverley College](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Wyverley_College "Wyverley College"), she draws the runes for "sleep," "quiet," and "rest" with her fingers, indicates the girls, and ties it all together with a master mark. The girls fall back asleep.[^1]

This is [Charter Magic](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Charter_magic "Charter magic"), which supports and encourages [Life](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life"). Its antithesis is [Free Magic](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Free_Magic "Free Magic"), which requires far less study, but that corrodes and destroys Life. The only person who can use both at the same time without destroying themselves is the [Abhorsen](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Abhorsen "Abhorsen").

### Sources of the Charter

*See [Five Great Charters](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Five_Great_Charters "Five Great Charters").*

The sources of the Charter in the [Old Kingdom](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Old_Kingdom "Old Kingdom") are the [Clayr](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Clayr "The Clayr") bloodline, the [Royal](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Royal_line "Royal line") bloodline, the Abhorsen bloodline, the [Great Charter Stones](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Great_Charter_Stones "Great Charter Stones") beneath the palace, the [Wall](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Wall "The Wall"), and individual [Charter Stones](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Charter_Stone "Charter Stone") erected around the Kingdom. If any of these become weakened or broken, Charter Magic is made more difficult, the [Dead](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Dead "Dead") can more easily slip into Life, and the Charter-made bindings of dangerous [Free Magic creatures](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Free_Magic_Creatures "Free Magic Creatures") can weaken. This was the case during the [Interregnum](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Interregnum "Interregnum"), when the [Old Kingdom](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Old_Kingdom "Old Kingdom") was almost post-apocalyptic in its dangers.

The Charter only exists in the world of the Old Kingdom. While it is still present in places like the Wall and nearby southern towns, such as Bain and Wyverley (especially when the wind blows from the north), it weakens with distance until it can be barely felt. This also applies to the steppes of the [far North](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_North "The North"), which, lacking the Charter, [have destructive Free Magic](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Shaman "Shaman") but no contrasting magic of healing or protection.

The only exception to this is in the person of [Nicholas Sayre](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Nicholas_Sayre "Nicholas Sayre"), who becomes a sort of portable Charter Stone after his possession by [Orannis](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Orannis "Orannis") and baptism by the [Disreputable Dog](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Disreputable_Dog "Disreputable Dog"). His presence allows the Charter to be used anywhere, even the [Empty Lands](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/The_Empty_Lands "The Empty Lands").

### Clariel: The Lost Abhorsen

Because *[Clariel: The Lost Abhorsen](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Clariel:_The_Lost_Abhorsen "Clariel: The Lost Abhorsen")* takes place six hundred years before the birth of Sabriel, and thus four hundred years before the Interregnum, the Old Kingdom is peaceful and prosperous: all the Great Charters are intact, the bloodlines numerous, and Charter Stones plentiful and unbroken. Charter Magic has therefore "fallen out of fashion" -- with Free Magic and Dead threats rare, the process of becoming a skilled Charter Mage is viewed as laborious and unnecessary, the work of servants. [Clariel](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Clariel "Clariel") herself has little interest in the Charter, strongly drawn to the instant vast power of Free Magic instead.

This realmwide lassitude is proven deadly when [Aziminil](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Aziminil "Aziminil"), [Baazalanan](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Baazalanan "Baazalanan"), and [Mogget](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Mogget "Mogget") trick Clariel into bringing them into the palace, where they plan to break all six Great Charter Stones with the king's blood. This would free Mogget from his Charter-made binding collar and protect the other two from ever being bound again. Their plan is foiled at the last minute, but the ease with which the Charter was threatened and the indifference of the Old Kingdom's people would later prove its undoing.

### Kerrigor and the Interregnum

Four hundred years later, [Kerrigor](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Kerrigor "Kerrigor") has a similar plan to Mogget's: he tries to destroy all six Great Charter Stones, and therefore essentially banish Charter magic from the realm, by sacrificing his family upon them. (The possibility of deception or treachery never occurs to them.) However, his half-brother [Touchstone](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Touchstone "Touchstone") goes [berserk](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Fury "Fury") and holds him off until reinforcements arrive. Still, at the time of *[Sabriel](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Sabriel_\(Book\) "Sabriel (Book)")*, [Sabriel](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Sabriel "Sabriel") herself is the only person left of the Abhorsen bloodline, Touchstone is the only person left of the Royal bloodline, two of the six Great Charter Stones are broken, and Kerrigor and his minions have broken many individual Charter Stones. Charter Magic is therefore much weaker than it had ever been, and the Old Kingdom's people live in fear and misery.

### Status as of Goldenhand

After the Restoration of the monarchy, Touchstone dedicated considerable amounts of his time and even more amounts of his blood to repair the Great Charter Stones (as only the blood of the Royal line can repair them). This is known to have taken considerable years off his life. Even twenty years after the defeat of Kerrigor, many individual stones remain broken, but on the whole the Old Kingdom is a far improved place now that the Charter is being slowly restored. The presence of three Royals (Touchstone, [Ellimere](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Ellimere "Ellimere"), and [Sameth](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Sameth "Sameth")), well as four Abhorsens (Sabriel, Ellimere, Sam, and [Lirael](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Lirael "Lirael")) and the first Wallmaker in millennia (Sam) has further strengthened the Charter.

### Trivia/Theories

- In the Old Kingdom, the Charter is treated as essentially divine, a deity in and of itself. This can be seen in sayings like "Thank the Charter" or "Charter preserve us."
- Prince Sameth is the only person of the great Charter bloodline of the [Wallmakers](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Wallmaker "Wallmaker") currently known, or who has existed for hundreds of years. His status as a Wallmaker is apparently purely spontaneous and should be impossible, with no direct family history. It may have occurred simply because a Wallmaker would be needed against Orannis.
- The heraldic representation of the Charter in art and sculpture is an unfurling scroll that turns back on itself.
- The "Great Charter" is another translation of the "Magna Carta."
- The Charter is actually seven songs woven together into one, sung by the Seven when they first stood against Orannis in order to bind him. At the [second binding](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Orannis%27_Binding "Orannis' Binding"), [Yrael](https://oldkingdomwiki.fandom.com/wiki/Yrael "Yrael") sings to contribute his power, as a Bright Shiner.
- Some fans at book events report that Garth Nix specified DNA as the inspiration for the Charter.[^2]
