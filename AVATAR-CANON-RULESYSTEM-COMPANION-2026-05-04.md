# Avatar Canon Rulesystem Companion

This companion note explains the sixth FANDOM canon set added to
`FANDOM-CANON-RULESYSTEMS-2026-05-03.md`.

Avatar is registered as a **spirit-cycle continuity law** system. Its central
question is not simply who has power, but how identity, memory, and authority
continue across incarnations without making every incarnation the same person.

## Source-Backed Primitives

## What The Series Says

The Avatar is the human embodiment of the Avatar Spirit. When one Avatar dies,
the cycle continues through reincarnation into the next nation in the element
order. The Avatar State can expose the skills and knowledge of past lives, but
it also creates a catastrophic failure condition: death in that state can break
the cycle.

Raava is the continuity carrier. Wan is the origin vessel. Aang is the active
instance in the source set. Past lives are the queryable memory chain. General
Fong supplies the coercive-trigger case by trying to force access to the Avatar
State through extreme emotional pressure.

## Why It Matters For The Vault

Avatar gives the vault a clean model for succession without flattening identity.

- The continuity carrier is not the same as the active instance.
- The active instance inherits memory and duty without becoming every prior instance.
- The memory bridge can be queried, weakened, or severed.
- Forced access to the high-power state is a risk, not a shortcut.

That makes Avatar useful for persona persistence, agent succession, and
continuity handoff. It should remain a canon rulesystem stream, not a Tree noun
standard and not a persona chamber standard.

## Boundary Rule

Avatar shares an enforcement philosophy with the Tree and persona work: make
the continuity surface explicit and checkable. It does not merge with those
systems.

The FANDOM registry owns the canon entry. Persona standards own persona
chambers. Tree noun standards own Touchstone anchors.
