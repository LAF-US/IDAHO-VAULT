### D&D Canon: The Magic of "Power Words"

In Dungeons & Dragons canon, **Power Words** represent a distinct and primal category of magic. They are high-level spells where the caster speaks a single syllable of such absolute, reality-altering authority that the universe is forced to instantly obey.

Here is a breakdown of how they function mechanically and exist within the lore:

#### The Lore and Cosmology

Within D&D canon, regular spells are like entering cheat codes into the universe, requiring intricate gestures (somatic components), specific materials, and complex incantations. Power Words bypass all of this.

- **Pure Verbal Magic:** They have only a verbal component. There are no wands to wave or components to crush. The magic is entirely tied to the spoken word.
    
- **The Language of Creation:** Canonically, these words are often tied to the underlying language of the multiverse—remnants of the "Words of Creation" used by gods to forge reality, or the "Dark Speech" used by ancient evils. To speak a Power Word is to momentarily speak in the commanding tongue of the cosmos itself.
    

#### The Mechanics: The Hit Point Threshold

What makes Power Words mechanically unique in D&D 5th Edition (and older editions) is that they generally **do not allow for a saving throw or require an attack roll**. They bypass a target's defenses, armor, and evasiveness entirely.

Instead, they rely on a strict **Hit Point (HP) threshold**. If the target's current HP is at or below the threshold, the spell takes full effect instantly. If their HP is even one point above the threshold, the spell simply fails and does nothing.

#### Canonical Power Word Spells

Here are the primary Power Word spells that exist in standard D&D 5e canon:

- **Power Word Kill (9th Level):** The most famous of the group. The caster speaks a word, and if the target has 100 hit points or fewer, they instantly die. There is no death saving throw or resisting it.
    
- **Power Word Stun (8th Level):** If the target has 150 hit points or fewer, they are instantly stunned. They are only allowed a saving throw at the end of their subsequent turns to shake off the effect.
    
- **Power Word Pain (7th Level):** If the target has 100 hit points or fewer, they are subjected to crippling pain. Their movement speed drops, they have disadvantage on attacks and ability checks, and casting other spells becomes incredibly difficult.
    
- **Power Word Heal (9th Level):** Unlike the offensive words, this is spoken to an ally. A wave of healing energy washes over the target, completely restoring their hit points and ending conditions like charmed, frightened, paralyzed, or stunned.

### Widen the Lens: The Cosmology of Language in D&D

When you pull back from the specific mechanics of _Power Word Kill_ or _Stun_, D&D cosmology reveals a universe where language isn't just a method of communication—it is the foundational architecture of reality itself.

If standard spellcasting is akin to writing complex software to manipulate the universe, speaking a Power Word is executing a command directly into the cosmos's root terminal.

Here is how the lore expands when you look at the cosmic role of language:

#### 1. The Source Code: Words of Creation and Dark Speech

At the dawn of the D&D multiverse, before the current pantheons settled into their domains, reality was shaped by primordial frequencies.

- **The Words of Creation:** In canonical lore (heavily featured in earlier editions), these are the spoken components of the universe's creation. They are words of pure, unadulterated good and cosmic order. Mortals lack the physiological and spiritual capacity to comprehend or speak them safely. When Celestials sing, they are often echoing fragments of these words.
    
- **The Dark Speech:** The cosmological antithesis to the Words of Creation. It is a language of absolute entropy, malice, and corruption, often associated with the creation of the Lower Planes (the Abyss and the Nine Hells). Merely hearing a word of Dark Speech can shatter a mortal's mind or physical form, and even powerful evil spellcasters must take extreme precautions to utter a single syllable without destroying themselves.
    

#### 2. The Weave as a Cosmic Lexicon

In settings like the Forgotten Realms, magic is accessed through "The Weave," a metaphysical fabric overseen by the goddess Mystra.

- Most wizards have to "pluck" the strings of the Weave through somatic (gestures) and material components, combined with precise, localized verbal incantations.
    
- Uttering a **Power Word** bypasses the localized plucking. It is a word so universally recognized by the Weave that the fabric of reality immediately folds to accommodate the speaker's demand, requiring no other catalysts.
    

#### 3. Truenames: The Ultimate Spoken Authority

The deepest and most potent form of spoken magic in D&D cosmology is **Truenaming**.

In this framework, every single entity, object, and place in the multiverse possesses a "True Name." This isn't just a label; it is a complex, almost incomprehensible metaphysical equation that perfectly describes that being's entire essence, history, and destiny.

- **Absolute Dominion:** If you know a creature's True Name and possess the magical skill to speak it, you hold absolute, unmitigable power over them. You can command them, alter their nature, or unmake them entirely.
    
- **The Shield of Aliases:** Because of this existential vulnerability, a cosmic game of hide-and-seek exists. Entities must navigate the world using everyday given names or adopted titles and aliases to shield themselves, fiercely guarding their true, hidden names to prevent subjugation. This is especially true for powerful, ancient beings like Fiends, Fey, and Dragons, who understand the danger of a linguistic vulnerability.
    

#### 4. The Primordial Tongues

Even regular, mortal languages in D&D carry echoes of this cosmic power.

- **Draconic:** Often considered the language of magic itself. Dragons do not cast spells the way human wizards do; their language is so old and tied to the world's creation that speaking a concept in Draconic naturally manifests magical energy.
    
- **Primordial:** The language of Elementals (Auran, Aquan, Ignan, Terran). It is less a structured language and more the raw, auditory expression of nature's chaos—the crackle of fire, the roar of the ocean.
