Artificial intelligence has been around for decades. But in the last few years, since the launch of ChatGPT in late 2022, the technology has spread and advanced at a breathtaking clip.

The healthcare industry isn’t immune, and the promise here is tantalizing. Could AI bring about faster cures? Could it make diagnosing patients easier? Can it improve outcomes?

There are downside risks, too.

On May 7th, we’re bringing together a panel of experts to talk about AI in Healthcare: Promise, Potential, Pitfalls. This morning event will be held at the Grove Hotel in Boise, moderated by BoiseDev’s Sarah Jacobsen. Doors open at 8 a.m., the panel discussion starts at 8:30 a.m., and we’ll be wrapped up by 9:30 a.m.

[Our panel includes:](https://boisedev.com/events/intersection/)

- JulieAnn McKellogg, Director of Partner Success at Abridge, a company building an AI product that is designed to help doctors shift their focus from documentation to listening to patients.
- Dr. Christopher Hall, Chief Clinical Officer at Saint Alphonsus Health System
- Dan Anolik, Chief Technology Officer for Regence BlueShield of Idaho.

The discussion will look at how these advances are changing the nature of patient care in our hospitals, clinics, and even our own homes.
