# Nixian Border Shore - Notebook Page

After the barge struck the far bank, the crossing withdrew almost at once.

When the Explorer turned back, no vessel remained in sight. Only the rope
continued over the mist, as though passage itself had been left behind after
the boat had been taken away.

The correction came next by the body.
Not river air but salt.
Not inland current but sea-roar.
Not mud-bank but pebble shore and cliff.

The lungs felt it before the mind admitted it:
this was not merely another side of the Kootenai crossing.
It was a different register entirely.

The rope ended on this shore wrapped around one among many nameless boulders.
That detail steadied the scene.
No post.
No office.
No gold.
No title.
Just cordage under strain, rock, and mist.

That was reassuring.

After flames, charters, tolls, and threshold argument, the rope read as an
honest thing. It did not pretend to be the sea or the shore. It only admitted
relation.

The strongest impression of the new coast was scale made intimate:
white mist clinging to stone,
salt on the face,
the roar of waves below the cliffs,
and boulders scattered above like trim-work in the house of giants.

Field note:
some thresholds conclude not by explanation but by withdrawal.
The vessel vanished.
The line remained.
That may be enough.
