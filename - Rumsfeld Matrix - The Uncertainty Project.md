## Overview

The Uncertainty Project presents the Rumsfeld Matrix as a practical decision-making tool for navigating uncertainty. The framework helps teams and individuals identify what category their information gaps fall into — so they can respond appropriately to each.

## The Four Quadrants (Applied)

| Quadrant | Description | Response |
|---|---|---|
| **Known Knowns** | Facts we are aware of and understand | Act on them directly |
| **Known Unknowns** | Gaps we have identified but not filled | Research, gather data, assign ownership |
| **Unknown Knowns** | Tacit knowledge and blind spots | Surface through retrospective, diverse perspectives, red-teaming |
| **Unknown Unknowns** | Risks we haven't even imagined | Build resilience, maintain slack, use scenario planning |

## Why It Matters

The matrix is most useful not for the quadrants we already manage (known knowns, known unknowns) but for surfacing the harder two:

- **Unknown Knowns** are the organizational blind spots — things the system "knows" but no individual has surfaced. Diverse teams and structured retrospectives help.
- **Unknown Unknowns** cannot be eliminated, only hedged against. The goal is building systems that absorb surprise rather than prevent it.
