[[Wendy Horman]] is a Republican [[Idaho House of Representatives|Idaho House]] member from District 30A (Ammon) who served as [[Joint Finance-Appropriations Committee|JFAC]] House co-chair 2023–2024, co-authored the February 2023 letter formalizing [[JFAC Working Groups|working groups]], and publicly introduced the [[Conditions Limitations and Restrictions|CLR]] framework before resigning to run unsuccessfully for Superintendent of Public Instruction.

## Basic Info

- **District:** 30A (Ammon — corrected from Idaho Falls)
- **Chamber:** House
- **Terms:** Sixth term
- **Role:** JFAC House co-chair (2023–2024); previously JFAC vice-chair under [[Scott Bedke]]
- **Party:** Republican

## Key Actions

- February 2023: Co-signed letter with [[Scott Grow]] formalizing working group structure without committee vote (improper under [[JFAC Rules|Rule 11]])
- Publicly introduced [[Conditions Limitations and Restrictions|CLR]] framework: "it is the job of JFAC to set fiscal policy"
- March 2024: Stated she was unaware [[Keith Bybee]] had changed intent language in the Chinden Campus bill after JFAC voted, and would have brought any change back to the full committee
- Unsuccessfully challenged [[Scott Bedke]] for the speakership in 2020
- Resigned JFAC co-chairship to run for Superintendent of Public Instruction; lost in primary
