[[Dustin Manwaring]] is a Republican [[Idaho House of Representatives|Idaho House]] member from District 29A (Pocatello) who served as Majority Caucus Chair 2022–2024, retains his [[Joint Finance-Appropriations Committee|JFAC]] seat as rank-and-file in 2026, and gave the most extensive on-record defense of working groups in interview while making significant unintentional admissions.

## Basic Info

- **District:** 29A (Pocatello)
- **Chamber:** House
- **Role:** Rank-and-file, 2026 (Majority Caucus Chair December 2022–December 2024; replaced by Crane)
- **Party:** Republican

## Title Note

Majority Caucus Chair two terms: December 2022–December 2024. Replaced by Crane at December 2024 organizational session when Tanner moved up to Asst. Majority Leader (replacing Dixon). Whether he briefly held Asst. Majority Leader after Dixon's September 2024 departure is not confirmed from primary source. [UNVERIFIED]

## Interview Findings [TRANS — verify against audio before quoting]

Key statements from interview transcript (collapsed speaker tags; requires audio verification):

- **"We know we can't have a quorum"** — places him in knowing-violation category under [[Idaho Code §74-208]] ($1,500 penalty tier); directly addresses the quorum-awareness that the statute targets
- **"I'm a sunshine person"** — performed preference; self-identifies as pro-transparency while defending the opacity structure
- **"Should it be an open and public process — I would air that, I would be fine with that personally"** — significant buried concession; not a principled defense of closure, only arguing he hasn't seen a violation
- **"The press has not been blocked out... the recording's not on"** — admits accountability-by-happenstance, not designed transparency
- **"Before work groups, you still had legislators that got together and met and did the exact same thing... it just wasn't on a piece of paper"** — informality-as-defense actually admits the problem predates named working groups

His bill-drafting analogy (defense of working groups) fails: [[JFAC Rules|Rule 9(b)(ii)]] covers only post-vote technical cleanup, not pre-vote substantive deliberation involving multiple members exchanging opinions.
